    Lasting images of JFK’s assassination

    President John F. Kennedy and Jacqueline Bouvier Kennedy walked down the steps of Air Force One as they arrived at Love Field in Dallas less than an hour before the president was assassinated on Nov. 22, 1963.
    Cecil Stoughton/The White House/JFK Library via Reuters | November 20, 2013

    President and Jacqueline Kennedy arrived at Love Field airport in Dallas as a television camera, above, followed them.
    Associated Press/File 1963 | November 20, 2013

    President and Jacqueline Kennedy smiled at the crowds lining their motorcade route.
    Bettmann/Corbis | November 20, 2013

    President Kennedy's hand reached toward his head within seconds of being fatally shot and Jacqueline Kennedy held his forearm as the motorcade proceeded along Elm Street past the Texas School Book Depository in Dallas. Governor John Connally was also shot.
    James W. (Ike) Altgens/Associated Press/File 1963 | November 20, 2013

    Kennedy was struck by an assassin's bullet as he traveled in the motorcade.
    Three Lions/Hulton Archive/Getty Images | November 20, 2013

    The limousine carrying the mortally wounded Kennedy raced toward Parkland Hospital seconds after the shooting. Secret Service agent Clinton Hill rode on the back of the car. Nellie Connally, wife of Texas Governor John Connally, bent over her wounded husband, and Jacqueline Kennedy leaned over the president.
    Justin Newman/Associated Press/file 1963 | November 20, 2013

    Spectators hit the ground after President Kennedy was shot.
    UPI/File 1963 | November 20, 2013

    Women burst into tears outside Parkland Hospital upon hearing that President John F. Kennedy had died of his wounds.
    Associated Press/File 1963 | November 20, 2013

    Jacqueline Kennedy, with bloodstains on her clothes, held hands with her brother-in-law, Attorney General Robert Kennedy, as the coffin carrying the body of President John F. Kennedy was placed in an ambulance after arriving at Andrews Air Force Base in Maryland.
    Associated Press/File 1963 | November 20, 2013

    Lyndon B. Johnson was sworn in as president as Jacqueline Kennedy stood at his side in the cabin of the presidential plane on the ground at Love Field in Dallas. Judge Sarah T. Hughes, left, administered the oath. In the background, from left, were Associate Press Secretary Malcolm Kilduff, holding microphone; Jack Valenti, administrative assistant to Johnson; Representative Albert Thomas; Lady Bird Johnson; and Representative Jack Brooks.
    Cecil Stoughton/The White House/JFK Library via Associated Press | November 20, 2013

    A young girl cried outside a memorial service being held for President Kennedy at Harvard University the day after he was killed.
    Ollie Noonan Jr./ Globe Staff/File 1963 | November 20, 2013

    President Kennedy's flag-draped coffin lay in state at the Capitol Rotunda on Nov. 24, 1963.
    Bob Gomel/Time Life Pictures/Getty Images | November 20, 2013

    Jacqueline Kennedy kissed her husband’s casket in the rotunda of the US Capitol, accompanied by their daughter, Caroline.
    Associated Press/File 1963 | November 20, 2013

    Accused assassin Lee Harvey Oswald was shot at point-blank range by Dallas night club owner Jack Ruby in a corridor of Dallas police headquarters on Nov. 24, 1963.
    Bob Jackson/Dallas Times-Herald via Associated Press/file 1963 | November 20, 2013

    Police and reporters reacted after the shooting of Lee Harvey Oswald. On the ground was a hat belonging to Oswald's shooter, Jack Ruby.
    Associated Press/File 1963 | November 20, 2013

    Oswald was placed on a stretcher after being shot in the stomach in Dallas.
    David F. Smith/Associated Press/File 1963 | November 20, 2013

    Jacqueline Kennedy was flanked by her brothers-in-law, Attorney General Robert Kennedy and Senator Edward Kennedy, in the procession carrying the coffin of slain President John F. Kennedy from the White House to St. Matthew's Roman Catholic Church on Nov. 25, 1963.
    Associated Press/FILE 1963 | November 20, 2013

    Caroline Kennedy, 5, looked to her mother, Jacqueline Kennedy, as she clutched her hand, accompanied by John F. Kennedy, Jr., 3, while leaving St. Matthew's Cathedral after the funeral Mass for President Kennedy. Behind them were Robert P. Fitzgerald, first cousin of the president, Rose Kennedy, mother of the president, and Robert F. Kennedy, his brother, far right.
    William C. Allen/Associated Press/File 1963 | November 20, 2013

    Three-year-old John F. Kennedy Jr. saluted his father's casket, three days after the president was assassinated. Widow Jacqueline Kennedy, center, and daughter Caroline Kennedy were accompanied by the late president's brothers, Senator Edward Kennedy, left, and Attorney General Robert Kennedy.
    Associated Press/File 1963 | November 20, 2013

    The funeral procession of President John F. Kennedy went into Arlington Cemetery in Washington.
    National Archives/Newsmakers/File 1963 | November 20, 2013

    The Irish cadet honor guard stood in formation as the US flag was lifted from the coffin of President John F. Kennedy during his funeral services at Arlington National Cemetery. The 18- and 19-year-old cadets were whisked from their remote barracks in County Kildare to perform a special ceremonial drill that had captivated the president when he saw it performed in Dublin months earlier.
    Associated Press | November 20, 2013

    Jacqueline Kennedy held the US flag that covered the coffin of her husband at graveside in Arlington National Cemetery.
    Eddie Adams/Associated Press/File 1963 | November 20, 2013
