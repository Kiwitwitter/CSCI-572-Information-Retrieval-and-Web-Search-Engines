By Kelly Sweeney Globe Correspondent  June 22, 2014

Athletes of the Year

Nick Fofana

Division 1: Newton North

Fofano finished his season by winning the decathlon at the National Outdoor Championships. This spring he claimed the Division 1 title in the 110-meter hurdles and javelin. At All-States, he again won the hurdles and was second in the javelin and third in the high jump.

All-scholastics

Jordan Allen

Thayer, junior

After winning the 100 and 200 and anchoring the winning 4x100 relay at the ISL championships, the junior was named the ISL MVP for running events and earned the All-ISL track award. Allen is a dean’s list student who also plays football.

Nick Carleo

Newburyport, junior

Advertisement

Carleo continued to build his legacy as a top miler. The junior placed first at the Division 4 championships (4:24.43), then took nearly 10 seconds off his time at All-States (4:15.42) to once again take home the prize.

Ben Colello

Lincoln-Sudbury, senior

Colello won the 400-meter hurdles at the Division 1 championships by more than a second with his 54.61 finish. He won the event at All-States in 54.68 and came in third in the 110-meter hurdles in Division 1 (14.57).

Andrew Connolly

Weston,

Connolly won the Division 4 championships in the javelin (186-08), contributing to Weston’s third-place finish. The senior then went on to throw 193-03 at All-States, good for first and almost 7 feet ahead of the runner-up.

Adam Couitt

Somerset Berkley, senior

Couitt was the top high jumper at All-States, leaping 6-8 to take home the top prize. The senior also placed first at the Division 3 championships (6-06) and managed to finish as the runner-up in the 100 meters (11.19).

Evandro DaCosta

Randolph, senior

DaCosta took home the Division 4 title in both the long jump (21-1) and the triple jump (44-4). The senior also managed to place second in the triple jump at All-States (46-5.25).

Evan Dombrowski

St. John’s Prep,

Advertisement

Dombrowski dominated the shot put this season, winning the All-State meet with a throw of 56-9. The senior helped lead St. John’s Prep to the Division 1 championship, placing first at the divisional meet (57-4) while also finishing third in the discus.

P.J. Garmon

Billerica, junior

Garmon took the top prize in the 800 meters at the All-State meet (1:52.97) after finishing first at the Division 1 championships by more than a second (1:54.75). His 4 x 400 relay team also placed second in Division 1.

Craig Green

Falmouth, junior

Green remained unchallenged in the 200 meters and was also a top finisher in the 100. The junior was the Division 3 champion in the 100 and 200 meters (22.86), and he went on to win All-States with his 21.83 finish in the 200.

Mark Hamalian

Bridgewater-Raynham,

Hamalian established himself as the state champion of the new pentathlon event this season, winning with a total of 3,231 points at All-States. The senior also took the title at the Division 1 championships, with an even better score of 3,238 points.

Paul Hogan

Burlington, senior

Hogan was less than two seconds away from breaking Alberto Salazar’s 39-year-old record in the 2-mile at All-States with his 9:01.47 finish. That shaved nearly 13 seconds off his time from the Division 3 championships, where he still took home the title.

Chris Jewett

Woburn, junior

Jewett established himself as the 400-meter champion at All-States (48.78), contributing to Woburn’s first-place finish overall. The junior also won the Division 2 championships in the 400 (48.92) and took fourth place in the 100 meters.

Dillon McGee

Westford, senior

McGee ended his final high school season as the Division 1 champion in the pole vault. His 13-3 finish was a foot and a half longer than the runner-up. The senior also came in sixth at the All-State meet (12-9).

Eric Mellusi

Framingham, senior

Melussi was undefeated in the discus, throwing 165-3 at All-States, almost 10 feet ahead of the next opponent. Melussi also placed first at the Division 1 championships, where he also came in eighth in the shot put.

Lucas Ribeiro

Belmont Hill,

The junior won both the shot put and the discus at the ISTA championships, setting the meet record in the discus. The next week he won the shot and discus at the NEPSTA Division 2 meet and was named the meet MVP.

Kevin Saccone

Old Rochester, junior

Not only did Saccone finish his season as the state champion in the long jump (22-00.75), but he was a top competitor in several events at the Division 4 championships: runner-up in the long jump, and third in the 400-meter and 110-meter hurdles.

Jeremy Thaller

Acton-Boxborough, junior

Thaller was a consistent top finisher in the 100 meters, placing second in the Division 1 championship and third with a personal-best 10.83 at All-States. The junior also contributed to his 4 x 100 relay team’s third-place finish in Division 1.

Relays

4 x 100

Woburn

The crew of Preston Gordon, Anthony Nguyen, Chris Jewett, Marvin Jean, and Nick Maginnis helped the Tanners to the state title. They took first place at All-States (42.48) with a final time not far off the 1995 meet record. They also placed second at the Division 2 Championships in 43.54.

4 x 400

Woburn

Preston Gordon, Anthony Nguyen, Josh Ercolini, and Christopher Jewett contributed to Woburn’s team title with their 4 x 400 relay success. They placed second at the Division 2 championships (3:23.23) before shaving roughly four seconds off their time and taking home the crown at the All-State meet with a time of 3:19.48.

4 x 800

Mansfield

Mansfield’s quartet of Jimmy Murphy, Chris Murphy, Mike Duggan, and Jack Crawford snagged the All-State title, setting a meet record (7:49.87) in the process. They cut almost 2 seconds off last year’s record run by Lincoln-Sudbury.

Coaches of the Year

Jeff Destino

Division 2: Gloucester

Destino has earned back-to-back Northeastern Conference coach of the year awards. He’s been the head coach for the last four years and was an assistant before that. The Fishermen went 6-0 this spring and were the NEC Small champions.

Michael Fowle

Division 2: Woburn

A graduate of Woburn High, Fowle led the boys’ track team to Middlesex League and All-State championships. He boats a 67-23 record in 11 years of coaching, and this season the Tanners were 6-0 under his leadership.

Chuck Ajemian

Division 3: Milton

The three-time Globe Coach of the Year has 32 years of coaching experience. This season he led his alma mater to second place in the Division 3 relays behind state power Bishop Feehan. The New Hampshire grad also coaches indoors.

Kevin Norton

Division 3: Stoneham

The 2003 All-Scholastic coach of the year has been coaching track for more than 15 years. Norton led Stoneham to a 4-2 record this season and is also the girl’s cross-country coach.
