Jason Aldean performs Tom Petty's ‘I Won’t Back Down' on SNL

By Felicia Gans Globe Staff  October 08, 2017

Six days after America’s deadliest mass shooting claimed 58 lives during a concert in Las Vegas, cast members and a guest star on Saturday Night Live took the stage to share in the country’s grief and try to make sense of what happened.

The evening included music from country music singer Jason Aldean, who had been performing on the Las Vegas stage when the shots rang out. Before performing on SNL’s Cold Open, Aldean told the audience that he is still struggling to understand.

“Like everyone, I’m struggling to understand what happened that night and how to pick up the pieces and start to heal,” he said. “So many people are hurting. There are children, parents, brothers, sisters, friends, they’re all part of our family.”

“So I want to say to them,” Aldean continued, “We hurt for you, and we hurt with you. You can be sure that we’re going to walk through these tough times together every step of the way because when America is at its best, our bond and our spirit, it’s unbreakable.”

Aldean then performed Tom Petty’s “I Won’t Back Down.” Petty died Monday night, one day after suffering a cardiac arrest.

Weekend Update hosts Colin Jost and Michael Che tackled the shooting with a dose of humor while discussing their frustration that gun control legislation has been so difficult to achieve in the United States.

Jost said the issue of gun control shouldn’t be a partisan argument, describing the discussion as a “debate in America between people who want common sense gun control and people who are wrong.”

He compared owning 47 guns to owning 47 cats — neither is necessary or should be considered normal.

“No one should own 47 of anything,” he said. “If you own 47 cats, you are not a responsible pet owner. You’re a crazy cat lady. And unlike with guns, the government will actually come and take your cats away because everyone agrees that’s insane.”

“Also this guy bought 33 of his guns in the past year and that didn’t set off any kind of alert?” Jost continued. “If I buy $100 worth of Chick-fil-A, I get a text message from my bank that says, ‘Did you just purchase $100 worth of Chick-fil-A? Please call and tell us this is fraud.’”

Che shared his own frustrations, telling viewers that he’s tired of the narrative that Americans all love guns when the majority of Americans don’t own one.

“Why is it so hard to get gun control in this country? I mean, who are these delicate snowflakes that we can’t just tell, ‘No, you’re not allowed to have 40 guns anymore, Earl,’?” Che said. “From now on, you can have one gun max and six bullets. If you can’t hit what you’re shooting with six bullets, then you don’t need to be shooting at it. Learn karate or use your words.”
