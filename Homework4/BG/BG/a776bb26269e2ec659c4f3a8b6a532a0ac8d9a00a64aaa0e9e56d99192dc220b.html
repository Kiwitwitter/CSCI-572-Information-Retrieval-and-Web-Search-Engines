WEDNESDAY’S SCHOOL ROUNDUP

Lexington follows through on plan in shutout win over Watertown

Joanne Rathe/Globe Staff

Lexington’s junior striker Sancho Maroto (No. 7) advances the ball into scoring position against Watertown’s Alex Abrahamyan in Tuesday’s clash of Middlesex League leaders.

By Logan Mullen Globe Correspondent  October 05, 2017

Lexington had a simple objective entering its Middlesex League soccer match against Watertown: score in the first 10 minutes. Entering the match, the Raiders (6-2-1) had allowed just five goals through eight games, so getting on the board first, and early, was pivotal in Wednesday’s 2-0 victory.

As he’s done many times, Sancho Maroto rose to the task for the Minutemen (7-1-1).

Advertisement

Just four minutes into the game, the junior striker headed in the ball off a cross from senior captain Lucas Aguirre.

“In the meeting at the beginning of the game,” said Maroto, “we said we’ve got to come out strong as a team and score in the first 10 minutes, and we did and set the pace early in the game.”

Maroto and Aguirre connected again in the 78th minute, with the senior finishing the second ball. Maroto distributed the ball to Aguirre, who got past an opposing defender and buried the dagger.

The 72 minutes between the goals, however, were a defensive-minded contest between the teams. Lexington was anchored by a solid back line and keeper Ben Stigberg. The ball often got stuck in midfield, and winning 50-50 balls followed by a well-placed through ball proved the primary source of offense for both sides.

As a result, play on both sides became physical, resulting in three yellow cards, with two issued to Watertown.

Having played a physical game two days prior against Melrose, Lexington coach Keith Beaty felt his team was adequately prepared to play a physical game without getting reckless.

“The physicality side is a big mental thing,” said Beaty. “It’s a physical sport, and the game was physical, but the big thing we’re telling our players is to go out and be physical, but be physical in the right way and when you need to be.”

Hamilton-Wenham 1, Triton 0 — Returning back from injury, senior captain Nick Ong netted the game-winner with 12 minutes remaining for the Generals (3-3-2).

Cohasset 8, Hull 0 — Senior Cam Pattison had one goal and four assists for the fifth-ranked Skippers (10-0).

Carver 2, Middleborough 1 — Freshman Mike Sawicki got Carver on the board first and classmate Cam Shores netted the winner with four minutes to go to push the Crusaders (5-4) above .500.

St. Mary’s 4, Arlington Catholic 0 — On his 18th birthday, senior captain Richie Mateo recorded his 10th shutout of the season for the undefeated Spartans (11-0, 6-0 CCL).

Boys’ cross-country

Newton North 17, Weymouth 41 — North senior Andrew Mah (13:54) set the 2.8-mile course record at Wompatuck State Park in Hingham. The previous mark was 13:56.

Boys’ golf

Medway 237, Hopkinton 239 — Sophomore Matt Peterson carded a 1-under-par 36 as the Mustangs slipped by the Hillers in a Tri-Valley League upset at Maplegate Country Club.

Hamilton-Wenham 149, Pentucket 90 — The Generals (9-2) clinched their second straight Cape Ann League Baker Division title with the victory.

Austin Prep 194, Malden Catholic 188 — Nick Hampoian took home medalist honors with an even-par 35, as the Cougars (11-0) stay perfect.

Girls’ cross-country

Newton North 26, Weymouth 30 — Senior Einat Gavish finished first overall on the 2.8-mile course for the Tigers with a time of 17:40. Not far behind in second place was junior Brianna Tantillo, Weymouth’s first finisher at 18:02.

Girls’ soccer

Tabor 5, Nobles 1 — Sophomore Cat Barry (1 goal, 3 assists), Freshmen Jaydah Bedoya (2 goals) and junior Tali O'Leary (1 goal) led the way for Seawolves (2-1-2).

Foxborough 6, Taunton 0 — Lizzy Davis provided two goals for the Warriors (6-4), while Lily Sykes added a goal and an assist.

King Philip 5, Stoughton 0 — Sophomore Avery Snead pitched in a pair of goals and assists as the Warriors improved to 11-1-0.

Medfield 1, Hopkinton 0 — Senior Alana Cooney scored off a corner kick with only 17 seconds left in regulation to salvage victory for the Warriors (7-2-1).

Lynnfield 3, Amesbury 0 — Sophomore Tori Morelli, as well as seniors Liz Shaievitz and Sydney Santosuosso, found the back of the net for the Pioneers (6-2-1).

Sandwich 1, Nauset 0 — Senior captain Molly McFarland scored the game-winner in the 25th minute of the second half for the Blue Knights.

Field hockey

Brooks 5, Thayer 1 — Juniors Caroline Kukas (1 goal, 2 assists), Caroline O'Keith (1 goal), Charlotte Marks (1 goal, 1 assist) and Caroline Yonce (3 assists), along with sophomore Quin Healy (1 goal) and senior Emma English (1 goal) led Brooks (4-1).
