THE EVOLUTION OF DIVERSITY Black and white: Diversity rose up as an issue in the workforce as a legal requirement in the 1960s, with civil rights pressure and affirmative action requirements. The focus was on how we looked, with our race, gender, and ethnicity driving the discussion.

anna parini for the boston globe

Life experience: From there, diversity evolved from being required to being valued. The focus shifted to wanting workforces that represented the population. A generational divide emerged, forcing employers to rethink hiring practices.

anna parini for the boston globe

Schools of thought: Today diversity is actually "diversity and inclusion," as companies realize how a diverse workforce gives them a broader range of ideas to help grow and attract new economic opportunities and advantages.
