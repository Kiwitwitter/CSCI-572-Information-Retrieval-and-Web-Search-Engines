HIGH SCHOOL GIRLS’ SOCCER NOTEBOOK

Masconomet Regional keeps marching onward

Matthew J. Lee/Globe Statf

Lynnfield High’s Kate Mitchell battled with Masconomet Regional Nikki Arrington for control of the ball during the second half of Monday’s 2-2 draw in Lynnfield.

By Ethan Schroeder Globe Correspondent  October 04, 2017

No one would have blamed the Masconomet Regional girls’ soccer program for encountering initial hiccups this fall.

In the first season for head coach Alison Lecesse , the Chieftains have faced challenges, not unlike many programs.

Start with integrating young talent into the lineup for a squad that finished 13-4-2 last November. Keep the focus on maintaining a solid defensive presence in front of the goal, where there is a two-player rotation at the position. And finally, adjust to the loss of a senior captain who is sidelined with a season-ending injury.

Yet there have been no hiccups. Instead, Masco has just marched on.

In an 8-0-1 start, the Chieftains have been dominant, outscoring foes 34-4, recording six shutouts and six victories by multiple-goal margins to earn the No. 7 ranking in this week’s Globe Top 20 poll. Their first blemish was a 2-2 tie at Lynnfield on Monday afternoon.

According to Lecesse, success has been the product of a team-centric mentality, with notable contributions across the board.

“Individual commitment to a group effort,” said Lecesse, quoting the legendary Vince Lombardi.

An assistant with the program last year who has also coached soccer at Peabody High and Gordon College, she added “Everyone has bought into the team mentality. Something about this team is just clicking very well. They’re surpassing where I hoped we’d be at this point.”

Junior Rachel Murphy is flourishing in her new role at sweeper. Senior captain Nikki Arrington paces Masco with seven goals and five assists.

And a number of newcomers have made a rather seamless transition to the varsity. Sophomore Bella Young has stepped up from the junior varsity to provide depth on defense. Two freshmen, Allie Vaillancourt and Morgan Bovardi , have bolstered an attack that was already prolific.

“These girls have grown into their roles quickly, and that has to do with the leadership with have,” Lecesse said.

“They’ve got the talent, and then our older players have helped them acclimate.”

The offense, said Lecesse, stems from strong midfield play. Consistent possession control by senior Olivia Bonacorso and junior Sophia Woodland leads to runs down the flanks of the field. Quick, limited touches allow for a swift attack and clinical finishes.

“Our attack can make it happen from wherever they are in the zone,” Lecesse said. “Even the defensive backs are getting involved.”

The defense is the epitome of Masconomet’s ability to adapt.

For starters, Lecesse regularly rotates two players in goal, returning junior Carolyn Hanson and sophomore Maria Lantzakis , creating healthy competition.

Hanson starts the match in net and Lantzakis starts the second half. The result is two keepers pushing each other for improvement each day, averaging a collective 0.44 goals allowed per game.

“The dynamic has definitely taken some time to get used to, but they’ve both found success thus far,” Lecesse said. “God forbid there’s ever an injury, we’ve got people to step in.”

Injuries have already become a reality for the Chieftains.

In the fourth game of the season, a Sept. 18 matchup against Cape Ann rival Amesbury, senior captain Allysha Proulx-Walter sustained a broken leg.

The loss of Masco’s defensive anchor left a void. But it highlighted the team’s flexibility.

Ask Proulx-Walter about the injury, and she briefly reflected before beaming about her replacement.

“Carly [Favazza ] has stepped in and done a wonderful job,” Proulx-Walter said. “When you’ve got strong players ready to step up, it’s easy to make substitutions.”

Talent is only one piece of the puzzle when moving with such fluidity. For a Masconomet squad that continues to change by the day, a focused mindset is the key to keeping it all together.

“This start has been great, but it’s only the beginning,” Lecesse said.

“We know we have to take it day by day and game by game. Looking too far ahead is what trips teams up.”

Nonetheless, the Chieftains will certainly be looking to improve upon a 2016 season in which they they earned the fifth seed in the Division 2 North tourney, but were shut out by Andover, 2-0, in the first round.

It’s hard to say they haven’t gotten a good jump on that goal.

“We have to earn it,” said Proulx-Walter. “Each game, we know we [face] a team eager to knock us off, so we’ve got no choice but to give it our all.”

Corner Kicks

■ Last week’s matchup between No. 5 Norwell and No. 20 Rockland was more than just a South Shore League battle. The game — a 5-1 win for Norwell — was a Cohasset reunion for coaches Kara Connerty (Norwell) and Lauren Rizzotti (Rockland).

As the junior varsity coach at Cohasset more than a decade ago, Connerty had Rizzotti as a player. The pair also coach against each other during lacrosse season.

“The way that she’s coaching them, she really just has a positive approach and the girls are responding well,” Connerty said of Rizzotti.

■ They say pressure can create diamonds, so expect strong teams to come out of the Hockomock League Kelley-Rex division come tourney time; Mansfield (8), Oliver Ames (15), Franklin (16) and King Philip (17) are all currently ranked in the Globe’s Top 20.

■  Whitman-Hanson remained at the top of topdrawersoccer.com’s Northeast regional poll this week, with Central Catholic staying put at No. 2. Natick (7) and Hingham (8) also made the Top 10.

Players of the Week

Ella Gagnon, Belmont: The No. 9 Marauders surged into the top 10 this week thanks in part to Gagnon’s second-half winner in last Wednesday’s tilt with previously-ranked Arlington .

Lizzie Shaievitz, Lynnfield: It’s been five goals in three games for the Saint Anselm recruit. Shaievitz reached double-digit goals on the season with a headed equalizer in Monday’s 2-2 draw with No. 7 Masconomet.

Avery Snead, King Philip: The sophomore striker scored a total of three goals in wins over No. 8 Mansfield and No. 16 Franklin. She leads the Hockomock League with 15 goals through 11 games.

Emma Tucker, Notre Dame Academy (Hingham): A junior captain, Tucker has scored five goals in her past three games for the Cougars (5-2-0). “She’s probably our most technical and most dangerous player,” said NDA coach Kelly Turner.

Kristi Vierra, Norwell: Vierra scored her 14th goal and tallied her sixth assist in Monday’s 6-0 win over Carver. The sophomore midfielder has helped lead the No. 5 Clippers to an undefeated start.

Games to Watch

■ East Bridgewater at No. 5 Norwell (Wednesday 4 p.m.): The No. 5 Clippers pulled out a 3-2 victory in the season opener, and will look to keep their undefeated record against the defending D4 champion Vikings.

■ Scituate at No. 4 Hingham (Friday 4 p.m.): The Sailors managed a tie against No. 2 Whitman Hanson — the only team that has beaten the No.4 Harborwomen this season.

■ No. 7 Masconomet at Manchester Essex (Friday 3:45 p.m.): A pair of undefeated Cape Ann league teams will meet before the weekend. The No. 7 Chieftains (8-0-1) lead the Kinney Division, while the Hornets (7-0-2) sit atop the Baker.

■ No. 14 Westford Academy at No. 10 Acton Boxboro (Saturday 7 p.m.): The Dual County League always promises a few enticing matchups every week. The No. 10 Colonials look to rebound at home against the undefeated Grey Ghosts after suffering a loss last week.

■ No. 18 Brookline at Danvers (Saturday 6:45 p.m.): The unranked Falcons (5-2) will look to pull off a home upset over the No. 18 Warriors and standout junior keeper Katherine McElroy.
