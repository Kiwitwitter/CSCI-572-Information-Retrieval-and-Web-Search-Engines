A few things about ‘Castle Rock’

Nicholas Hunt/Getty Images

“Castle Rock” costars (from left) Andre Holland, Bill Skarsgard, Melanie Lynskey, and Sissy Spacek took part in a panel discussion at New York Comic Con over the weekend.

By Matthew Gilbert Globe Staff  October 10, 2017

Here’s what we know about “Castle Rock,” the psychological-horror series based on Stephen King’s fiction that’s due on Hulu next year.

1. Three of the lead cast members have already worked in the King world. Sissy Spacek starred in “Carrie,” Melanie Lynskey was in the miniseries “Rose Red,” and Bill Skarsgard played Pennywise in “It.” Other cast members include Terry O’Quinn (“Lost”), Andre Holland (“The Knick”), and Jane Levy (“Suburgatory”).

2. The show, whose executive producers include J.J. Abrams, is being written by creators Sam Shaw and Dustin Thomason. The idea is to introduce an original story, but then bring in themes and characters from the King universe. So far, we don’t know which King characters will show up in “Castle Rock,” and what the original story line will involve. The characters played by Spacek, Skarsgard, Lynskey, and Holland have been created for the series.

3. King and Abrams, two gods of entertainment, previously worked with Hulu on the miniseries adaptation of King’s “11.22.63,” which starred James Franco.

4. We know very few story facts, but we do know that Skarsgard’s character is imprisoned in Shawshank Prison, Holland will play his attorney, Spacek will play Holland’s adoptive mother, and O’Quinn will play a pillar of the Castle Rock community. Scott Glenn is also on board as retired sheriff Alan Pangborn from King’s “The Dark Half” and “Needful Things.”

5. “Castle Rock” is an anthology show, with different story lines and casts each season. The first season will be 10 episodes.

6. The show, set in Maine, is the first scripted TV series in 30 years to be filmed in Massachusetts. It’s filming at New England Studio in Devens and Orange.

7. King named his Maine town Castle Rock after the location in William Golding’s novel “Lord of the Flies.” Castle Rock is also the name of a production company, which cofounder Rob Reiner named for King’s town after he had great success adapting King’s story “The Body” into the movie “Stand By Me.”
