By Nancy Shohet West Globe Correspondent  October 06, 2017

HANDMADE CRAFTS ON DISPLAY The New England Woodcarvers group holds its annual Spirit of Wood Show on Sunday, Oct. 15, from 9 a.m. to 3 p.m. at Middlesex Community College, 591 Spring St., Building 8, Bedford. More than 150 pieces will be judged in the woodcarving competition. Other highlights include carving tool and equipment vendors; craftspeople selling their work; carving demonstrations; and seminars on sharpening tools, painting carvings, basic carving, and scroll saw carving. Admission is $4; free for youths under 17. For more details go to www.newc.org.

The Concord Piecemakers Quilt Guild holds its Biennial Quilt Show on Friday, Oct. 13, from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m., and Saturday, Oct. 14, 10 a.m. to 4 p.m., at St. Matthew’s United Methodist Church, 435 Central St., Acton. The show includes over 100 member quilts, a vintage quilt display, a boutique, vendors, a raffle quilt, demonstrations, cookie sale, book and magazine sale, coffee shop and a children’s scavenger hunt. General admission is $7; children under 12 free. For more information, go to www.concordpiecemakers.org .

GETTING OUTDOORS Newton Cemetery arborist Robert Reardon offers a guided tour showcasing the wide variety of trees in Newton Cemetery’s arboretum on Saturday, Oct. 14, at 1 p.m. The tour lasts approximately 90 minutes. Admission is free. The cemetery is located at 791 Walnut St., Newton. Meet just inside the main gate in front of the office. For more information, call 617-332-0047 or go to www.newcemcorp.org.

Celebrate the season at Autumn Spirits Weekend on Saturday, Oct. 14, and Sunday, Oct. 15, from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. at Tower Hill Botanic Garden. Enjoy a fairy house walk, food trucks, story time, games on the lawn, live music, face painting, crafts, a guided woods walk and a garden tour — and for the adults, beer, cider, and wine tastings. Visitors are encouraged to wear woodland fairy or animal costumes. Many events on the program are included with regular admission, which is $15 for adults, $10 for seniors 65 and older, $5 for youth 6 to 18, and free for children under 6; a few events require an extra fee. Tower Hill Botanic Garden is located at 11 French Drive, Boylston. Call 508-869-6111 or go to www.towerhillbg.org/fall-fest for more information.

HISTORIC HOUSE TOUR The Lexington Historical Society presents a house tour with the theme “The Golden Age of Merriam Hill, 1865-1940” on Sunday, Oct. 15, from 11 a.m. to 5 p.m. Savor the atmosphere of a bygone era with a house tour on Merriam Hill, a display of vintage cars, live music, costumed interpreters, floral displays from Lexington Field & Garden Club, and an exhibit of the Lexington Historical Society’s ephemera. Advance tickets are $30 members; $35 nonmembers. Same-day tickets are $35 members; $40 nonmembers. For tickets or more information, go to www.LexingtonHistory.org or call 781-862-1703. All tickets may be picked up beginning at 10 a.m. on the day of the event at the Lexington Historical Society office, 13 Depot Square, Lexington.

ON STAGE New Repertory Theatre presents David Mamet’s play “Oleanna,” on the MainStage Theater at the Mosesian Center for the Arts, 321 Arsenal St., Watertown, with performances Oct. 14 to Nov. 5. Tickets are $35 to $65. For tickets or a complete performance schedule, call 617-923- 8487 or go to www.newrep.org..

Theatreworks USA presents the off-Broadway family musical “Pete the Cat” on Monday, Oct. 9, at 11 a.m. and 1 p.m. as part of the Magic Ark Performing Arts Series for Families at the Leventhal-Sidman Jewish Community Center, 333 Nahanton St., Newton. Tickets are $18 general admission and can be purchased at www.bostonjcc.org/magicark or by calling 617-558-6486.

MUSICALLY SPEAKING Songwriter, pianist, and Indian Hill Music faculty member Dan Masterson performs alternative pop-rock songs and shares the stories behind his compositions on Friday, Oct. 13, at 7 p.m. at Indian Hill Music, 36 King St., Littleton. Tickets are $15 (free for IHM students). For more information, call 978-486-9524 ext. 116 or go to www.indianhillmusic.org.

Canadian performer April Verch and her band will perform at the Circle of Friends Coffeehouse on Saturday, Oct. 14, at 8 p.m. Verch is a multitalented native of Ottawa Valley, Canada, known for her fiddling, vocals, and step dancing. The Circle of Friends Coffeehouse is located in the Meetinghouse at 262 Chestnut St., Franklin. Doors open at 7:30 p.m. Tickets for this show are $25. Call 508-528-2541 or go to www.circlefolk.org to purchase tickets or for more information.
