COLLEGE ROUNDUP

Wisconsin holds off Northwestern

Morry Gash/AP

Wisconsin's Chris Orr sacks Northwestern's Clayton Thorson, one of eight sacks the Badgers racked up in the game.

Associated Press  September 30, 2017

Jonathan Taylor ran for two touchdowns to help the offense overcome a sluggish start and No. 10 Wisconsin’s defense swarmed Northwestern before holding on for a 33-24 win on Saturday in Madison, Wis.

Alex Hornibrook threw for 197 yards and a touchdown, settling down after two interceptions in the first half. Garrett Dooley had three of Wisconsin’s eight sacks.

Northwestern’s last drive was thwarted when quarterback Clayton Thorson couldn’t find a receiver out of his own end zone and was sacked by safety D'Cota Dixon for a safety.

Thorson was having a good quarter until then, throwing two TD passes in less than two minutes. The second to Garrett Dickerson with 2:53 left got the Wildcats within a touchdown.

But Wisconsin (4-0, 1-0 Big Ten) stood up the Wildcats (2-2, 0-1) again late to seal the win in each team’s conference opener.

It was all Wisconsin in the second half until Northwestern’s late push in the fourth.

Hornibrook hit Quintez Cephus on a 61-yard pass to the Northwestern 11 on the Badgers’ first drive of the third quarter. Taylor scored on the next play to give Wisconsin the lead for good, 14-10 with 9:16 left in the third.

Safety Natrell Jamerson had a 36-yard interception return for a touchdown for a 31-10 lead with 9:54 left in the game, what initially looked like the final blow to Northwestern.

But the Wildcats came back. Wisconsin allowed two quick touchdowns before Dixon’s safety finally finished off Northwestern.

Taylor, a freshman, finished with 80 yards on 19 carries. Dixon had a team-high 12 tackles and 1½ sacks.

Hornibrook was 11 of 20. Thorson finished 29 of 45 for 219 yards and three touchdowns and two interceptions.

Penn State 45, Indiana 14 — DaeSean Hamilton caught nine passes for 122 yards with three TDs and became the school’s career receptions leader as the No. 4 Nittany Lions (5-0, 2-0 Big Ten) routed the Hoosiers (2-2, 0-2) in State College, Pa.

Hamilton caught his first two touchdowns from quarterback Trace McSorley and a third from running back Saquon Barkley to cap the scoring in the fourth quarter. He hauled in his 180th career catch in the fourth quarter to set the record held by Deon Butler since 2008.

Barkley returned the game’s opening kickoff 98 yards for a touchdown and finished with 221 yards to pad his Heisman Trophy résumé.

Penn State turned two first-quarter Indiana turnovers into touchdowns to spark the rout. McSorley added a rushing TD, Nick Scott scored on a fumble return, and Tyler Davis added a pair of field goals for Penn State.

Georgia 41, Tennessee 0 — Jake Fromm threw a touchdown pass and ran for two more scores in Knoxville, Tenn., as the No. 7 Bulldogs (5-0, 2-0 SEC) sent the Volunteers (3-2, 0-2) to their first shutout loss in nearly a quarter-century.

Nick Chubb added 109 yards rushing as Georgia raced to its first 5-0 start since 2012, the last year the Bulldogs reached the SEC championship game. Tennessee was shut out for the first time since a 31-0 loss to Florida in 1994.

The Bulldogs got more good news in the fourth quarter when quarterback Jacob Eason made his first appearance since spraining his left knee in the season opener. Fromm was 7 of 15 for 84 yards and rushed for 20 yards before giving way to Eason with the game in hand.

Auburn 49, Mississippi State 10 — Jarrett Stidham passed for 264 yards and two touchdowns, and Kerryon Johnson ran for 116 yards and three scores to lead the No. 13 Tigers (4-1, 2-0 SEC) over the Bulldogs (3-2, 1-2) in Auburn, Ala.

Auburn etablished itself as the most likely challenger to No. 1 Alabama in the Western Division with an explosive offensive performance. Mississippi State has lost two straight lopsided games since blowing out LSU to start a string of three matchups with top 15 teams.

Stidham launched a handful of deep balls for Auburn, including a 47-yard touchdown to a wide-open Will Hastings and a 57-yarder to Eli Stove from his own end zone. He wound up 13-of-16 passing.

Mississippi State’s Nick Fitzgerald passed for 157 yards and ran for 56 but was intercepted twice.

Louisville 55, Murray State 10 — Lamar Jackson had a short but productive day, throwing for 249 yards and three TDs after playing just over a half as the No. 17 Cardinals (4-1) routed the visiting Racers (1-4).

The reigning Heisman Trophy winner, who had one play after halftime, added 100 yards rushing and a TD on seven carries. He’s now just 13 yards short of becoming the seventh player in ACC history to reach 10,000 total career yards.

Jawon Pass stepped in after Jackson’s day ended, throwing for 115 yards and one touchdown and added another TD on the ground with 29 seconds left in the game. The two completed passes to 14 separate players as the Cardinals offense totaled 364 yards through the air. Louisville rolled up 312 yards on the ground.

South Florida 61, East Carolina 31 — Quinton Flowers threw for two touchdowns and ran for another, and the No. 18 Bulls (5-0, 2-0 American) extended their school-record winning streak to 10, routing the Pirates (1-4, 1-1) in Greenville, S.C.

D'Ernest Johnson rushed for 111 yards and two touchdowns, Marquez Valdes-Scantling took an end-around 75 yards for a touchdown and caught a 4-yard scoring pass from Flowers, and Auggie Sanchez returned an interception 43 yards for a TD.

South Florida overcame some rare struggles on defense and remained perfect under first-year coach Charlie Strong by scoring on 10 of the 12 possessions with the starting offense. USF outscored the East Carolina, 30-7, in the second half.

Florida 38, Vanderbilt 24 — In Gainesville, Fla., Lamical Perine scored three touchdowns, Malik Davis added two, and the No. 21 Gators (3-1, 3-0 SEC) beat the Commodores (3-2, 0-2) despite losing quarterback Luke Del Rio to a broken left collarbone.

Perine bookended two 1-yard plunges around a 13-yard scamper as the Gators stayed unbeaten in SEC play and beat the Commodores for the 26th time in the last 27 meetings.

Perine, a cousin of former Oklahoma and current Washington Redskins running back Samaje Perine, finished with 58 yards on 15 carries.

Davis, a freshman, ran 17 times for 124 yards. His final run was huge. On a fourth-down play, Davis broke through the line, stumbled and then regained his footing for a 39-yard score that sealed the victory.

Notre Dame 52, Miami (Ohio) 17 — Brandon Wimbush threw for three touchdowns and ran for another in South Bend, Ind., as the No. 22 Fighting Irish (4-1) rolled over the RedHawks (2-3) for the 900th victory in school history.

Josh Adams added touchdown runs of 72 and 59 yards in the first half before leaving the game with an injury to his left ankle. Adams, who entered the game as the No. 9 rusher in the country, still led the Notre Dame with 159 yards on the ground despite the short night.

Wimbush, who had his eighth rushing touchdown of the season in the first quarter, finished with 119 passing yards in three quarters and had 36 yards on 11 carries. The Irish led the RedHawks, 45-14 at the half, the most first-half points scored by an Irish team under Kelly.

SMU 49, UConn 28 — Ben Hicks passed for 280 yards and four touchdowns as the Mustangs (4-1, 1-0 American) pulled away with three fourth-quarter touchdowns to beat the Huskies (1-3, 0-2) in Dallas.

Trey Quinn caught 15 passes for 116 yards and two TDs and Courtland Sutton finished with seven catches for 112 yards and two scores for SMU.

Bryant Shirreffs passed for 408 yards and two TDs for UConn but was sacked eight times.

Troy 24, LSU 21 — Jordan Chunn rushed for 191 yards and a touchdown as the Trojans (4-1) stunned the No. 25 Tigers (3-2) in Baton Rouge, La.

Brandon Silvers was 16-for-28 passing for 157 yards for Troy, which has won four straight since a season-opening loss at Boise State. Danny Etling was 17 of 25 for 198 yards for fading LSU, which narrowly avoided an upset against Syracuse last Saturday after losing to Mississippi State two weeks ago.

Lafayette 10, Holy Cross 7 — Jeffrey Kordenbrock’s 43-yard field goal on the last play of the game gave the Leopards (1-4, 1-0 Patriot League) their first win of the season. The lone score for the host Crusaders (2-3, 1-1) came on Miles Alexander’s 8-yard run in the second quarter.

Men’s hockey

BU 4, Union 1 — Patrick Harper had two goals and an assist, Jake Oettinger stopped 31 shots, and the second-ranked Terriers opened the season with a victory over the No. 16 Dutchmen at Agganis Arena.

Dante Fabbro’s goal at 12:11 of the final period snapped a 1-1 deadlock. Harper, who assisted on Fabbro’s goal, followed with his second goal before Shane Bowers closed the scoring with an empty-netter at 19:50.

Brett Supinski scored for Union.
