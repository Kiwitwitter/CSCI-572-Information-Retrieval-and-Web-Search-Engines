Sylvia Fowles leads Minnesota Lynx to fourth WNBA title

  • E-Mail

    Share via e-mail

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Google+
  • LinkedIn
Comments Print
The Boston Globe Tweet Share

Associated Press  October 04, 2017

Sylvia Fowles had 17 points and broke her WNBA Finals record by grabbing 20 rebounds to lead the Minnesota Lynx to their fourth championship in seven years with an 85-76 victory over the Los Angeles Sparks in Game 5 on Wednesday night in Minneapolis. Fowles was named MVP of the Finals. Maya Moore had 18 points and 10 rebounds and hit a huge runner with 26 seconds to play, Lindsay Whalen had 17 points and eight assists and Seimone Augustus added 14 points, 6 assists and 6 boards to help the Lynx move into a tie with the Houston Comets for most titles in league history. Candace Parker had 19 points, 15 rebounds and 5 assists for the Sparks, who were looking to become the first repeat champions in 15 years . . . Dan Hughes, 62, who spent 11 years coaching the San Antonio Stars before retiring in 2016, was named coach of the WNBA’s Seattle Storm.

Colleges

NCAA to examine transfer reforms

An NCAA committee is developing a proposal that would eliminate a school’s ability to essentially block an athlete from transferring while an idea to allow some undergraduate athletes to switch schools and be immediately eligible to compete will not be considered. The NCAA said Wednesday that the Division 1 Council could introduce legislation as early as January. The Division I Transfer Working Group met this week and considered feedback on transfer reforms from more than 2,000 administrators, coaches and faculty members. The NCAA says the most significant change that would be considered this year is taking away from coaches and schools the ability to restrict aid to athletes after transferring. The possibility of tying academic benchmarks to immediate eligibility will not be considered.

Fund created to aid injured Harvard football freshman

Advertisement

The Harvard Varsity Club, in conjunction with Harvard Athletics and Friends of Harvard Football, created the Benson M. Abercrombie ’21 Fund to aid freshman football player Ben Abercrombie recover from a serious cervical injury, which the cornerback from Hoover, Ala., suffered in his first game for Harvard on Sept. 16 at Rhode Island. The fund, which raised $70,000 the first hour it went live, will provide support to Abercrombie’s family as well as future Harvard student-athletes with severe or catastrophic injuries. “We’ve had a tremendous response,” Harvard coach Tim Murphy said at the weekly New England Football Writers luncheon at Dillon Fieldhouse. “I am really proud of the support from the Harvard community.”

Donations to the fund can be made on the Harvard Varsity Club website or by calling 617-495-3535.

Golf

FedEx Cup champ Thomas named Tour’s player of year

Justin Thomas was voted PGA Tour player of the year for his remarkable season of five victories, his first major championship and the FedEx Cup title. Thomas all but locked up the Tour award when he closed with a 66 at East Lake and was runner-up at the Tour Championship to capture the FedEx Cup. Xander Schauffele, who won the Tour Championship, was voted PGA Tour rookie of the year.

US women’s soccer star sorry over Disney incident

Alex Morgan apologized on social media for an incident at Disney World over the weekend. Morgan, the star for the World Cup-winning US women's national team who also plays for the Orlando Pride, was among three soccer players ejected from an Epcot park restaurant after authorities said the players were verbally aggressive with park security and other guests . . . Castroneves, however, will still drive for Roger Penske at the 2018 Indy 500.
