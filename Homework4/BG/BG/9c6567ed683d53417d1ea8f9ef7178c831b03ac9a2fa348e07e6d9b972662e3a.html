After 11-game winning streak, Chiefs’ season suddenly over

The Boston Globe Tweet Share

By Julian Benbow Globe Staff  January 17, 2016

FOXBOROUGH — It had been so long since the Chiefs lost a game that it almost forgot what it felt like.

The disappointment felt foreign.

Advertisement

“I don’t even know how to feel right now, it’s been so long since we lost,” running back Charcandrick West said. “It’s heartbreaking right now.”

For 11 straight weeks, they were essentially playing elimination games. A 1-5 start had them dangling over the cliff. The only team to reach the postseason after a start so bad was the 1970 Cincinnati Bengals.

Their only choice was to string wins together, even after star running back Jamaal Charles went down with an ACL injury.

A 23-13 win over the Pittsburgh Steelers on Oct. 25 snowballed into a 10-game winning streak that propelled them into the playoffs. They routed the Houston Texans, 30-0, in the wild-card round for the franchise’s first playoff victory since 2013.

When the ride finally came to an end with a 27-20 loss to the Patriots at Gillette Stadium on Saturday, it was difficult for the locker room to process how much of a blur the past four months had been.

Advertisement

“It’s crazy how fast it ends, right?” said quarterback Alex Smith. “It’s over. That’s it. It’s over right now — just like that.”

For a team that did so much to dig itself out of a hole that seemed entirely too deep, coach Andy Reid said there was a lot to be taken from the loss.

“It gives us a great example of where we need to be,” Reid said. “So this is a good experience for us. That’s the way we’ll take it. We’ll learn from our mistakes and how you go about your business if you want to continue to play in the postseason.”

Momentum wasn’t an issue for the Chiefs. But injuries were. A mild high ankle sprain kept wide receiver Jeremy Maclin out of practice all week. A knee injury did the same for linebacker Justin Houston.

How much they could contribute was a question they had to wait until game time to answer.

With just two catches for 23 yards before aggravating his injury, Maclin was essentially a non-factor.

“Maclin did as much as he could do,” Reid said.

Though he tried to gut through it, Maclin said eventually the pain was too much.

“It’s tough, you know,” Maclin said. “Any time you lose a football game, period, it’s tough, but obviously when you know what’s on the line, losing a playoff game after the run we were on, this is tough to swallow. It was tough but I told myself I was going to go out there and do what I can to help this team. I tried to play and I did and then it came to a point where the pain kind of took over and it was time for me to come out of the game.”

No one in the locker room wanted to use the injuries as excuses, but they all recognized the lengths teammates went to push through them.

“We had a lot of guys today that we thought weren’t going to play and they fought through it for this team,’’ West said. “It just shows you the character we have in this locker room.”

Wide receiver Jason Avant, who spent the bulk of his career playing for Reid in Philadelphia when the Eagles were a powerhouse in the NFC East, said he could see value in the loss as well.

“Hopefully we take a lot out of it,” Avant said. “There is no team that is head and shoulders above the rest and we were one of those teams that could win. To go up against the champion and see the fight you have to have and the tenacity and the perseverance you have to have, it can’t do anything but good.”
