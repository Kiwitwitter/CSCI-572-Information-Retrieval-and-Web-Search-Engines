  September 15, 2017

MUSIC

Rock & Pop

BIG THIEF Adrienne Lenker, the vocalist of and songwriting force behind this superlative Brooklyn quartet, writes intricately detailed, at times harrowing lyrics that her bandmates swaddle in guitar fuzz and swaying rhythms. Sept. 17, 8 p.m. $18, $16 advance. The Sinclair, Cambridge. 617-547-5200, www.sinclaircambridge.com

Advertisement

ED SHEERAN A big swath of 2017’s pop landscape can, for better or worse, be credited to this British busker-turned-arena-headliner, who’s penned hits for Justin Bieber (the acerbic “Love Yourself”) and One Direction (the delicate “Little Things”) in addition to his own radio-dominating smashes like the John Mayer-echoing ballad “Thinking Out Loud” and the lite-reggae flirtation “Shape of You.” Sept. 22-23, 7:30 p.m. $39.50 and up. TD Garden. 800-745-3000, www.tdgarden.com

QUICKSAND The rejuvenated post-hardcore act, who released two pummeling albums of twisted-metal guitars and bellowed vocals in the ’90s before taking a lengthy break, hits Boston ahead of “Interiors,” its third full-length and first in 22 years. Sept. 23, 8 p.m. $30, $28 advance. Paradise Rock Club. 617-562-8800, www.crossroadspresents.com

MAURA JOHNSTON

Folk & World

EMILY MURE A singer-songwriter from New York City with a lovely, sibilant singing voice and a penchant for confessional, stately chamber folk, Mure celebrates the release of her third album, “Worth,” with a full band (including not just one, but two cellos) this Wednesday. Sept. 20, 8 p.m. $15. Club Passim, Cambridge. 617-492-7679, www.passim.org

JAYME STONE’S FOLKLIFE Jayme Stone’s website characterizes him thusly: “Banjoist — Composer — Instigator.” His latest instigation is “Folklife,” which grew out of his last one, the “Lomax Project,” and again finds him gathering “a community of like-minded musicians” to unearth old field recordings and folk songs and “recycle, reimagine and recast them.” Sept. 20, 7 p.m. $15-$20. Shalin Liu Performance Center, Rockport. 978-546-7391, www.rockportmusic.org

Advertisement

SAM OUTLAW/MICHAELA ANNE Nothing but country here, courtesy of Angeleno Sam Outlaw and Music City transplant Michaela Anne. Rumor has it that Ms. M. will do double duty, opening the evening with a solo set and then singing and playing as part of Outlaw’s band. Sept. 23, 10 p.m. $12. Atwood’s Tavern, Cambridge. 800-838-3006. www.brownpapertickets.com

STUART MUNRO

Jazz & Blues

THE GEORGE GARZONE TRIO The astonishing, Boston-based tenor saxophonist is an undersung world-class talent who always tears it up. With bassist Oscar Stagnaro and drummer Mark Walker. Sept. 19, 7:30 p.m. $10. Jocko’s Jazz at the Sahara Club, 34 Bates St., Methuen. 603-898-1591, www.jockosjazz.com

ANA POPOVIC The sizzling Belgrade-born blues singer, songwriter, and guitarist has been nominated for numerous Blues Music Awards, appeared on the covers of Vintage Guitar and Guitar Player, and was the only female guitarist featured in the nationwide “Experience Hendrix” tour alongside such icons as Buddy Guy and Bootsy Collins. Sept. 21, 8 p.m. $34. Bull Run Restaurant, 215 Great Road, Shirley. 978-425-4311, www.bullrunrestaurant.com

STAN STRICKLAND/JOSH ROSEN QUARTET Saxophonist Strickland and pianist Rosen have been performing as a playful, profound, and near-telepathic duo for a decade. The four-way dialogue of their quartet, featuring bassist Bruno Råberg and drummer Dave Fox, ramps up the creative stakes even further. Sept. 22, 8 p.m. $15-$20. Third Life Studio, 33 Union Square, Somerville. 617-628-0916, www.thirdlifestudio.com

KEVIN LOWENTHAL

Classical

BOSTON SYMPHONY ORCHESTRA Kicking off the BSO’s centenary tribute to Leonard Bernstein, music director Andris Nelsons opens the new season on Sept. 22 with an all-Bernstein gala program featuring soprano Julia Bullock, mezzo Frederica von Stade, and principal flute Elizabeth Rowe. On Sept. 23, Nelsons will be back on the podium with Mahler’s Symphony No. 1 and Haydn’s Symphony No. 103. Symphony Hall. 617-266-1200, www.bso.org

EMMANUEL MUSIC A new Late Night at Emmanuel series debuts this week with a program titled “Howl” and featuring Lee Hyla’s white-hot setting of Allen Ginsberg’s iconic poem for string quartet and speaker. Also on the bill will be Ginsberg settings by Elena Ruehr and Andy Vores, as well as the premiere of John Harbison’s “Schwartzsongs,” based on the poetry of Boston’s own Lloyd Schwartz. Sept. 23, 8 p.m. and 10 p.m., Emmanuel Church. 617-536-3356, www.emmanuelmusic.org 

BOSTON NEW MUSIC FESTIVAL New music initiatives from across the city come together under one banner for this eclectic festival, whose first performance features Boston Musica Viva in works by Elliott Carter and Bernard Hoffer under Richard Pittman’s veteran baton. Festival runs Sept. 23 to Oct. 1, multiple venues, www.juventasmusic.com/bostonnewmusicfestival.html 

JEREMY EICHLER   

ARTS

Theater

REVERSIBLE A hypnotically beautiful exploration of the role family history plays in the shaping of individual identity, performed by circus artists whose collective virtuosity is something to see. Written, directed, and choreographed by the immensely gifted Gypsy Snider, “Reversible’’ works on both an aesthetic and emotional level. Through Sept. 24. Production by The 7 Fingers. Presented by ArtsEmerson. At Cutler Majestic Theatre, Boston. 617-824-8400, www.artsemerson.org

GYPSY As the deluded but unstoppable Mama Rose, Leigh Barrett carries this production — which is sometimes in need of carrying — with the avidity and assurance of a supremely skilled performer who knows she is tackling one of the juiciest roles in all of musical theater. Directed and choreographed by Rachel Bertone. With a book by Arthur Laurents, lyrics by Stephen Sondheim, and music by Jule Styne — each of them working at the top of his game. Through Oct. 8. Lyric Stage Company of Boston. 617-585-5678, www.lyricstage.com

FACELESS A young woman falls in love with a member of ISIS, converts to Islam, and is put on trial for conspiring to commit acts of terrorism. Her prosecutor is a practicing Muslim and a graduate of Harvard Law School. Selina Fillinger’s drama is directed by David J. Miller. Through Oct. 7. Zeitgeist Stage Company. At Plaza Black Box Theatre, Boston Center for the Arts. 617-933-8600, www.zeitgeiststage.com

DON AUCOIN

Dance

DANCE UP World Music/CRASHarts celebrates dance talent from around New England with this promising showcase encompassing a range of styles, from tap and hip-hop to contemporary dance. The featured artists are the Wondertwins, Ian Berg’s tap company Subject:Matter, james morrow/ The Movement, Kat Nasti Dance, Doppelgänger Dance Collective, and Alexander Davis Dance. Sept. 22-23. $28-$32. Institute of Contemporary Art. 617-876-4275, www.worldmusic.org

LORRAINE CHAPMAN THE COMPANY The veteran choreographer and her troupe present a wide-ranging evening of dance theater that includes Chapman’s work, a new work created by Plymouth State University Dance Director Amanda Whitworth with the company and PSU dance alumni, and a new solo choreographed especially for Chapman by Paula Josa-Jones. Sept. 22-23. $15-$30. Plymouth State University Silver Center for the Arts, Plymouth, N.H. 603-535-2787, www.plymouth.edu/silver-center

BU DANCE SHOWCASE 2017 The dance program at Boston University showcases its faculty and students, as well as a select group of guest artists, at this lively annual event. In addition to performances by Boston Ballet’s training company BBII, the program will feature choreography by Janelle Gilchrist, Lynn Modell, Margot Parsons, DeAnna Pellecchia, Marin Orlosky Randow, and Liz Roncka. Sept. 22-23. $12-$20. Boston University Dance Theater. 617-358-2500, www.bu.edu/fitrec

KAREN CAMPBELL

Galleries

CLAIRE ASHLEY: (((CRZ.F.4.NRS.AAK))) The Scottish artist blows up vaunted notions of 20th-century art in roughly 30 cartoony and kinetic inflatables that link Abstract Expressionism, Pop art, and monumental sculpture. Her coded title translates as: “Crazy Female Foreigners Alive and Kicking.” Through Dec. 3. 808 Gallery, Boston University, 808 Commonwealth Ave. 617-353-3329, www.bu.edu/art/

COLOR:CODED There’s color theory, about how colors interact with one another and affect the eye, and then there are social constructs around color, often having to do with race and gender. These artists use the first to explore the second. Through Oct. 21. New Art Center, 61 Washington Park, Newtonville. 617-964-3424, www.newartcenter.org

GOHAR DASHTI: PHOTOS 2017 The Iranian photographer presents three bodies of work dealing with themes of home, war, and nature – or fragility, threat, and endurance. They include staged photos, large-scale photograms and cyanotypes, and instant photos. Through Oct. 31. Robert Klein Gallery @ Ars Libri, 500 Harrison Ave. 617-267-7997, www.robertkleingallery.com

CATE McQUAID

Museums

EDDIE MARTINEZ: ANTS AT A PICKNIC Martinez, who had his first solo gallery show in Boston in 2005, has gone on to an international career. This exhibition features drawings, sculptures, and large-scale mandala paintings in his signature jaunty style. Sept. 19-Dec. 17. Davis Museum, Wellesley College, 106 Central St., Wellesley. 781-283-2051, www.wellesley.edu/davismuseum/

NATURE’S MIRROR: REALITY AND SYMBOL IN BELGIAN LANDSCAPES Landscape as a genre was born in the 16th century in what is now Belgium, an outgrowth of the Renaissance’s spirit of inquiry. Artists have used it to celebrate nature, and to signify personal, societal, and spiritual matters. Through Dec. 10. McMullen Museum of Art, Boston College, 2101 Commonwealth Ave. 617-552-8587, www.bc.edu/artmuseum

RESONANT SPACES: SOUND ART AT DARTMOUTH Eight artists bring site-specific sound art to campus. A science center becomes a musical instrument; sounds are buried beneath an amphitheater; and the sun speaks through solar panels. Through Dec. 10. Hood Downtown, Hood Museum of Art, Dartmouth College, 53 Main St., Hanover, N.H. 603-646-2808, hoodmuseum.dartmouth.edu/

CATE McQUAID

EVENTS

Comedy

JEN KIRKMAN The Needham native returns on her “All New Material Girl Tour,” featuring bits you won’t hear on a special until her new one, “Just Keep Livin’?,” comes to Netflix in January. Sept. 20, 7:30 p.m. $26. The Wilbur, 246 Tremont St., Boston. 617-248-9700, www.thewilbur.com

BOSTON COMEDY FESTIVAL There’s a lot going on this weekend, so check the festival’s website for a complete list. Robert Kelly is awarded Comedian of the Year on Saturday and Barry Crimmins gets the Lifetime Achievement Award and plays the Ding Ho reunion earlier that evening. The digital audio from his special “Whatever Threatens You” is out Friday. Sept. 20-24. www.bostoncomedyfestival.com

LENNY CLARKE AND THE WOMEN OF BOSTON COMEDY One of the city’s most celebrated comics hosts two shows featuring Amy Tee, Zenobia, Andrea Henry, Carolyn Plummer, and Christine Hurley. Sept. 23 at 7:15 p.m and 9:30 p.m. $27.50. Giggles Comedy Club, 517 Broadway (Route 1), Saugus. 781-233-9950, www.gigglescomedy.com

NICK A. ZAINO III

Family

TOUCH THE TRUCKS Fire trucks. Construction trucks. Tow trucks. If trucks aren’t your thing, we’re told race cars and police cars will be on site, too, at this New Hampshire fund-raising event for nonprofit End 68 Hours of Hunger, complete with music, games, and face-painting. Sept. 17, 10 a.m.-2 p.m. Cash donation. Epping High School, 21 Academy St., Epping, N.H. www.boston.com/event/touch-the-trucks-6541081

TRAILS & SAILS — CELEBRATE THE GLOUCESTER SEA SERPENT! Loch Ness Monster? Never heard of her. This Saturday is to celebrate the 200th anniversary of the sighting of the far more infamous Gloucester Sea Serpent. Bring your creative side to make your own serpent sculpture and partake in other art activities. Sept. 23, 10 a.m.-noon. Free. Cape Ann Museum, 27 Pleasant St., Gloucester. 978-283-0455. www.capeannmuseum.org/events/trails-sails-celebrate-gloucester-sea-serpent/

32ND ANNUAL OAK STREET FAIR Ever wanted to see a watermelon eating contest? If so, you are in luck, as it’s a specialty of this fun-filled family event in Boston’s Chinatown. Even if not, there will be plenty of games, entertainment, and arts and crafts activities with which to busy yourself at this year’s fair. Sept. 23, 11 a.m.-2 p.m. Free. Josiah Quincy Elementary School Playground, 885 Washington St. 617-603-5240. bcnc.net/upcoming-events/2017/9/23/32nd-annual-oak-street-fair
