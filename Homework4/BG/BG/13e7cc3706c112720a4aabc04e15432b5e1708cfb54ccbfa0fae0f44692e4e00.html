CAMPUS ANGLE

Bettez hits her stride at Tufts

The Boston Globe Tweet Share

By Marvin Pave Globe Correspondent  September 29, 2017

Natalie Bettez’s 2016 cross country season featured finishing second overall at the New England Small College Athletic Conference championships and sixth overall at the regional championships with a personal best of 21:32.86.

She’s picked up this year where she left off. At the 6K Purple Valley Classic in Williamstown on Sept. 23, the junior from Bolton led the Tufts contingent, finishing seventh overall with a 22:43.1 clocking.

“Natalie’s top six in regionals was a great race for her, and she was gutsy in the NESCAC, too,’’ said Tufts head coach Kristen Morwick. “She has another gear to shift into close to the finish, and she is smart at practice during the week so she has something left for Saturday.’’

A biochemistry and Spanish major, Bettez has been a consistent point-scorer for Tufts’ indoor and outdoor track teams at the 3, 5, and 10K distances.

She was also a three-season runner and mid-Wachusett B League All-Star at Nashoba Regional, where she was the Class of 2015 salutatorian. Her father, Erik, was a varsity swimmer at MIT.

Q. You’ve come a long way as a runner since high school. What changed?

A. I had to deal with anemia my junior and senior years, and I was a lot slower compared to my sophomore seasons. It was discouraging. But I stayed patient, started on iron supplements, and was determined to stick with it.

Q. How difficult was the transition to running in college?

A. Freshman year was a big transition for me and a big shock. I had to get used to harder training, especially in the summer. I’ve started to see some of the payback. I’ve exceeded my expectations since freshman year.

Q. How do you put last year’s second-place finish at the NESCAC cross country championships in perspective?

Advertisement

A. It was a really hilly course and a rainy and cold day at Colby, and so it was a matter of going out and testing the runners in front of me. I have always found I do well when I listen to how I feel. I found myself near the front and decided to stick close to the lead pack and passed the runner in front of me with 30 meters to go. That gave me a lot of confidence that has carried over.

Q. What was your path to running cross country in high school?

A. I had tried out for soccer and was cut my freshman year and that turned out to be one of the best things that happened to me. I liked cross country so much that it led to my being a three-season runner.

Q. What do you hope to be doing after college?

A. I was an EMT while in high school and I volunteer for Tufts Emergency Medical Services and my goal is to attend medical school and become a doctor. My mom, Nancy, is an oncology nurse practitioner at Lowell General Hospital and is a role model.

Q. How do you prepare for a meet nutritionally?

A. Making sure I have a good meal the night before like spaghetti and meatballs. The day of the meet I usually have yogurt and toast.

Q. You will be studying in Spain next semester. What will you be doing there?

A. My plan is to join a language immersive program and surround myself in a Spanish-speaking environment so I can hopefully gain fluency and learn more about the culture. I had a great Spanish teacher in high school, Donald Nicole, which led to my passion for the language.

Q. How did it feel to be salutatorian?

A. I had worked very hard academically, so I was proud to finish my high school days with that honor.

Q. You had your first taste of competing in the Division 3 national cross country championships last fall. What did you take from it?

A. I had never been in such a competitive environment and I learned a lot. If we go back this year I’ll know what to expect.

Q. How do you relax?

A. I’d rather read a book, mainly fiction, or do a puzzle instead of watching TV.

MARVIN PAVE
