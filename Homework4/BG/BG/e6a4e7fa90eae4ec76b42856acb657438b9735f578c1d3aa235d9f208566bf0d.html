ST. JOHN PAUL II 30, CATHEDRAL 18

St. John Paul II passes CCL test at Cathedral

By Brandan Blom Globe Correspondent  September 30, 2017

Entering Friday night’s Catholic Central Small clash of unbeatens at Roberts Field in Dorchester, St. John Paul II had lost seven of its previous eight matchups against Cathedral.

The Lions were determined to take a victory back to Hyannis.

Advertisement

With senior quarterback Devan Harrison throwing three touchdown passes, the Lions rolled to a 4-0 start with a 30-18 victory over Cathedral.

“This was a huge test for us,” said Harrison, who threw for 211 yards.

“They were coming in 3-0 and we knew this would be a big one. We’ve had it marked since last year.”

The St. John Paul offense stalled early, but Harrison connected with Tyler Noe on a 27-yard scoring strike and an early 8-0 lead.

Cathedral (3-1) recorded a safety on the Lions’ next drive when a lateral pass from Harrison hit the turf and bounced out of the back of the end zone.

Advertisement

On its next trip, St. John Paul showed more polish. The Lions moved 39 yards on two plays before Harrison hit Mike Satkevich for 35 yards and a 14-2 lead.

“It was tough running against them early on so we were going to the pass,” said first-year St. John Paul II coach Matt Marshall.

“We have some funky formations and we thought we’d throw them off. It’s tough to key on one guy when you’ve got four or five guys you can throw to.”

Cathedral starting clicking toward the end of the first half. Anthony Mejia hooked up with Khalil Blair on a 10-yard pass to cut the deficit to 14-10 at the half.

But Harrison registered his third touchdown pass in the third quarter, connecting with Satkevich for an 11-yard score to extend the margin to 22-10. The Panthers answered with a 40-yard strike to Blair in the fourth quarter to cut the deficit to 22-18.

It was too late. St. John Paul II chewed up the clock with a 10-play drive and Richard Mulcahy cemented the W with a 2-yard rush with just over a minute to play.

“I learned that they’re fighters. I think we learned a lot about ourselves. It’s definitely a stepping-stone,” St. John Paul coach Matt Marshall said.

“When you go 1-7 against this team and know they have had our number for many years — I told the guys this week that it’s time for a change.”
