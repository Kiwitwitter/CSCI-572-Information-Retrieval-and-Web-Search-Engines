EF Education First expands its Cambridge campus again

David L. Ryan/Globe Staff

The circus tent rose on EF Education First’s campus for Monday’s groundbreaking.

By Jon Chesto Globe Staff  October 01, 2017

EF Education First is bringing the big top to Cambridge as it breaks ground for its latest campus expansion — a new 12-story building — on Monday. The company expects to welcome more than 2,500 guests to a circus tent near its existing US headquarters, overlooking the Charles River in the city’s North Point area. The circus theme was inspired by the site’s former use as a staging area for circuses that performed nearby at the old Boston Garden. The company, which offers language classes and overseas educational trips, employs about 1,200 people at the campus but plans to have 1,500 workers there by 2020. State officials have awarded the company $6 million in tax incentives to help subsidize the latest expansion, and MassDevelopment has agreed to issue $25 million worth of bonds to help pay for road and sewer work.
