Patriots add some high-octane back to the offense

By Ben Volin Globe Staff  January 17, 2016

FOXBOROUGH — The Patriots’ 27-20 divisional-round win over the Chiefs felt like taking a trip in a time machine.

The performance Saturday night, and the quotes coming out of the Chiefs’ locker room, looked a lot like they did for the Patriots in September and October, before the team was ravaged by injuries.

“The ball coming out quick was the biggest challenge,” Chiefs pass rusher Tamba Hali said. “The rush wasn’t getting there. When the ball’s coming out within one or two seconds, there’s not much you can do.”

The bye week served the Patriots well, despite an injury scare with Rob Gronkowski’s back, and the band was finally back together Saturday night — Julian Edelman, Gronkowski, Danny Amendola, the starting five offensive linemen, and Tom Brady, the conductor. It was impossible to know what to expect from the Patriots’ offense on Saturday night with so many new faces — it’s one thing to look good in practice, but how would they do in game speed against a tough Chiefs defense?

The Patriots aced their test, and the result was a return to the offensive efficiency and production of the first 10 games, when the Patriots ripped off 10 wins in a row.

“It was a great win,” said Tom Brady (28 of 42, 302 yards, two touchdowns). “Took everything we’ve got. A lot of guys played hard, a lot of guys have been fighting through some stuff.”

Edelman’s return, of course, was the biggest addition. Gronkowski and Amendola played the last several games of the regular season, though they clearly weren’t at 100 percent after dealing with knee injuries. But Edelman had missed the last seven games of the regular season with a broken foot, and his loss was impactful. An offense that scored 33.7 points per game and converted 50 percent of third downs with Edelman scored just 23.1 points per game and converted only 30 percent of third downs without him.

Saturday night’s performance wasn’t perfect, but this was the Patriots’ offense we remember. They scored a touchdown on their first possession to take a lead they would never relinquish, went 6 for 12 on third down (after going 4 of 25 the last two games) and turned three of four red zone opportunities into touchdowns. They didn’t bother trying to establish the run, calling 45 passes against just eight handoffs, instead relying on Edelman to move the chains.

They were able to use tempo and the no-huddle offense, forcing the officials to call off one play because the zebras weren’t ready for the snap. And they pulled off an impressive 98-yard touchdown drive in the second quarter to take a commanding 14-3 lead.

“When you get ‘11’ back, it makes things out there easier — not just a couple players but for everybody man,” said receiver Keshawn Martin, who had two catches for 57 yards. “That guy, he’s been doing it for so long, it makes our offense run a lot smoother. You saw that today.”

The Patriots sputtered to the finish line in the regular season, losing four of their final six with Edelman on the sideline. He has been back at practice for several weeks, but no one really knew how he or his wounded teammates would perform against a stingy Chiefs defense.

Edelman more than met the challenge, playing 52 of 55 snaps (including penalties), getting a game-high 16 targets and catching 10 passes for 100 yards. He had a few too many drops in the first half as he worked off the rust, but there’s no question that Edelman brings a higher octane to the offense.

“I’m super proud of him,” Gronkowski said. “It’s just not easy to come back right in midseason and just have a performance like that. You’ve got to give him credit for all the hard work he put in to be ready and help this team.”

Edelman wasn’t the only one to tough it out for a full game. Gronkowski, who had a back injury flare up on him last week that held him out of two days of practice, played 54 of 55 snaps, and caught seven of eight passes thrown his way for 83 yards and two gargantuan touchdowns. Amendola, who has tweaked a knee injury a couple of times this season, played 43 of 55 snaps, though he only had two catches for 18 yards.

And wouldn’t you know it, the offensive line magically played a lot better, too. Part of that was getting Sebastian Vollmer back from a high ankle sprain suffered three weeks ago, but he was still clearly hobbled. This was still an offensive line that had an inconsistent Marcus Cannon at right tackle, and rookie Shaq Mason playing his first game at right guard.

But Brady didn’t take a sack on Saturday, for only the second time all season. He had a clean pocket, and got the ball out of his hands quickly to Edelman and Gronk. The Chiefs’ pass rushers were banged up — Justin Houston barely played with a knee injury — but to not get sacked in 45 passing plays is impressive no matter how you slice it. And Brady was coming off a sprained ankle suffered two weeks ago, too.

Edelman’s return affected the offense across the board.

“Huge for them, right?” Hali said of getting Edelman back into the offense. “If you can get rid of the ball — Peyton [Manning] does it all the time — if you can get rid of the ball and never get touches on the quarterback it’s 7-on-7. It’s huge.”
