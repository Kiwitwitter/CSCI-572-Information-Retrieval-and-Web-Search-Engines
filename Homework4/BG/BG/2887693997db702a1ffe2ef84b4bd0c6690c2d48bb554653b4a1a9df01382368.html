By Matthew Gilbert Globe Staff  January 27, 2017

SUPERIOR DONUTS A few years ago, it might have been “Superior Muffins,” or even “Superior Croissants.” This new sitcom is based on the play by Tracy Letts, the actor-writer who won a Tony for playing George in “Who’s Afraid of Virginia Woolf?” and penned the Pulitzer Prize-winning “August: Osage County.” It’s about the gruff owner of a small doughnut shop in Chicago (Judd Hirsch) and his go-getter employee (Jermaine Fowler). Feb. 2, 8:30 p.m., CBS

24: LEGACY We should be grateful that Kiefer Sutherland is busy with “Designated Survivor,” so that Fox had to spin off “24” instead of zombifying it all over again. This new iteration of the real-time drama — which will premiere after the Super Bowl — is about a war hero, played by Corey Hawkins, who returns to America and needs help from CTU to prevent a terror attack on US soil. Miranda Otto and Jimmy Smits costar. Also notable: Carlos Bernard, Tony Almeida from “24,” is on the recurring cast list. Feb. 5, 10:30 p.m., Fox

Advertisement

THE DETROITERS Sam and Tim are best friends and wannabe advertising men in this comedy produced by Jason Sudeikis and Lorne Michaels. Can they build an advertising empire making local ads for Detroit businesses? Sam Richardson, so brilliant as Richard Splett on “Veep,” and “Saturday Night Live” writer Tim Robinson are the two stars, and Sudeikis, Keegan-Michael Key, and Malcolm-Jamal Warner are among the guest stars. Feb. 7, 10:30 p.m., Comedy Central

LEGION Based on the Marvel Comics character, this FX drama was created by Noah Hawley, the creator of FX’s “Fargo.” That’s auspicious, as is the casting of Dan Stevens as David Haller, a mutant in a psychiatric hospital who has psychic abilities. Two “Fargo” alums — Jean Smart and Rachel Keller — fill out the cast, which also includes Aubrey Plaza and Bill Irwin. Feb. 8, 10 p.m., FX

DAVID BRENT: LIFE ON THE ROAD If you are a fan of the original British version of “The Office,” this movie needs little introduction. It follows Ricky Gervais’s David Brent, former GM of the Slough branch of Wernham Hogg, as he lives his dream of being a rock star. He’s on the road with bandmates, who, like his employees back in the day, don’t really want to hang out with him. What’s Brent’s music like? “My stuff is new romantic, but modern. A bit Bublé, a bit David Essex,” he says. Feb. 10, Netflix

CRASHING Comedian Pete Holmes, who grew up in Lexington, stars as a comedian named Pete whose wife leaves him for another man — an Italian boxer named Rocco. He works to make a fresh start and dig in to the New York standup world. Judd Apatow executive produces the show, and Sarah Silverman, T.J. Miller, and Artie Lange appear as versions of themselves. Feb. 19, 10:30 p.m., HBO

BIG LITTLE LIES Oscar winners Nicole Kidman and Reese Witherspoon, along with Laura Dern, Shailene Woodley, Alexander Skarsgard, and Adam Scott star in this — oh, who cares with a cast like that. But anyway, the David E. Kelley miniseries is based on the 2014 novel by Liane Moriarty about a group of mothers of first-graders. The series opens with a murder at a gala, but we won’t know the identities of the victim and the perp until the end. Feb. 19, 9 p.m., HBO

Advertisement

THE GOOD FIGHT This could be a bad idea two times over. First, it’s a spinoff of “The Good Wife” focusing on Christine Baranski’s Diane Lockart. To be honest, I was a diehard “Good Wife” fan, but I was ready to let it go when it left the air last year; the stories had been told. Second, after a premiere on CBS, the series will only be available on the pay streaming service CBS All Access. Will viewers be willing to pay to watch? I hope I’m surprised and it all works out. The cast includes Cush Jumbo, Rose Leslie, and Delroy Lindo. Feb. 19, CBS All Access

TRIAL AND ERROR Just what we need: another murder-mystery series. But wait, this is a mockumentary comedy that makes fun of all the familiar real-crime tropes. So there’s hope. Nicholas D’Agosto, from “Masters of Sex” and “Gotham,” plays a New York lawyer who heads to a small Southern town to defend the eccentric John Lithgow, who has been accused of murdering his wife. March 7, 9:30 p.m., NBC

HARLOTS This eight-part drama is set in 1760s London, where an enterprising mother (the great Samantha Morton) runs a brothel. She goes up against a rival madam, while balancing her family responsibilities. Jessica Brown Findlay, who was Lady Sybil on “Downton Abbey,” costars as the eldest daughter. March 29, Hulu MATTHEW GILBERT
