Joe Biden tweets support for Julia Louis-Dreyfus after cancer disclosure

Colleen Hayes/ Associated Press

Julia Louis-Dreyfus in "Veep."

By Sarah Betancourt Globe Correspondent  September 29, 2017

Former vice president Joe Biden tweeted his support to his television counterpart Julia Louis-Dreyfus after she announced her cancer diagnosis on Twitter.

He said, “We Veeps stick together. Jill and I, and all of the Bidens, are with you, Julia.”

Advertisement

Biden’s own son Joseph “Beau” Biden died in 2015 after a long battle with brain cancer. He has since worked with various cancer research institutions in hopes of finding a cure.

The “Veep” star wrote that one in eight women gets breast cancer.

“The good news is that I have the most glorious group of supportive and caring family and friends, and fantastic insurance through my union. The bad news is that not all women are so lucky, so let’s fight all cancers and make universal health care a reality,” she tweeted.

Louis-Dreyfus on Sept. 17 won her sixth Emmy award for her portrayal of Selina Meyer.

The Biden family founded the Beau Biden Foundation for the Protection of Children in 2015 in honor of Biden’s son, who once served as attorney general of Delaware.
