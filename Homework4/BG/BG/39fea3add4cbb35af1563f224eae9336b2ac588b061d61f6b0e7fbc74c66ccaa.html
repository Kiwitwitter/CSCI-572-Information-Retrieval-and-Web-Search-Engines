OHIO 58, UMASS 50

Ohio outlasts winless UMass, 58-50, in shootout

J. Anthony Roberts/AP

UMass receiver Brennon Dingle hauls in the first of his two TD catches against Ohio.

By Nick Ironside Globe Correspondent  September 30, 2017

AMHERST — The UMass offense rolled Saturday, posting 50 points and 550 yards of offense on Ohio.

But the defense couldn’t produce a stop when needed, and the winless Minutemen went on to a 58-50 defeat to the Bobcats in front of 7,696 at McGuirk Stadium.

Advertisement

“We needed one play on defense,” UMass coach Mark Whipple said.

The Minutemen (0-6) didn’t lack opportunities.

In the third quarter, an errant snap that flew over the head of Bobcats quarterback Nathan Rourke tumbled into the end zone with multiple Minutemen defenders in pursuit. But Ohio’s Dorian Brown grabbed the ball instead, resulting in 2 points for the Minutemen rather than 6.

Later, Ohio faced a third-and-5 from the UMass 42 with less than two minutes remaining, and a stop could have given the Minutemen offense a chance to tie the game. But Brown burst through the UMass defense on a 42-yard touchdown run that extended the Bobcats’ lead to 58-43.

Ohio rushed for 165 yards on 12 attempts in the fourth quarter and totaled 292 yards on the ground, figures that devastated the Minutemen.

“We knew we had to stop the run and we didn’t do a good job there,” said Whipple. “That’s what their M.O. is, and I thought Rourke played really well.”

Two Minutemen cornerbacks, Isaiah Rodgers (upper body) and Bakhari Goodson (lower body), suffered injuries Saturday. UMass was also without starting cornerback Lee Moses (undisclosed injury).

Ohio converted on 5 of 10 third-down attempts and didn’t allow the Minutemen defense to seize momentum.

“The last three games we did a good job of getting three-and-outs, but we didn’t do that today,” said linebacker Steve Casali, who had a team-best nine tackles. “They excelled off that. They had a good offense and we just didn’t play well.”

The Ohio quarterback’s counterpart kept the game close at the end.

UMass’s Andrew Ford threw for a career-best 390 yards and five touchdowns on 32-of-54 passing, with 201 yards and three touchdowns coming in the fourth quarter. Solid play from the offensive line, a group that allowed 30 sacks in the first five games, afforded Ford more time to find his favorite target, tight end Adam Breneman (12 catches, 131 yards, 2 TDs).

Marquis Young’s 81-yard kickoff return midway through the second quarter set the Minutemen up at Ohio’s 6. From there, Ford found Breneman, the fifth-year senior tight end, across the middle for his first touchdown of the season to pull the Minutemen within 24-20.

Five minutes later, Ford led the Minutemen on a go-ahead 71-yard drive that culminated in a 30-yard touchdown pass to wideout Brennon Dingle — the first of his career. The two sides went into halftime tied at 27.

UMass’s Bilal Ally (13 carries, 57 yards) breaks off a first-half run.

The Bobcats struck quickly after the break. On the first play of the second half, Rourke connected with Brendan Cope for a 48-yard gain. Running back A.J. Ouellette (12 carries, 125 yards) capped the six-play drive with a 5-yard rushing touchdown. Ohio struck again after a Minutemen three-and-out — this time, Rourke found Ouellette on a 12-yard pass for a 41-27 lead.

Ohio (4-1) scored touchdowns on three of its first four drives in the second half. Meanwhile, the Minutemen offense carved out drives deep into Ohio territory twice in the third quarter, but they resulted in turnovers on downs at the Bobcats’ 20 and 25.

“One [drive] was a 45-yarder, field goal, a little bit into the wind,” said Whipple. “I thought about it, but I felt like we had a good play. Just the feeling on the sideline was that was a second-guess a little bit, but I thought we had a good play.”

Those missed opportunities allowed Ohio to create separation that UMass attempted to overcome with a late rally.

Trailing, 51-35, Ford threw a 7-yard touchdown to Breneman with 3:23 remaining and connected with Andy Isabella on the 2-point conversion to pull within 8. But Brown scored on the 42-yard run to extend Ohio’s lead to 15.

Ford connected with Dingle for a 16-yard touchdown with 11 seconds remaining. But time ran out for the Minutemen, who now have lost at least six games in each of the past seven seasons.

The Minutemen have two weeks off before they take on Georgia Southern on Oct. 21.

“We just needed one of those [game-changing plays],” said Whipple. “We’re right there and our guys are fighting.”
