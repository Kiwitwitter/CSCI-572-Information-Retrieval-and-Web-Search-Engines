Julian Edelman shakes off the rust

The Boston Globe Tweet Share

By Jim McBride Globe Staff  January 17, 2016

FOXBOROUGH — Long before he lined up for the first snap of the game, Julian Edelman let the 66,829 at Gillette Stadium know it was all systems go.

Or “full throttle,” as he likes to put it.

Advertisement

The 5-foot-10-inch, 200-pound sparkplug came flying out of the tunnel as he normally does (well, he is the flying squirrel) and raced the length of the field and into the end zone as he pumped up the crowd – and himself.

“It was good,’’ said Edelman. “It’s those little things — feeling the energy of the stadium and the fans. It was an exciting moment and I visually prepared for that moment for a long time. It’s those little things that you look forward to.’’

Suffice to say, the Patriots were looking forward to big things from their dynamo.

Edelman not only returned to action in Saturday night’s 27-20 AFC divisional-round win over the white-hot Kansas City Chiefs, he came back with a vengeance.

Playing for the first time since breaking his foot against the Giants on Nov. 15, Edelman was everything the Patriots hoped he would be.

Advertisement

And then some.

Edelman was targeted 16 times, made 10 receptions, and collected 100 yards — all game highs as the Patriots punched their ticket to a fifth straight AFC Championship game next Sunday. The 10 catches tied his career postseason high and it was his second 100-yard playoff effort — both done in last year’s Super Bowl win over Seattle.

He also added an end-around run for an 11-yard gain.

Though he was spotted heading into the X-ray room following the win, Edelman answered “no” when asked if he received an X-ray.

He promised in the days leading up to his return that he’d be going full tilt from the get-go and that was certainly the case as he played 52 of 55 snaps. Though he hadn’t played since Week 10 — Edelman did return to practice on Dec. 11.

“Any time you go out with your teammates and get a win — in the divisional round against a really good football team it feels good,’’ said Edelman, who cracked that he almost “forgot what it felt like after a game” as he gingerly got changed. “You get bumps and bruises but it’s part of the game but it felt good to get out there.’’

Tom Brady on Julian Edelman’s return

Edelman said he wasn’t limited in any way by his foot and he sure proved it on the game’s first drive — and 11-play, 80-yard touchdown march. He played every snap, catching two passes for 24 yards as he flashed his trademark quickness, cutting, and toughness. He caught an 11-yarder on the third play and snagged a 13-yarder on the next one. Both catches drew thunderous applause. He did miss two passes — and those are the plays that stuck with him.

“I dropped some balls that I shouldn’t have — trying to do stuff. Trying to make a move before I caught it and I just had to slow down and get back to the fundamentals and I’m going to be working on those a lot this week,’’ said Edelman. “That was just me trying to be too fast.’’

Edelman said he’s looking forward to getting right back at it and being even sharper next week.

“You can always play better. I’m personally going to have to do a lot more,’’ he said.

Edelman credited the coaching and support staff with helping him not miss a beat despite not being able to be in games.

“We do a good job of making practices tough here,’’ he said. “I knew what to expect, I knew it was going to be tough. I knew it was going to be a battle and it was it good to go out and get a team win.’’
