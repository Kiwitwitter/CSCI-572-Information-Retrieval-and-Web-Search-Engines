An early look at Puro Ceviche Bar, serving citrusy seafood on Newbury Street

Jim Davis/Globe Staff

By Devra First Globe Staff  August 04, 2017

Where to Puro Ceviche Bar, a Back Bay spot from the team behind South End Greek restaurant Kava Neo-Taverna.

What for Many versions of ceviche, the citrus-marinated raw seafood dish, in a cute and unpretentious Newbury Street setting.

Advertisement

The scene On a sunny evening, the patio is filled with post-work diners and couples with babies and dogs. Staffers wearing concert T-shirts (Pink Floyd, Rolling Stones) offer a friendly welcome as you descend into the restaurant, narrow and loud, with a solid soundtrack of ’80s hits: “Big in Japan,” “Don’t You (Forget About Me),” “Heaven Is a Place on Earth.” On the walls are murals of women’s faces in black paint on white brick, and angsty graffiti like “I write because nobody listens.” There are oysters on ice in the window, candles burning on the bar. Someone’s ceviche is ready: The cook dings a bell and flashes the customer a smile.

What you’re eating Ceviche might be made with red snapper, sweet potato, and leche de tigre or sea bass with coconut milk, mint, cilantro, and chiles. There are other variations on crudo and carpaccio, plus selections such as grilled octopus, short rib empanadas, the paella-esque arroz con mariscos, and hearts of palm salad.

Care for a drink? You’ll find Pisco sours and other cocktails, along with a standard list of microbrews and an unexpected, international selection of wines from France, Spain, Greece, Slovenia, and beyond.

Overheard Talk of micheladas, meeting cute, and the merits of various diets. “It’s like a Bloody Mary, but with beer,” a man explains to the barmates who are curious about his drink. “The menu wasn’t gay, you know,” a woman whispers to her friend. “Whatever that means,” the friend replies, with a roll of her eyes. “He had this vegetable dish he could not stop talking about,” someone is explaining to the bartender. “Does it ring a bell?” “Every restaurant now sounds like my junior high school dance,” one friend says to another. “Vanity! And security!” they sing along with the soundtrack.

264 Newbury St., Back Bay, Boston, 617-266-0707, www.puro
cevichebar.com
