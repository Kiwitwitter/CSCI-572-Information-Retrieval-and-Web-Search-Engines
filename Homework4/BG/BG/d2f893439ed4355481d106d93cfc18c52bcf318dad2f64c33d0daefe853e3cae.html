76ers reward oft-injured Joel Embiid with maximum extension

Abbie Parr/Getty Images

The 76ers are banking that injuries are behind Joel Embiid after agreeing to an eight-year extension with the center.

FROM STAFF AND WIRE REPORTS  October 10, 2017

A person familiar with the situation told The Associated Press that the Philadelphia 76ers and Joel Embiid have agreed on a maximum contract extension. The person, speaking on condition of anonymity Monday because the contract has not been officially announced, said the sides agreed to a five-year, $148 million extension that cound be worth even more if the 7-foot center reaches certain incentives. Embiid’s career has been riddled with injuries dating to his college career at Kansas and all three seasons with the Sixers. The No. 3 overall pick in the 2014 draft didn’t make his debut until last season, but played only 31 games before having surgery in March to repair a torn meniscus in his knee. He averaged 20.2 points and 7.8 rebounds. He was only recently cleared to participate in 5-on-5 drills but the Sixers must feel confident enough in his health to offer him the max deal. With his social media savvy and dominant offensive game, Embiid has become an instant fan favorite in Philadelphia. But his popularity hasn’t matched his production. The Sixers slapped him with minutes restrictions in his rookie season, even when he was healthy, and also held him out for at least one game in back-to-back sets. Embiid had 32 points and seven rebounds in a Jan. 27 game against Houston and has not played or practiced since his he was hurt. The Sixers are counting on Embiid and No. 1 overall picks Ben Simmons and Markelle Fultz to carry the team from the bottom of the league into an Eastern Conference playoff team.

SOCCER

Iceland shuts out Kosovo to earn its first World Cup spot

Iceland qualified for the World Cup for the first time after beating Kosovo, 2-0, at Reykjavik on Monday. Gylfi Sigurdsson put the hosts ahead in in the first half and Johann Gudmundsson added an insurance goal in the second. Iceland topped Europe Group I and advanced automatically to the finals in Russia next year. Iceland is the smallest nation in terms of population — 330,000 — to make the World Cup. Iceland won the group by 2 points over star-studded Croatia, which beat host Ukraine, 2-0 . . . Aleksandar Prijovic’s 74th-minute goal helped Serbia qualify for the World Cup for the second time since becoming an independent nation in 2006 with a 1-0 win over visiting Georgia in Belgrade. Serbia won Group D by 2 points over Ireland, which got a goal from James McClean in a 1-0 win at Wales to seal second place and a spot in the playoffs for one of the four non-automatic European World Cup berths . . . Italy scraped past host Albania, 1-0, on Antonio Candreva's 73rd-minute strike to ensure itself of top-seed status in the eight-team playoff. The Italians finished 5 points behind Spain in Group G after the Russia-bound Spaniards won in Israel, 1-0, thanks to a long-range strike by Asier Illarramendi in the second half . . . The United States won its second straight game at the Under-17 World Cup, beating Ghana, 1-0, on Ayo Akinola's goal in the 75th minute at New Delhi, India. The Americans, who lead Group D with 4 points, followed by Colombia and Ghana with 3 each, and India with 0, complete group play Thursday against Colombia at Navi Mumbai.

Officials expect Serena’s return for Australian Open

Australian Open officials are hopeful Serena Williams will make her competitive comeback to tennis in January to defend her title in Melbourne. Tournament director Craig Tiley said Williams was planning on playing the Australian Open, which starts Jan. 15. Williams was pregnant last January when she beat her sister, Venus, in the final. The tournament’s overall prize pool will be $42.7 million. The men’s and women’s singles champs will earn about $3.1 million, an increase of 20 percent . . . 16th seed Juan Martin del Potro reached the second round at the Shanghai Masters, beating qualifier Nikoloz Basilashvili, 6-2, 3-6, 6-4, on Monday. American qualifier Frances Tiafoe beat Benoit Paire, 6-4, 6-4, to advance to the second round, where he will play either 10th-seeded American Sam Querrey or Yuichi Sugita . . . Minnesota left wing Zach Parise returned to practice after missing the Wild’s first two games because of an unspecified injury. Monday, however, he said the injury was not to his back but declined to be specific. There is no timetable for his return. The Wild are off until Thursday at Chicago . . .
