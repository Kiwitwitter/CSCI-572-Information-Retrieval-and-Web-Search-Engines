2017 Preps All-Scholastics

Globe Staff  June 30, 2017

ISL

Owen Drometer, Thayer, track

The ISL and New England champion in the 800 and 1,500 meters, the sophomore from Medfield was the league MVP. He also anchored the ISL champion 4x400 relay squad (3:36.5) with a 51.1 finish. Drometer also plays soccer.

Harris Foulkes, Roxbury Latin, tennis

The Independent School League MVP, the junior from Belmont was 29-1 at first singles for the unbeaten Foxes, and 11-1 at first doubles. A three-time league all-star, he will play regional and national tourneys this summer.

Hailey Johnson, Governor’s Academy, softball

Advertisement

The Amesbury resident was a dual threat. The senior batted .500 with three home runs and 24 RBIs while going undefeated in the circle and posting a 0.80 ERA. Johnson, the Independent School League MVP, will attend Suffolk.

Cassidy Kearney, Thayer, track

The ISL MVP this spring and last fall (cross-country), the junior from Rockland set league meet records in the 800 and 1,500, along with running a leg on the record-setting 4x400 squad. She qualified for nationals in the 800 and mile.

Jules Kennedy, BB&N, lacrosse

The senior captain from Winthrop leaned on her lightning quickness to make 132 saves and earn Independent School League MVP honors. Also a ISL hockey all-star, she will play both sports at Hobart and William Smith next year.

John Minicus, Milton Academy, track

The junior from Hingham was the NEPSTA D1 champion in the long jump, triple jump, and javelin, as well as the ISL field MVP. In eight meets, he registered 20 first-place finishes, including a school-record long jump of 22-10¼.

Amanda Ofulue, Milton Academy, track

In her first season in the US, the sophomore from Kenya was the NEPSAC Division 1 champ in the discus (123-8) and set the school record in the shot put (37-9). Ofulue was the Independent School League MVP in field events.

Semi Oloko, Milton Academy, tennis

Advertisement

A straight-A student headed to Brown in the fall, the Canton resident was the Independent School League MVP on the court this spring. The senior was undefeated playing out of the No. 1 singles position for the Mustangs.

John Piatelli, St. Sebastian’s, lacrosse

The Independent School League MVP netted 60 goals with 72 assists to propel the Arrows to the league title. An All-American pick who also suits up for the Warrior Elite club squad, the Wrentham resident will play at Cornell.

Jack Roberts, Nobles, golf

The Wellesley resident received the Miller Medal as the most distinguished student-athlete in his class at Nobles. With a 15-1 record in match play (59-9-6 career), he repeated as ISL MVP. Roberts will play soccer and golf at Princeton.

Matt Tabor, Milton Academy, baseball

The two-time ISL MVP was the state’s Gatorade Player of the Year, capping his career with a 0.60 ERA and 75 strikeouts. A 6-foot-1 righthander from Westford drafted 82nd overall by the Diamondbacks, he has committed to Elon.

EIL

Duru Cosar, Bancroft, tennis

A three-time Eastern Independent All-Star and this year’s MVP, the junior from Shrewsbury compiled a 9-0 mark at No. 1 singles. The high honors student plays USTA tourneys, is a pianist, and a member of the yearbook club.

Kaitlin Kolb, Winsor, track

The Wellesley teen repeated as the EIL MVP, as an eighth grader. She lowered her school record in the 100-meter hurdles, and won 100- and 300-hurdles at the league meet. She was second in the 300 in the D2 NEPSTA meet.

Delia Levine, Newton Country Day, softball

The Eastern Independent League MVP, the senior hit .477 with 14 runs, 21 RBIs, and 7 steals for the Falcons (10-3). She also compiled a 9-2 record, striking out 95 in 73 innings. The West Roxbury resident will study nursing at Penn.

Hannah Marino, Pingree, lacrosse

The senior from Peabody was stellar in goal, propelling Pingree (13-5) to an 8-0 mark in the EIL. The league MVP, Marino registered a 58.67 save percentage. Also the girls’ volleyball captain, she is headed to Colby.

Raimundo Riojas, Portsmouth Abbey, tennis

The three-time Eastern Independent League MVP racked up his third straight undefeated season (15-0) for the Ravens, who won their first NEPSCAC Class C title this spring. The senior from Guatemala will attend Notre Dame.

Ryan Sullivan, Berwick, baseball

The Eastern Independent League MVP, the junior from Amesbury hit .339 with eight RBIs while compiling a 4-1 record with a 1.29 ERA and two saves. He allowed six earned runs. Sullivan also plays for the Seacoast United Pirates.

Sam Welsh, Concord Academy, track

What a debut for the 6-foot-4, 230-pound junior from Lunenburg. He set the EIL/NEPSAC record in the discus (191 feet, 1 inch), was the EIL MVP, and also established school marks in the shot put and 4x100 relay.

Conor White, Pingree, lacrosse

The Melrose resident stopped 60 percent of the shots he faced this year (75 percent vs. EIL foes). He yielded no goals in the NEPSAC Small School final. A three-time EIL defensive MVP, the two-time All-American will play at Hobart.

IGC

Jayme Callum, Gann Academy, softball

A two-time Independent Girls Conference MVP, the catcher from Peabody batted .602 with five homers and 28 RBIs. Also the league volleyball MVP last fall, the two-sport, two-year captain will play both sports at Ursinus.

Isabel Buckley, Montrose, lacrosse

The junior attack from Cohasset registered 47 goals and 23 assists to earn MVP honors for the 11-0 Independent Girls Conference champions. The class president and member of the National Honor Society, the captain-elect is a two-league all-star with 114 career goals.

Zoe Weiss, Gann Academy, tennis

The senior from Newton was the Independent Girls Conference MVP with an 8-0 mark at first singles. Also a NEPSAC and IGC volleyball all-star last fall and the editor-in-chief of the satirical school paper, Weiss will attend Brown.

MBIL

Mike Ballin, Waring, lacrosse

Playing half the season with a broken hand, the senior attack from Gloucester tallied 34 goals and 31 assists to earn co-MVP honors in the MBIL. A two-time league all-star with 79 career goals and 89 assists, he will attend Colby.

Ryan Donovan, Chapel Hill, ultimate frisbee

With 200 points this spring, the Peabody resident earned the MBIL’s top honor. Donovan was an MBIL All-League player the last three years and won the Captain’s Award as a freshman. He’ll play for the club team at Bryant.

Elan Ganz, Gann Academy, lacrosse

Averaging 46 minutes per game as a long stick midfielder, the junior from Newton was the MBIL co-MVP and a NEPSAC/Eastern All-Region pick. Coach Chris Gelinas calls him a physical and vocal leader. He also runs cross-country.

Jonathan Hodge, Boston Trinity, baseball

The junior catcher from Waltham hit .478 with eight RBIs and eight stolen bases to power Boston Trinity to the MBIL championship game against Brimmer & May. Hodge is a .500 career hitter. He also plays soccer and basketball.

Sam Rueter, Cambridge School of Weston, tennis

A two-year captain, the senior from Winchester was the MVP in the MBIL after compiling a 12-1 record at first and second singles (34-5 in four seasons, plus 11-1 in doubles). Awarded a presidential scholarship with a 3.99 GPA, Rueter will play soccer at Grinnell.

NEPSAC

Chris Lanzilli, Dexter, baseball

In Dexter’s run to an 18-0 season and the NEPSAC Small title, the senior infielder/outfielder from Stoneham was a force. A team captain, he hit .595 for Dan Donato’s club with eight homers. He will play at Wake Forest.

Emily Picard, New Hampton, softball

In powering New Hampton to a 15-2 mark and its second straight Lakes Region title, the catcher/infielder from Berlin, N.H., hit .492 with six homers and 30 RBIs. Her two-out, two-run double was clutch in the clincher vs. Tilton.
