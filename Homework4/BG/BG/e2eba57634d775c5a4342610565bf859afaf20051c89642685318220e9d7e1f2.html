FRIDAY’S SCHOOL FOOTBALL ROUNDUP

King Philip gets off to impressive start

  • Twitter

  • LinkedIn

The Boston Globe Tweet Share

By Henry Brechter Globe Correspondent  September 09, 2017

WRENTHAM — Reigning Division 1A champion King Philip made an impressive opening statement on Friday night in a 24-14 Hockmock League victory over North Attleboro.

Senior quarterback Brendan Lydon threw for 143 yards and two touchdowns, senior back Shane Frommer piled up 101 yards and a touchdown on 16 carries, and the Warriors defense dominated, registering five sacks and picking off a pair of passes.

Advertisement

“We made a lot of mistakes,” said North Attleboro coach Don Johnson. “We didn’t do a good job protecting [quarterback Chad Peterson] and giving him the opportunity to make plays.”

The first interception set up a 25-yard field goal for a 3-0 lead. But North Attleboro senior Sam Bullock recovered a fumble at the KP 25-yard line. The turnover set up a 21-yard touchdown from Peterson to senior tight end Nolan Buckley with under nine minutes left in the first half.

The Rocketeers’ 7-3 lead was short-lived — with 6:30 to go in the second quarter, Lydon found junior wideout Aidan Bender down the seam for a 71-yard touchdown strike.

Another Lydon touchdown pass to junior receiver David Morganelli gave the Warriors a 17-7 lead going into halftime.

In the fourth quarter, Peterson piloted a 17-play, 79-yard touchdown drive, capped by a 27-yard toss to senior Mario Bresko, cutting the deficit to 17-14 with 8:03 left.

Advertisement

A King Philip punt with 6:30 left to play gave the Rocketeers a chance, but Peterson was picked off on a deep pass at the 2 by KP’s Luke D’Amico.

With a minute left, Frommer exploded through the defense for a 58-yard touchdown run to put the score out of reach, 24-14.

King Philip coach Brian Lee was pleased with his team’s ability to preserve the lead.

“We tell the kids, ‘You don’t think North will come over here and fight?’ That’s what they do all the time, but we just hung on. Sometimes that’s all you have to do.”

BC High 35, Andover 7 — One year after rushing for a school-record 385 yards and four touchdowns against Andover, senior Danny Abraham churned out 262 yards and another four TDs as the visiting Eagles rolled to the nonleague win. The Harvard commit broke off a 68-yard scoring run on BC High’s first play from scrimmage.

St. John’s (Shrewsbury) 33, Catholic Memorial 3 — Eamonn Dennis took the game’s opening kick 96 yards for a score and Steven Bucciaglia hooked up with Coleman Drugotch on a 34-yard catch-and-run a minute later as the visiting Pioneers spoiled the head coaching debut of former Patriot Brent Williams at CM.

Holliston 24, Millis 7 — In his first career start, senior Ryan Benco fired two touchdown passes and rushed 53 yards as the visiting Panthers pounced on the defending Division 4A Super Bowl champions and Tri-Valley League rival in the teams’ 63rd meeting.

Marshfield 44, Cranston West (R.I.) 8 — The Rams exploded for 44 points against Cranston West. Senior Danny Hickey caught a touchdown and returned an offensive fumble for a score. Brothers Jackson and Casey Phinney went wild for Marshfield, as Casey rattled off three touchdown runs, two of which were over 50 yards, and Jackson threw for a score in the third quarter.

Bay State

Natick 17, Needham 0 — A trio of seniors delivered all the points for the Redmen as Christian D'Antonio and Malik Williams turned in a pair of touchdown runs.

Commonwealth

Lynn Tech 34, Chelsea 0 — Senior Steffan Gravely led the Tigers with 114 rushing yards on 10 carries and he added 73 receiving yards on two receptions. Senior captain Keoni Gaskin had 96 yards on 11 carries and a fumble recovery.

Boston City

East Boston 14, New Mission 6 — Senior captain Brandon Ortega accounted for all 14 points for the Jets, including a go-ahead 54-yard run in the fourth quarter.

O'Bryant 28, Boston English 0 — Amarj Alexandre led the Tigers with two touchdowns on 60 yards.

Cape Ann

Lynnfield 32, Newburyport 0 — Anthony Murphy scored on two short runs and Matt Mortillete had scoring tosses of 21 and 49 yards.

Merrimack Valley

Lowell 32, Lawrence 17 — The Red Raiders stormed back to win behind running back Anthony Doung and Brandon Tighe (two touchdowns apiece).

Middlesex

Reading 48, Wakefield 14 — Senior quarterback Matt Panacopoulos ran for three touchdowns and passed for a fourth as the host Rockets cruised. Reading returned two kickoffs for scores, one by John DiMare, and one by Jack Geiger.

South Coast

Old Rochester 41, Wareham 40 — Senior Harry Smith broke the Bulldogs all-time scoring record, running for five touchdowns and adding two 2-point conversions. However it was Will Garcia who notched Old Rochester’s final touchdown, setting the table for a go-ahead two-point rush from Smith with under three minutes remaining. A stop on fourth-and-long near midfield sealed the opener for the Bulldogs. Wareham senior back Issac Nascimento set his own school record, running for two touchdowns and adding a third through the air. Senior running back Seth Andrade added three touchdowns for the Vikings.

Fairhaven 28, Greater New Bedford 6 — The ground-and-pound Blue Devils did just that with seniors Jake Cadieux, Chance Negalha and junior Zac Guillotte helping tally 277 total rushing yards. Cadieux finished with a pair of touchdowns and two 2-point conversions.

South Shore

Rockland 3, Cohasset 0 — Bulldogs kicker Matt Dalton broke a scoreless tie with a 20-yard, field goal with 14 seconds to play. Bulldogs coach Nick Liquori earned his first win after serving as the program’s assistant for 11 years.
Tri-Valley

Medfield 34, Dedham 14 — Mitchell Gonser and Stephen Williams connected on three consecutive touchdowns as Medfield rode a strong second half to victory.

Nonleague

Minuteman 22, Matignon 20— The Mustangs snapped a two-year, 11-game losing streak behind junior Ngor Manyang (39 carries, 112 yards, three touchdowns). Manyang caught a 57-yard strike from Danny Johanson with under two minutes remaining to put Minuteman ahead for good.

Hopkinton 18, Wayland 14— Receiver Will Abbott (13 catches, 146 yards, TD) threw the game-winning touchdown to Luke D'aloia on a trick play. Hopkinton quarterback Ryan Kelleher completed 25 of 37 passes for 288 yards and 2 touchdowns with no interceptions. Senior captain Mike Ianelli came through with two interceptions in the fourth quarter.

Dracut 39, Westford 7 — Senior Nikhaysh Carter (3 touchdowns) returned a kick 65 yards to open scoring and Kuzi Mvududu scurried 71-yards to put the Middies on top for good in the second quarter.

North Reading 31, Brighton 8— Kyle Bythrow used his legs and his arm to lead the Hornets (1-0) past host Brighton. The senior QB for 187 yards and passed for 93 more, throwing three touchdown passes and running in a pair of conversions.

Mansfield 34, Walpole 13— Senior Danny Degirolamo caught a touchdown pass and ran for another, propelling the Hornets.

Marblehead 16, Triton 9 — Quarterback Dan Doherty tossed two touchdown passes for the Magicians.

Hull 21, Blue Hills 6 — Senior Christian Olivieri hauled in a 21-yard touchdown reception in the first quarter and added a 20-yard TD run in the fourth to power the visiting Pirates.

Waltham 21, Newton North 18 — Senior Nick Caliri rushed for 97 yards and two touchdowns to lead the Hawks.

Melrose 32, Masconomet 6 — Senior Charlie Stanton threw a touchdown pass and ran for another, propelling the Red Raiders.

Pembroke 33, Westwood 14 — Junior Jack Kelley was 8-of-13 passing for 113 yards and two touchdowns.

Scituate 34, Milford 7 — Andrew Blanchard ran through the Scarlet Hawks defense, rushing for 201 yards and a pair of touchdowns to lead the host Sailors. Quarterback Aidan Sullivan was 20 of 28 for 182 yards in the air with a pair of touchdowns, while Scituate’s defense forced a fumble with two interceptions.

Taunton 25, New Bedford 15 — Quarterback Noah Leonard had a touchdown pass and PJ Mendes added a pair of touchdowns on the ground for the Tigers.

West Roxbury 52, Pope John 20 — The backfield tandem of Ismael Presume (120 yards, 2 TDs) and Kolawole Omo (75 yards, 2 TDs) tore apart the Tiger D. Omo also had a 46-yard pick-six as the Raiders defense registered three touchdowns.

Ipswich 14, Essex Tech 0 — Ben Yanakakis (1-yard run) and Tommy Herron (37-yard reception) scored for the host Tigers.

Salem 27, Austin Prep 14 — Seniors Vinny Gaskins and Aneudy Espinal had two touchdowns each for the Witches. Gaskins rushed for 206 yards on 19 carries and Espinal had 100 yards on six carries.

Stoneham 14, Archbishop Williams 12 — Trey Pignone led the Spartans with 225 rushing yards and two catches for 133 receiving yards.

Carver 27, Martha’s Vineyard 14 — Sawyer Shaw scored from 5 and 15 yards out as the visiting Crusaders built a 27-6 through three quarters. Zach Moreis rushed for 94 yards on nine carries for MV.

Cathedral 28, Latin Academy 12 — Khalil Blair ripped off a 75-yard kickoff return for a score, had 51-yard touchdown run, tossed a 36-yard TD pass and registered two interceptions to propel the Panthers to victory at White Stadium. Khamal Headen delivered the go-ahead score with a 39-yard sprint in the fourth quarter, breaking a 12-12 tie.

Wilmington 27, Shawsheen 14 — John Kenney led the Wildcats with 77 yards and two scores on 15 carries.

Lincoln-Sudbury 24, Dighton-Rehoboth 7 — In his first career start, quarterback Braden O'Connell rushed for 130 yards on 18 carries, including three touchdowns.

Lowell Catholic 13, Greater Lowell 6 — Tyler Longo provided both touchdowns for the Crusaders. He reeled in a 34-yard touchdown pass and returned an interception for the second score.

Methuen 41, Malden 16 — Jordan Perdomo (117 yards on 18 carries, 4 TDs) and Saul Cespedes (21-104, 2 TDs) fueled a dominant ground game.
