ST. MARY’S 48, CARDINAL SPELLMAN 8

Unbeaten St. Mary’s dominates rival Cardinal Spellman

Robert E. Klein for the Globe

St. Mary's quarterback Calvin Johnson (166 rushing yards) looks for running room against Cardinal Spellman defender Jamauri James.

By Nate Weitzer Globe Correspondent  September 30, 2017

BROCKTON — On the very first play from scrimmage in a pivotal conference clash at Cardinal Spellman, St. Mary’s grabbed the inside track towards another Catholic Central League title.

Marlon Scott took a sweep 75 yards for a touchdown to put the Spartans up, 6-0, just 21 seconds into the game and St. Mary’s never looked back on the way to a 48-8 victory at Potvin Field on Friday evening.

Advertisement

“The kids came out ready to go and it was a great team effort,” said St. Mary’s coach Matt Durgin.

“We’ve had a difficult time [at Cardinal Spellman] and we knew coming in here that it would be tough, but I thought our kids were well prepared and played excellent on both sides of the ball.”

Cardinal Spellman (3-1) lost starting quarterback Rob Maguire to an apparent lower leg injury on Scott’s touchdown burst, limiting the Cardinals’ offensive options with sophomore Will McAlpine taking over under center.

On the other side of the ball, St. Mary’s (4-0) had no such issues, as senior quarterback Calvin Johnson rushed for 166 yards and three touchdowns and added a passing touchdown in yet another stellar effort.

The Lynn resident now has 17 all-purpose touchdowns through four games, leading a Spartans team that is averaging the most points per game (50) in all of Eastern Massachusetts and second-most in the state.

Advertisement

“[Johnson] just has so much ability in terms of how he can run, pass, and provide leadership,” praised Durgin. “And then he plays defense and he’s on the kickoff team, so he plays everywhere and he’s just been unbelievable.”

Along with Johnson and Scott, James Brumfield also eclipsed 100 yards rushing. Overall, St. Mary’s averaged 12.6 yards per carry on 35 totes to amass 441 rushing yards in the contest.

“Coach prepares us well in practice and this is what we do,” said Johnson. “We have an uptempo offense that can burn teams and hit them when they’re down and that’s what we keep doing into the fourth quarter.”

Justin Olowu paced the Cardinals with 95 rushing yards and a touchdown.
