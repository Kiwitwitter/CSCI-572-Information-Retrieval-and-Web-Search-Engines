Patrice Bergeron leaves Bruins’ practice early with injury

John Tlumacki/The Boston Globe

Patrice Bergeron did not make it all the way through Tuesday’s practice.

By Kevin Paul Dupont Globe Staff  October 04, 2017

Sign up for Globe sports news alerts.

Amid all the buzz Tuesday surrounding a new season about to dawn for the Bruins, an old reliable face became the focus of some immediate concern.

First-line center Patrice Bergeron did not make it all the way through the one-hour workout at the Warrior Ice Arena in Brighton. The 32-year-old star pivot, who underwent hernia surgery following the completion of the playoffs in April, has a lower-body injury, according to coach Bruce Cassidy.

“We’ll classify it as day to day for now,” said Cassidy, who hopes to start the new season Thursday night with Bergeron anchoring Brad Marchand and rookie Anders Bjork. “I think that’s accurate, and we’ll see where he’s at tomorrow. We’ll have a much better update then. “

When asked if Bergeron could be sidelined for a while, Cassidy added, “I mean, I don’t want to speculate. It’s a lower-body injury, and from there, I guess I’d say I don’t expect it to be long term. I honestly can’t tell you, we’ll see how he is in the morning.”

Bergeron finished fourth in team scoring last season with a line of 21-32—53. One of the league’s most proficient faceoff men for the last decade, he is perhaps the club’s most vital forward, a mainstay both in even-strength and special-teams situations.

“I am, yeah . . . but it’s fine,” said Bergeron, when asked about his injury following the workout. “It’s day to day, so we’ll see.”

The injury, said Bergeron, is not related to his offseason surgery, which was performed to correct a hernia that he sustained in the World Cup in September of 2016. He managed capably with the injury all season, missing but three games, then underwent the knife after the Bruins were eliminated by the Senators in the first round of the playoffs.

Subban to Vegas

Advertisement

Goalie prospect Malcom Subban, who only last week turned in the best Garden performance of his career, was claimed off waivers Tuesday by the Vegas Golden Knights.

Per league rules, Subban, 23, must remain on the Golden Knights varsity roster, be it as a second or third option behind the presumptive No. 1 tender, Marc-Andre Fleury, the former Penguin.

Fleury, 32, had a 12-year run in Pittsburgh and is expected to be the workhorse for the Knights, who have been planning to go with former Avalanche Calvin Pickard as his backup.

“Am I surprised?” said Bruins GM Don Sweeney, the Knights claim becoming official while the GM attended the club’s annual media day in Brighton. “I think Malcolm has taken a step, and I’m not surprised in the situation. Vegas is trying to build.”

With the ebullient Subban headed west, his game still a work in progress, Boston’s AHL affiliate in Providence now is expected to have Zane McIntyre and Dan Vladar (recovering from wrist surgery) splitting the net chores.

“Obviously, Zane had experience last year, had a very good run,” noted Sweeney. If Malcolm does stay [with Vegas], Dan probably will get an opportunity to back up in Providence and get the exposure there at the next level. It’s disappointing. We’ve had a lot of time invested in Malcolm, and we are seeing him grow as a person on and off the ice. It’s a loss for our hockey club.”

A former first-round draft pick (No. 24 in 2012) by then-GM Peter Chiarelli, Subban in four seasons with Providence was slow to make his game NHL ready. In fact, he still must prove capable of being an NHL stopper, though his work in the exhibition season showed some promise.

“I think he looked great, “said Tuukka Rask, Boston’s franchise stopper. “We were joking the other day that he looks like a goalie now with his new pads — he looked bigger and filled the net really well. And you guys know, he’s a great guy, kind of tough to see him go, because he’s a great character guy. We wish him well in the future, because I think he’s going to be a good goalie.”

Krug on IR

The Bruins announced Tuesday night that defenseman Torey Krug and forward Austin Czarnik were placed on injured reserve.

Defenseman Rob O’Gara was shipped to Providence of the AHL.
