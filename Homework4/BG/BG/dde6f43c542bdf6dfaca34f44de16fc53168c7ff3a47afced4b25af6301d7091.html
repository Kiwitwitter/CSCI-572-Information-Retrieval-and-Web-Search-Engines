ISL

Katie Benzan, Nobles, basketball

The Massachusetts Gatorade Player of the Year finished with 2,153 career points and was a four-time ISL and New England champion with the Bulldogs. Benzan was awarded the ISL MVP and will play basketball at Harvard.

Casey Carreau, Thayer, hockey

The ISL Keller MVP as a junior, Carreau had 34 goals and 37 assists this season. In his three varsity seasons, the left wing has 52 goals and 61 assists. The Boston College-commit also plays shortstop for the baseball team.

Gracie Doyle, Nobles, squash

Advertisement

The Bulldogs’ junior co-captain finished second in the NEPSAC championships. Doyle also won the Division 1 NEPSAC girls’ individual title. She is ranked No. 1 in the US High School rankings and is an All-American.

Frederic Gregoire, St. George’s, hockey

The senior forward led the Dragons to a NEPSAC playoff berth. Gregoire finished with 20 goals and 39 assists, and his 2.36 points per game average led all New England Prep schools. He will play at Harvard.

Karim Ibrahim, St. George’s, squash

The senior finished the ISL campaign undefeated and placed second at New Englands. Ibrahim was awarded the ISL MVP and has been playing for the Egyptian national team since he was 14. He will attend St. Lawrence.

Nicola Katz, Nobles, alpine skiing

The three-time All-Scholastic is a four-time Class A All-New England selection and three-time New England Class A event champion. The senior helped lead Nobles to its 13th ISL title in the last 14 years.

Beth Larcom, St. George’s, hockey

The senior goalie was named the Division 2 NEPSAC Player of the Year and helped guide the Dragons to the NEPSAC Division 2 title. Larcom was on the gold-medal winning US under-18 team and will play hockey at Harvard.

Caitrin Lonergan, Nobles, hockey

Advertisement

The two-time ISL MVP led the Bulldogs to a 31-1-1 record and the NEPSAC title. Lonergan tallied 67 points on 30 goals and 37 assists and was named NEPSAC Division 1 Player of the Year. She will play hockey at Boston College.

Ikenna Ndugba, Brooks, basketball

The senior point guard led the Bishops with 13 points, 6.5 assists, and 4 steals a game. He was the ISL Player of the Year and New England Class B Tournament MVP. Ndugba will play basketball at Bryant.

Eliot Pierpont, Middlesex, Alpine skiing

Pierpont is a three-time all-league and all-New England selection in the giant slalom. The senior was the captain of the five-time defending ISL champions. He also plays football and is captain of the lacrosse team.

Peter Tedesco, Belmont Hill, wrestling

The senior only lost twice, finishing with an overall record of 76-2. In ISL competition, Tedesco never lost (41-0). He is a three-time New England Tournament champion and All-American. He will wrestle at Maryland.

EIL

Shandon Brown, Beaver Country Day, basketball

Just a sophomore, Brown not only earned EIL MVP honors but was also named to the All New England Class B team. The guard averaged 16.2 points and 2.7 assists per game while shooting 41 percent from behind the arc.

Alyssa Hulst, Berwick, hockey

Hulst finished her senior year with 25 goals and 27 assists, pushing her over 100 points for the past two seasons. Berwick went 11-7 this winter and won the EIL title. The team captain, Hulst also earned All-Star honors as a junior.

Alexa Jacobs, Winsor, squash

The junior went 8-0 in the EIL and led her team to an 11th place finish at Nationals in Division 1 and sixth place at New Englands. Jacobs is ranked No. 22 in the country in her age group and was the EIL MVP.

Andrew Macdonald, Berwick, swimming

The Bowdoin-bound senior won four gold medals for Berwick this season, taking the 200 and 500 free titles, along with the 200 and 400 free relay titles. He also set EIL records in the 200 and 500 free, and the 200 free relay.

Maddie Mullin, Beaver Country Day, basketball

The EIL Player of the Year and Class C All-Star averaged 25.8 points per game. Mullen had 543 points on the season, including five games over 30. She made 84 3-pointers and was an 85 percent shooter from the foul line.

Maddie Wallis, Dana Hall, swimming

The junior took Swimmer of the Meet honors at the NEPSAC championships after winning the 50 free and 100 free titles. Wallis was the EIL MVP and has been on both the US Junior National and National teams.

Chris Weiss, Portsmouth Abbey, wrestling

The three-time EIL champion and national qualifier finished his career with a 102-16 record. Weiss was 33-3 this season and was awarded his second EIL MVP. He placed second in the New England Prep championships.

GIL

Jamayra Castro, Bradford Christian, basketball

The sophomore guard averaged 12.6 points and 8.7 rebounds per game and was named the Division 1 GIL MVP. Castro helped lead the Sentinels to a NEPSAC Class D title and was selected to the NEPSAC Class D All-Star team.

IGC

Catherine Conroy, Montrose, basketball

The senior guard averaged 18 points and nine assists per game. Conroy was awarded the IGC MVP and was named a NEPSAC All-Star for the third time. She is a 1,000-point scorer and four-time IGC League All-Star.

MBIL

Ben Edry, Gann Academy, basketball

The 5-foot-10-inch guard averaged 19.6 points and 4.3 assists per game. Edry, a junior, was the Mass. Bay Independent League co-MVP and an MBIL All-Star. With a year to go, he has 535 career points.

Michael Gergely, Covenant Christian, basketball

The junior guard and Mass. Bay Independent League co-MVP averaged 12 points, 5.6 rebounds, and 4.7 assists a game for the MBIL champion Cougars. He also plays goalie on the soccer team and ultimate frisbee.
