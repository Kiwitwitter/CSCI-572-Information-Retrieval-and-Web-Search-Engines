TUESDAY’S SCHOOL ROUNDUP

No. 5 Wellesley field hockey, No. 3 Walpole battle to draw

By Ryan Hathaway Globe Correspondent  October 04, 2017

Sign up for Globe sports news alerts.

For the fifth-ranked Wellesley field hockey team, battling third-ranked Walpole to a 1-1 draw at home was an accomplishment fit for celebration.

After falling short in the South sectional quarterfinals last fall in a 1-0 loss to Dennis-Yarmouth, the upstart Raiders (9-0-2) have been proving that they deserve to be on the short list of state title contenders this season.

Advertisement

Wellesley battled the reigning Division 1 champion Porkers (7-0-2) to a draw once already in the season opener (a scoreless tie on September 5) and coach Erin Stickle knew how much the contest meant to her squad the second time around.

“They like competing against them,” Stickle said. “They like competing against the best.”

Just 10 minutes into the game, the Raiders struck first. Junior Brooke Hamilton, Wellesley’s leading scorer, launched a rocket that deflected off the padding of Walpole sophomore goalie Giovanna Anello and found the back corner of the net.

The Porkers countered two minutes later when senior Celia Walsh beat Wellesley senior goalie Natalie Boyle to the far side to even the score with 48 minutes to play.

Both teams matched each other blow for blow for the remainder of the game. Wellesley seemed to create more opportunities to pull away, including a 7-3 advantage in penalty corners, but could not get another ball past Anello.

Walpole missed its best chance to take the win in the game’s final two minutes.

After turning away a penalty corner in their own end, the Porkers stormed downfield looking to break the tie.

Senior Samantha Rodia had a clean shot on the net, but Boyle made a breathtaking kick save for Wellesley to preserve the tie.

“I think I work pretty well under pressure,” said an out-of-breath Boyle of her last-minute heroics.

Stickle said Boyle may have been a little more nervous than she let on.

“Last time we played them, she faced 19 shots,” Stickle said. “She was a little nervous having not faced that many shots since then. I wasn’t nervous, though.

“I told [Boyle] she practices [against] one of the best teams around.”
