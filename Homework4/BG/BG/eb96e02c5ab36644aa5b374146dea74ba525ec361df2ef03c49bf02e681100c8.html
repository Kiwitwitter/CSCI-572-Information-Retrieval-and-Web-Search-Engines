HIGH SCHOLL TRACK

Now on separate paths, Westwood, Milton runners have same goal

Barry Chin/Globe Staff

Sarah Conant, left, and Ashley Walker, center, lead their teammates on the Westwood girls' cross-country team in a run.

By Katherine Fominykh Globe Correspondent  September 29, 2017

Ashley Walker likes to get lost in the woods. Not really during a race, of course, because if she did, she wouldn’t be the reigning runner-up in Division 2 girls’ cross-country. But she says the sport holds a mystique for her that no other can offer.

“Everyone starts off together, but right when the gun goes off, you’re on your own,” she said. “You can either have a great race and push yourself, or just not give it your all.”

Westwood High is looking at the best shot it has ever had. Hardly a flicker on other schools’ radar a few years ago, the program helmed by head coach Jen Teahan legged its way into the top five at the D2 all-state meet last November. They’ve also swept the Tri-Valley League meet for the last two years.

At all-states, Walker held 15th place for most of the race.

“A lot of athletes would get anxious about that, saying ‘I need to move up,’ ” said Teahan. “But Ashley has enough will and understanding of her strengths to say ‘I’ll move up when it needs to happen.’ ”

Walker passed 13 athletes to finish behind only Milton’s Bridget Mitchell .

Her trick to success is one a yoga teacher could get behind: She relaxes.

Before big races like the Ocean State Invitational Sept. 23, where she finished second overall among Massachusetts girls, Walker doesn’t think about where she’s going. She eats oatmeal, a banana, and peanut butter. She lays out her clothes the night before to avoid stressing in the morning. And during the race, most importantly of all, she listens. When she does, she can hear the cheers of her teammates enveloping her.

“I do better in an intense race,” she said. “When there are more people supporting me.”

She tries to teach positivity to the rest of the team at all times.

Following long runs, team members reward themselves with cupcakes, apple-picking trips, and team dinners. They extend their training grounds beyond home turf as well, traveling to places like Castle Island to put in their miles.

“Running is very repetitive,” said Walker, “so it’s nice to add extra, fun things.”

Without it, Westwood’s runners wouldn’t be near where they are today.

“It’s much more of a team-dominated group; it’s not just individual success anymore,” said Teahan. “The whole culture of the program. Everyone’s bought in.”

The goal this fall has suddenly become more attainable. With Milton and other perennial power Tewksbury off to Division 1, the competitive terrain has changed dramatically.

Their likely new competitors include Norton, Lenox, and Mt. Greylock Regional.

“Over the next two invitationals, it will become very clear,” said Teahan.

“They all left,” said junior Sarah Conant of the teams that moved up.

“They’re too scared of us.”

Originally a sprinter, Conant is already a team leader alongside Walker. She’s also one of three Conant triplets running cross-country for Westwood, including her sister Emily and brother Zach, the latter captain of the boys’ team after a breakout season.

“[Sarah] is so hungry,” said Teahan. “[Her] best time [was] 18:45 last year.”

But the Westwood girls’ bid isn’t based solely on the team’s closeness or its standout performers. Teahan, a math teacher, is incorporating data into this year’s preparation.

“We have a lot of nerdy days talking about race performance and effort vs. output, where people need to be,” she said. “We talk about the pack running and the benefits of that versus having people string it out.”

While Westwood wrestles with the chance to rise, Milton, dominant the last two years, will now have to battle Needham and Grace Connolly for that top spot.

As defending D2 All-State champion, Bridget Mitchell will constitute Milton’s best shot. She put up a time of 19:15 in that race, nine seconds ahead of Walker, propelling Milton to its third team title in four years.

“She looked pretty far away to me,” said Walker.

Head coach Tom Shaw sees Mitchell as a beacon for others to follow.

“Having an athlete up front with a big reach is one of the more important things you need to win at the highest level,” he said. “We’ve been very lucky to have someone like her.”

Mitchell wants to defend her position as number one this fall, but knows she’s just a part of a whole.

“You’re only as good as your seventh runner,” she said.

Elise O’Leary, Milton’s seventh runner at last year’s all-states, operates on the same philosophy. As a co-captain in her junior year, she’ll be taking the reins entirely from Mitchell next season, but appears unfazed by the upcoming challenge.

“Whatever I can do for the team,’’ she said, “is good enough.”

She is the fourth in her family to represent Milton, following her sister Colette, who graduated last year, and her parents, Walter and Tara.

In Bay State Conference meets, Milton won its its first four races, including beating Needham on Sept. 19.

Though the two schools won’t face off this fall, as they had hoped to, the goal is the same.

“It’s going to be fun to watch,” said Shaw.
