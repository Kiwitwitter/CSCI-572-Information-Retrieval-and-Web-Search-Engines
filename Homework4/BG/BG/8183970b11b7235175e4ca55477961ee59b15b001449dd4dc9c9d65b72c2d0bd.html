THURSDAY’S SCHOOL ROUNDUP

Manchester Essex field hockey rallies vs. Masconomet

By Jeremy Rellosa Globe Correspondent  October 06, 2017

The Manchester Essex field hockey team entered Thursday night’s Cape Ann matchup against host Masconomet with a seven-game shutout streak in its 7-0 start.

Masco broke through with an early goal from sophomore Makayla Graves in Topsfield. And, for the first time this season, coach Kara Crovo’s squad trailed in a game.

“We haven’t been here before,” she recalled telling the Hornets during a timeout.

They were in unfamiliar territory, but not out of the game.

“We wanted to rid that feeling of being down,” she added. Crovo told her team to apply a little more pressure.

Soon enough, the Hornets were back and buzzing, rallying for a pair of goals in a 2-1 victory.

With help from team captain Chanel Bullock, the Hornets’ first goal came from freshman Lily Athanas off a corner for a 1-1 tie at the half.

But with less than 16 minutes remaining, freshman Ana Coyne connected with sophomore Bridget Twombly, who scored on a corner to push the Hornets ahead.

Although a shutout would have been ideal, Crovo said her team has focused on applying pressure throughout the entirety of the game.

“This is the best game we’ve played two halves of field hockey,” she said of the matchup against Masco (8-2-2).

“We’ve been working on shooting and scoring in the second half, and we achieved that today.”

Their win Thursday evening is typical of the Hornets’ past success, both this year, and last. As most teams will attest, graduating top-performing seniors is a challenge, but Crovo said her team _ especially captains Samantha Booma, Christina Calandra and Bullock — has stepped up.

She commended her younger talent as a factor in the team’s success. “They haven’t [previously] had varsity experience, but they’ve adapted really well. They’re a very coachable group of kids,” Corvo said.

The Hornets went undefeated in the regular season last fall, and they have their eyes set on continuing that pattern. “Tonight’s game was a good stepping stone,” Crovo said.
