It’s time to pick up the Rams’ Jared Goff

MICHAEL AINSWORTH/ASSOCIATED PRESS

Jared Goff has accumulated more fantasy points than a host of quarterbacks who are owned in season-long leagues.

By Ed Ryan Globe Staff  October 05, 2017

Last week we told you the words “Goff” and “bust” are no longer mentioned in the same sentence — a blatant recommendation to either pick up Rams quarterback Jared Goff or consider using him in daily fantasy formats.

With a quarter of the season in the books, we’ve seen enough of the Rams’ signal-caller and the Rams offense to maintain our stance. It’s time to start paying attention to this guy.

Advertisement

Goff is averaging 21 fantasy points per start and has thrown for seven touchdowns. He’s thrown only one interception and has accumulated more fantasy points than a host of quarterbacks who are definitely owned in season-long leagues: Matthew Stafford, Cam Newton, Marcus Mariota, Philip Rivers, Derek Carr, Ben Roethlisberger, and Matt Ryan.

Last season was not pretty for Goff. He was the No. 1 overall pick in 2016, and when he played, he appeared overmatched. Now, with former head coach Jeff Fisher out of the way and a new staff led by Sean McVay, the Rams are first in the league in scoring (35.5 points per game) and Goff is throwing the ball almost 30 times per game.

The Rams’ matchup with Seattle in Week 5 is a bad spot for Goff (a Seattle matchup is a bad spot for any quarterback), which makes now the ideal time to make the acquisition; he’ll be available on the waiver wire. Stash Goff this weekend, as bye weeks begin, and reap the benefits later.

Three up:

Duke Johnson, RB, Cleveland: Johnson has averaged a little more than 15 fantasy points per game in the Browns’ last three. He won’t break the bank in your daily lineup.

Bilal Powell, RB, NY Jets: Powell excels when the Jets get him involved, and he should be very involved against the Browns.

DeVante Parker, WR, Miami: When facing the Tennessee defense, opponents suddenly fall into the “must-start” category. Dare I also suggest Jay Cutler as a cheap option at quarterback in daily fantasy?

Three down:

Cam Newton, QB, Carolina: The temptation to start Newton is understandable after his big day in New England, but that production will be difficult to replicate in Detroit. If you need an available quarterback, look at Arizona’s Carson Palmer.

Eddie Lacy, RB, Seattle: Now that Chris Carson is injured, avoid Lacy and all other Seattle backs.

Jordan Howard, RB, Chicago: Howard is saddled with a difficult matchup against Minnesota and a rookie quarterback (Mitchell Trubisky) making his first career start on a Monday night.
