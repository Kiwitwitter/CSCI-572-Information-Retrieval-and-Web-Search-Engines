FRIDAY’S SCHOOL ROUNDUP

Duo lifts Lynn Classical to girls’ soccer win

  • Google+

The Boston Globe Tweet Share

By Henry Brechter Globe Correspondent  September 30, 2017

At times this season, the Lynn Classical girls’ soccer team has had trouble finding the back of the net. When a goal is needed, the team’s best scorers — Isabel McGaughey and Abbey Ierardi — are counted on to make a play.

This was the case Friday night, as both girls connected for goals to lift the Rams to a 3-1 Northeastern Conference North victory over visiting Everett.

Advertisement

“Usually when we win, either one or both of them are involved in the scoring,” said Lynn Classical coach Mark Ierardi.

“Even when we’re holding a territorial edge, we struggle to score, but when we do, they’re usually the ones getting it done.”

After the teams exchanged goals in the opening minutes, Abbey Ierardi beat the Crimson Tide goaltender late in the half to put the Rams up, 2-1, at the intermission.

Minutes into the second half, McGaughey scored for a second time to put the game out of reach.

The Rams are now 4-3 while Everett dropped to 0-4.

Archbishop Williams 7, Fontbonne 3 — Freshman Ari Hay was one of seven players to score for the Bishops.

Silver Lake 2, Plymouth North 0 — Senior Caity Smith recorded her fourth shutout and sophomore Calliste Brookshire and junior Hannah Vogt scored for the Lakers (7-2).

Mystic Valley 8, Northeast 0 — Junior Lily Rhuda had a season-high three goals and an assist for the Eagles (4-3).

Boys’ soccer

Dedham 4, Norfolk Aggie 0 — Senior Dan Perno had two goals for the Marauders (3-5).

Saugus 8, Cathedral 0 — Junior Jonathan Rodriguez recorded four goals and Ryan Pugh added two for the Sachems (4-1).

Roxbury Latin 5, Thayer 0 — Senior forward Matt Uek netted two goals for the Foxes (2-1) at the ISL Showcase in Lancaster.

East Boston 6, Tech Boston 0 — Senior Luis Santo scored a goal and assisted on another for the Jets (4-0-2).

Sacred Heart 5, Hull 0 — Brandon Carroll netted four goals for the host Saints.

West Bridgewater 3, Middleborough 2 — Freshman Joe Larosa and Aiden Wells had goals for the Wildcats (6-1-1).

St. Mark’s 1, Lawrence Academy 0 — Senior Cooper Schmitz scored in the 85th minute to lift the host Lions (4-1).

Field hockey

Westwood 2, Medfield 0 — Kayla Conway and Hannah Medsker scored for the Wolverines (4-2-2).

Bourne 9, Wareham 0 — Carly Lacasse had three goals and two assists for the Canalmen (4-1-1).

Manchester Essex 3, Ipswich 0 — Sophomore Christina Bullock recorded two goals for the Hornets (6-0).

Andover 5, Chelmsford 1 — Senior forward Julia Carzon recorded her first hat trick of the season for the Golden Warriors (9-0).

Dennis-Yarmouth 8, Nauset 0 — Sophomore Lily Holmes and senior Summer Walsh each had a hat trick for the Dolphins (5-1-1).

Lexington 5, Reading 0 — Senior Lauren O’Keefe and sophomore Sydney Wardwell scored a pair of goals each to lift the No. 18 Minutemen (7-1). Junior Carly Briggs netted the other goal while dishing out three assists.

Pentucket 2, North Reading 0 — Meghan Bean and Bell Smith scored for the host Sachems (5-2).

Plymouth North 6, Pembroke 2 — Freshman Maeve Reynolds and senior Sophia Cotone scored two goals each for the Eagles (6-1).

Georgetown 5, Amesbury 0 — Jeremy Perkins collected two goals and an assist for the Royals (1-5-1).

Watertown 3, Wilmington 0 — Catherine Connors, Olivia Lampasona, and Ally Kennedy scored for the Raiders (7-1).

Falmouth 2, Marshfield 1 — Juniors McKayla Caswell and Jackie Tallino scored for the Clippers (5-1-1).

Girls’ volleyball

Norwood 3, Stoughton 2 — In the deciding fifth set, senior captain Kiki Ryan registered five kills and four blocks to help lift the host Mustangs (5-5). She finished with 35 kills and eight blocks to knock off the visiting Knights (8-2).

West Roxbury 3, Boston English 0 — Senior Noemi Ferrera notched 10 aces and 15 digs in the victory.

Barnstable 3, Dennis-Yarmouth 0 — Senior Caroline Brodt racked up 32 assists and seven digs to power the top-ranked Red Raiders (8-0). Junior Riley James (23 kills, 6 digs) and Ingrid Murphy (12 kills, 7 digs) delivered strong efforts.

Billerica 3, Lowell 2 — Ashley Crowley led the way for the victorious Indians with 15 kills and 20 blocks. Nicole Wedge added 33 assists over the five sets.

Melrose 3, Arlington Catholic 0 — Senior Lily Fitzgerald registered 27 assists to lift the host Red Raiders (10-1).

Boys’ golf

Austin Prep 214, Lowell Catholic 165 — Senior Nick Hampoian shot a 1-under 35 for Prep at Four Oaks Country Club.

Essex Tech 9, Greater Lawrence 0 — Junior Aaron Fournier shot a 41 to lead the Hawks (5-0) at the par-36 Crystal Lake Golf Club.

Case 144, Seekonk 118 — Senior Jake Machado shot a 41 to lead the Cardinals (5-3) at Touisset Country Club in Swansea.

Girls’ swimming

Haverhill 99, North Andover 79 — Meghan Driscoll finished first in the 100 fly (1:04.54) and the 100 backstroke (1:07.35), and teammate Katelyn Quinlan took first place in the 100 breast (1:14.70) and the 200 IM (2:25.41) for the victorious Hillies.

Methuen 101, Notre Dame (Tyngsborough) 73 — Methuen/Tewksbury sophomore Kyra Donahue won the 500-yard freestyle (6:00.39) and qualified for the state sectionals.
