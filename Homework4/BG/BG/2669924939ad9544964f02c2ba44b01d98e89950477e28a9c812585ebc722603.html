Patriots 27, Chiefs 20

Patriots turn it on, shut down Chiefs

By Michael Whitmer Globe Staff  January 17, 2016

FOXBOROUGH — Losing streak? What losing streak?

Showing the ability to pick up where they left off when Julian Edelman was on the field two months ago, the Patriots gave their fans exactly what they were hoping for: Flipping the switch on their late-season slide through the play of their returning stars, and advancing to another AFC Championship game.

Advertisement

Edelman caught 10 passes for 100 yards in his long-awaited comeback, and Rob Gronkowski – who missed two practices leading up to the game with back and knee issues – caught a pair of touchdowns from Tom Brady as the Patriots put an emphatic end to Kansas City’s 11-game win streak. Saturday’s 27-20 win over the Chiefs at Gillette Stadium in an AFC divisional playoff game sends the Patriots to the AFC Championship Game for the fifth straight season, matching the Raiders’ league record, set from 1973-77.

They’ll play for a spot in Super Bowl 50 on Jan. 24, either in Denver if the Broncos beat the Steelers on Sunday, or at Gillette Stadium if Pittsburgh wins.

After starting the season 10-0, the Patriots stumbled down the stretch, dropping four of their final six games, including their last two, as injuries piled up and ineffective play creeped in, especially along the offensive line.

None of that seemed to matter on Saturday.

Finally whole again on offense – or as whole as they can expect to be this time of year – the Patriots wasted no time getting Edelman into the flow. Edelman had missed the final seven regular-season games after breaking a bone in his foot on Nov. 15, but returned to practice less than a month later, targeting the playoffs for his return.

Advertisement

Edelman was targeted by Brady on the second play of the game (incomplete), then caught an 11-yard pass on the next play, his first reception in more than two months. All told, Edelman was targeted four times by Brady on the game-opening drive, which ended with an 8-yard touchdown pass to Gronkowski.

“Gronk made a big play. The game comes down to turnover ratio, third down, and red area, and I think we may have won all three,” Edelman said. “We take care of the ball, we execute on third, and we do well in the red area, that’s the formula to win.”

Edelman, who had a game-high 16 passes sent his way, limped into the X-ray room after the game. When asked if he had X-rays taken, Edelman anwered, “No.”

(Insert collective sigh of relief throughout New England here.)

The opening drive marked a polar opposite approach to the regular-season finale at Miami, when the Patriots — without Edelman and tackle Sebastian Vollmer, and limiting Gronkowski’s snaps and chances to preserve him for the playoffs — ran the ball on eight of the first nine plays. The conservative approach cost the Patriots a victory, which denied them home-field advantage throughout the playoffs.

Against the Chiefs, Brady dropped back to pass on the Patriots’ first 14 plays; according to the NFL, it’s the longest pass-only start to a playoff game since 1991, when the statistic started being kept.

Having Vollmer back in the lineup was just as important. Going up against a Kansas City defense that had 47 regular-season sacks, the Patriots kept a clean pocket for Brady throughout the game. The Chiefs had no sacks, and the only time Brady was hit hard was by linebacker Dezman Moses, well after the quarterback had released a pass. Moses was hit with a 15-yard roughing the passer penalty, which extended a second-quarter touchdown drive.

“You want to give Tom all the time in the world. Sometimes it works out, sometimes it doesn’t. That’s the goal every week,” said Vollmer, who started at left tackle. “I don’t think he got hit all that much, but we’ll see how well we did.”

In their wild-card win over the Texans, Kansas City opened the game with a kickoff return touchdown, which set the tone in a 30-0 rout. Faced with a deficit this time less than five minutes in, the Chiefs responded with a 17-play drive, converting four third-down plays. But they failed on the fifth third-down try, settling for a 34-yard field goal by Cairo Santos.

After a collection of punts (four straight, two by each team), the Patriots took over at their 2-yard-line midway through the second quarter, still leading, 7-3. This time they ran the ball; five times, in fact, including the final two plays. On second-and-goal from the 11, Brady scrambled for the right pylon, going airborne as he tried to nudge the ball across the goal line. A replay review determined that Brady was just short, but he took it in on a quarterback sneak on the next play, giving the Patriots a 14-3 lead. It came on the drive when Moses was penalized for roughing Brady.

Another field goal by Santos with 12 seconds left in the second quarter left the Patriots with a 14-6 halftime lead.

The game’s only turnover helped the Patriots create even more separation in the second half.

Chiefs quarterback Alex Smith completed a short pass to Knile Davis, who was stripped of the ball by Chandler Jones. Dont’a Hightower recovered, giving the Patriots the ball at their 31-yard-line.

Jones, six days after an incident that sent him to the police station, then the hospital, started the game and was credited with a sack and the forced fumble. He did not speak with the media after the game.

Five plays later after Davis’s fumble, the Patriots were in the end zone. Edelman and Gronkowski combined for 59 of the 69 yards on the drive, with Gronkowski catching a 16-yard touchdown pass from Brady, who completed 28-of-42 passes for 302 yards. The extra point made it 21-6, and the memories of a sloppy slog to the regular-season finish line were instantly replaced by another, more common scene in these parts this time of year.

The Patriots got some of their key players back, and it showed. Now they’re a win away from the ninth Super Bowl appearance in franchise history.

A pair of Chiefs touchdowns sandwiched a pair of field goals by Stephen Gostkowski. Charcandrick West’s 1-yard touchdown run with 1:13 left made the score 27-20, but Gronkowski recovered an onside kick, and Brady ended the game by taking a knee.
