CELTICS NOTEBOOK

Marcus Morris playing game of catch-up at Celtics’ training camp

John Raoux/AP

The Celtics acquired Marcus Morris from the Pistons in a July trade.

By Gary Washburn Globe Staff  October 08, 2017

WALTHAM — Marcus Morris was working out with Celtics assistant coach Jerome Allen on Sunday, swishing midrange jumpers from the side with ease. Morris should get plenty of opportunities for wide-open jumpers as he blends into the Celtics’ offense as the starting power forward.

Missing the first 10 days of training camp because of an assault trial (he was acquitted of all charges) has taken a physical toll on Morris. Although he wanted to play in Friday’s preseason win over the Philadelphia 76ers, he was held out and that likely will be the case again in Monday’s rematch at TD Garden.

Morris acknowledged he is far from optimal condition, saying he needed to lose weight. The Celtics are being cautious so he’s fully prepared for the season opener Oct. 17 against the Cavaliers.

“It’s OK, could be better. Conditioning, getting up and down, trying to get my feet under me,” Morris said after participating in half of practice. “Coach [Brad Stevens] has done a great job of letting me take my time and I gotta lose about seven pounds, I’m working on that still. These games are a great tuneup but it’s about being ready for Cleveland.”

Morris’s potential in the Celtics’ offense is intriguing. With Kyrie Irving, Gordon Hayward, and Al Horford the featured weapons in the starting lineup, the 6-foot-9-inch, 235-pound Morris will be matched against bigger power forwards or smaller small forwards. He created issues for former Celtic Jae Crowder in the past because of his ability to shoot over him.

Quickness will be Morris’s advantage when he faces bulkier power forwards.

“Brad is a great coach, so he’s putting me in positions where I’m at an advantage,” Morris said. “Playing around a great group of guys and starting at the 4 [power forward] should be great. The game is starting to move to smaller forwards that can be mobile. That’s right up my alley.

“I don’t think I can name two or three 4-men that could really do anything with me. I’m just trying to make sure I keep my strength and be a great rebounder. But on the offensive side, it will be too easy.”

Stevens lauded Morris’s versatility. “I think there are a lot of people that are playing smaller at that spot. One of the ways that we try to play is our wings do similar types of things,” he said. “[Morris is] not doing things that are much different than twos or threes. And then we go to who’s got the matchup we like and we post that person. If he’s being guarded by a big and he can play in space, catch behind the 3 and shoot or play off the 4-5 pick-and-roll, those are things he can do. We’re excited about what he would bring to us. Probably a long and versatile lineup when you think about him, Hayward, Jaylen [Brown], Jayson [Tatum], those types of lineups.”

But first, Morris has to play. The Celtics have two preseason games remaining, with the finale Wednesday against Charlotte. It’s still uncertain whether Morris will play in the preseason.

“It’s killing me. I really want to play,” he said. “I still feel like I’ve got a lot to prove in this league. I want to help my team. It’s about being smart and protecting my body, make sure no injuries happen.”

Said Stevens: “It’s obviously going to be an adjustment. Any time you’re going straight from not having done any practice to practicing every day, I think there’s going to be some time. Hopefully he gets back sooner rather than later to full-go. But we’re going to be appropriate to make sure he feels great.”

Making progress

The Celtics entered camp with 10 new players under contract, and Stevens is pleased with their progress. “I feel like we’re really gaining traction on what we want to do,” he said. “The next nine days are an important nine days. The game is only a small part of that. We want to feel healthy; we want to have a clear mind going into that season opener. We’ve got to still work on a lot of things to do that.” . . . The Celtics signed former William & Mary guard Daniel Dixon, who is likely headed for G-League Maine. Dixon worked out with the club the past two days. Dixon averaged 19.2 points last season for the Tribe along with 4.7 rebounds and 37.8 percent 3-point shooting. The Celtics had an open roster spot when they waived Devin Williams on Thursday.
