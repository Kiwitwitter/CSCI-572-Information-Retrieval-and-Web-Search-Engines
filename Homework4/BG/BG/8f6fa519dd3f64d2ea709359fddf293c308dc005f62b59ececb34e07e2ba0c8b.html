By Andrew Mahoney Globe Staff  September 29, 2017

By Jim McBride and Andrew Mahoney, Globe Staff

The Patriots improved to 2-1 with a thrilling 36-33 win over the Houston Texans, and now Week 4 brings a matchup with the Panthers, who are 1-2, on Sunday at 1 p.m. at Gillette Stadium. It’ll be televised on FOX.

Here are three keys for each team from Globe Patriots reporter Jim McBride.

Panthers:

Advertisement

1. Carolina on my mind: Cam Newton has to be engaged from the get-go. He’s recovering from shoulder surgery and is a more reluctant scrambler, but he’s still one dangerous dude.

2. Carolina blues: Christian McCaffrey is another dual threat. He is an elusive runner out of the backfield and also excels as a receiver. He has good vision and slippery open-field moves.

3. Carolina hurricanes: Julius Peppers (he’s still bringing it) and Mario Addison have to blow in off the edges swiftly to protect the young secondary and make Tom Brady uncomfortable.

Patriots:

1. Double trouble: Kelvin Benjamin and Devin Funchess are huge receiving targets who create mismatches with their height and physicality. Get on them quickly and play through the whistle.

2. Double score: This is Tom Brady’s offense always, but a complementary ground game is needed. Establish a presence early and let Brady carve them up late.

Advertisement

3. Double down: The Patriots have been victimized by the big play too often. Improve the communication, tighten the gaps, and set the edges to limit the home run balls.

To read McBride’s full scouting report on the game and his prediction, visit BostonGlobe.com/sports.

Some additional reading suggestions:

■ The last time the Patriots played the Panthers was in 2013 on Monday Night Football. The game ended with a Brady interception in the end zone. Linebacker Luke Kuechly bear-hugged Rob Gronkowski and a flag was thrown, but officials waved off the penalty, sealing the Panthers’ victory. Gronkowski did his best to deflect questions about the last matchup, writes Anthony Gulizia.

■ Kuechly admitted he might have gotten away with one, writes Christopher Price for the Boston Sports Journal

■ Rachel G. Bowers has a comprehensive look at what you need to know about the Panthers.

■ The Patriots managed to defeat Deshaun Watson and the Houston Texans. Brad Almquist writes that the defense will be tested once again by a mobile quarterback with Newton and the Panthers coming to Gillette Stadium.

■ Writing for ESPN.com, Aaron Schatz believes the Patriots are no longer the prohibitive Super Bowl favorites they looked to be before the season began.

■ Sports Illustrated’s Andy Benoit believes the struggling Panthers need to play to Newton’s strengths.

■ The deep pass has become the go-to weapon for the Patriots this year, writes Almquist.

■ Brady has been the best quarterback in the NFL through the first three games of 2017, and it’s not close, writes Almquist.

■ Week 4 got off to an ugly start in the Thursday night game between the Chicago Bears and Green Bay Packers. Bears linebacker Danny Trevathan lowered his head and smashed into the facemask of Packers wide receiver Davante Adams with the crown of his helmet. Adams had to be taken off the field on a stretcher. Adam Kilgore believes the NFL should adopt the NCAA’s targeting rule. and Trevathan would have been ejected if it did.

The Packers defeated the Bears, 35-14 as Aaron Rodgers threw for four touchdowns.

FORECASTING THE WEEKEND: The Globe staff’s NFL picks are in. Ben Volin is off to a fast start.

■ In his unconventional preview, Chad Finn believes that Newton will not put up the same numbers that Watson put up last week.

■ The 10-second runoff came at a bad time for the Detroit Lions, and Ed Ryan.

■ After Watson picked apart the Patriots in Week 3, Ryan thinks it is safe to declare that the Houston Texans quarterback is a legitimate fantasy football pick.

■ In his roundup of experts picks for this weekend, Eric Wilbur points put that the Patriots top in the league and total offense, while the defense has surrendered an NFL-worst 461 yards per game.

ANTHEM FALLOUT: Devin McCourty was one of eight NFL players, along with teammate Matthew Slater, invited to the league offices to participate in a discussion with Roger Goodell about President Donald Trump’s comments from last weekend and the various player demonstrations before Sunday’s games, writes Volin.

The Patriots’ protest could be limited to just last Sunday’s game, as wide receiver Danny Amendola revealed that he believes the entire team will stand this week.

■ Tennessee Titans tight end Delanie Walker said he and his family have received death threats since he told fans not to come to games if they felt disrespected by NFL players’ protests.

■ Washington Redskins running backs Keith Marshall and Rob Kelleybought an Xbox for a young boy they spotted wearing a Colin Kaepernick jersey.
