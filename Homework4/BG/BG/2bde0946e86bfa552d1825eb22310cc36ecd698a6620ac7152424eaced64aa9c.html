BC HIGH 42, BROCKTON 18

Danny Abraham makes himself at home in BC High rout

  • Facebook

The Boston Globe Tweet Share

By Owen Pence Globe Correspondent  September 30, 2017

Come 2018, Danny Abraham will grace the turf at Harvard Stadium with regularity, donning the crimson for what he hopes will be a long and successful Ivy League career.

Friday night, the BC High senior treated fans to a taste of what’s to come.

Advertisement

Playing on Harvard’s storied grounds because of field maintenance being done at their Viola Stadium, the Eagles confidently sliced apart a helpless Brockton defense on their opening drive. Naturally, it was Abraham who delivered the opening salvo.

Lining up at the Brockton 39, Abraham received his second touch of the evening, the 6-foot, 205-pound bruiser scurrying towards the right hash before bouncing outside, a demoralized Boxers safety in his wake.

The touchdown set the tone for a 42-18 victory, BC High (4-0) exhibiting efficiency in all three facets of the game and converting on a handful of key fourth downs to secure the result.

“It’s a wonderful privilege,” said Abraham of playing at Harvard Stadium. “Not everyone gets to do this. I wanted to take the most from this opportunity and just have fun and get the feel of [playing] here. It’s like a test drive. It was a great experience, I had fun and it [was] really important to me.”

Though Abraham garnered the ooh’s and ah’s from those in attendance, accumulating 121 yards and two TDs on 15 carries, it was quarterback Will Bowen’s steadying presence that helped keep Brockton (1-3) at bay.

Advertisement

After Bowen’s lone mistake of the night — an underthrown floater intercepted by Marquis Dos Santos — resulted in a Dexter Cumberlander touchdown, the 6-foot-3-inch senior took command.

Bowen scored on each of BC’s next three possessions — runs of 7, 3, and 5 yards — giving BC a commanding 28-6 lead entering halftime.

“I feel like he’s doing great,” said Abraham. “Like they said last week, it’s a three-headed monster: Will Bowen, Bobby DeMeo, and myself. We just keep on going behind a dominant O-line. He’s transitioned into a great quarterback and I’m happy to have him at the position.”

The Bowen/Abraham combination was aided by a stout Eagles defense that held Brockton to just 75 yards of total offense in the opening two frames.

“We were aggressive defensively,” said BC High coach Jon Bartlett. “I thought we did a nice job overall. Out tackling was solid and as we talked about coming in, [with] Brockton-BC High, the records get thrown out the window.”

Though Brockton briefly made things interesting early in the third, quarterback Jose Monteiro Jr. flaunting his stellar arm and taking advantage of an Eagles defensive breakdown by finding senior Paul Mitchell for an 81-yard touchdown strike, the effort wasn’t nearly enough.

BC High re-established control on its ensuing drive, Bowen converting on fourth-and-1 in Brockton territory to set up Abraham’s score, a 25-yard run.

Eagles senior Bobby DeMeo provided the fourth-quarter fireworks breaking off a 96-yard touchdown run, emerging from a pack of Boxers on an end-around action to seal Brockton’s fate.
