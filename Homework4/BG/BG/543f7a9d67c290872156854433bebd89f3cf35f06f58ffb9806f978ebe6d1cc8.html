The Weekender: Labor Day? Let’s do this.

By Michael Andor Brodeur Globe Correspondent  September 01, 2016

There’s really no wrong way to Labor Day: Some like to loaf and laze, others choose to grill and chill, and still others may even go out and (gasp!) do stuff. For those in that latter camp (you weirdos) there is no shortage of solid options to keep you occupied this weekend.

WILD WEST: While researchers continue to sift through and decode Kanye West’s cryptic speech from last week’s VMAs, His Yeeziness is already busy blowing minds with his hotly anticipated “Saint Pablo” tour, which comes to TD Garden on Saturday. Expect a set list full of surprises (generously grabbing from “The College Dropout” all the way up to “The Life of Pablo” and beyond), long lines at the merch table, and a slight crick in your neck the next morning from watching’s West’s amazing airborne stage hover above the audience like a spacecraft. BRO!!!!!!

TALKING HEADS: The enduringly goofy ventriloquist Jeff Dunham explores his many personalities through “the guys in the trunk” — a gaggle of armbound characters that test political correctness as well as Dunham’s larynx. Among them: Walter, an ostensibly lovable old curmudgeon who calls it like he sees it (uh-oh), Jose, a jalapeno pepper/stereotype on a stick (yikes), and Achmed, the googly eyed skeleton of a dead terrorist (double yikes). Dunham brings his “Perfectly Unbalanced” tour to the South Shore Music Circus in Cohasset on Friday. (Or for another kind of political (puppet) theater, you can always check out the free performance of “Whatforward Circus” from Vermont’s Bread & Puppet Theater at Cambridge Common on Sunday, as they investigate the “forward moving passions and politics of our capitalist culture.” (Sounds like a party!)

A LITTLE THING TO DO TOGETHER: Long smitten with Stephen Sondheim, the Lyric Stage Company opens its fall season with “Company,” the enduring tale of the chronically uncoupled Robert’s 35th birthday, and all of its attendant anxieties. Director Spiro Veloudos (who has already tackled most of Sondheim’s oeuvre) is celebrating 20 years as the producing artistic director at the Lyric Stage, and when it comes to identifying with “Company,” he feels like he’s got plenty of . . . company: “When you direct this play, you can’t help but think about whether or not you have a little bit of Robert in you,” he tells the Globe. The show starts performances Friday and runs through Oct. 9.

BACK IN THE SADDLE: Aerosmith frontman Steven Tyler brings his “Out on a Limb” tour to the Citi Wang Theatre on Sunday, moseying through a set list of his band’s biggest hits as well as selections from his new solo album of country tunes (a project inspired by the death of Phil Everly). He’ll be backed by the six-piece Nashville country-rock outfit the Loving Mary. And if Tyler gives Loving Mary singer Marti Frederiksen a turn at the mike, you may experience some light deja vu – he was the voice of the band Stillwater in Cameron Crowe’s “Almost Famous.” You can still grab tickets here.

SCREEN TIME: If sitting in a cool, dark theater sounds better to you than leaning over a hot grill, there are a couple of promising films opening Friday. Director Ira Sachs (who brought us the beguiling “Love Is Strange”) returns with “Little Men,” a film that Ty Burr calls “a small, beautifully observed New York fable about gentrification and growing up” with a standout performance by the young Michael Barbieri. Burr gives it 3½ stars. And Newton native (and “The Office” alum) John Krasinski stars and directs in “The Hollars,” a dysfunctional family comedy with a highly functional cast including Anna Kendrick, Mary Elizabeth Winstead, Sharlto Copley, Charlie Day, Mary Elizabeth Winstead, Josh Groban (whose comedic chops continue to surprise), and the endlessly marvelous Margo Martindale. Three stars.

Ansel Adams

Ansel Adams’s “Manzanar from Guard Tower” (1943).

UNSETTLING IMAGES: One of 10 “relocation centers” for people of Japanese descent during WWII, Manzanar (which translates to “apple orchard”) housed more than 11,000 people (most of them American citizens) who were forcibly removed from their own communities. The renowned American photographer Ansel Adams was invited four times over the course of 1943 to document life at the camp in photos (i.e. shoot propaganda), and a new exhibition at the Addison Gallery of American Art collects 50 of these rare works for a show that Mark Feeney calls both moving and disquieting. It’s up through Dec. 31.

A MORE PERFECT UNION (SQUARE): Now in its seventh year, the annual Starlabfest Music and Arts Festival kicks off Saturday at noon in Somerville’s Union Square. Starlab Studios is a top-notch multimedia production facility, but it’s also the hub of one of Boston’s most creative communities, and this rollicking open house finds its brightest talents out in full force. New Jersey’s powerhouse trio Screaming Females and Montreal’s Bloodshot BILL join a solid roster of local bands, including Mini Dresses, The Furniture, GYMSHORTS, Ascend/Descend, Save Ends, Animal Flag, Adam PC, and more. You can also take in comedy from Nick Chambers, Emily Ruskowski, and Sam Ike; throw some quarters at a pop-up video arcade from Bit Bar Salem; enjoy free barbecue from Scott Brothers New American Meat Co. (including veggie options); and shop around a flea market of clothing, records, and miscellany curated by High Energy Vintage.

FOR THE HOMEBODIES: MTV is getting a leg up on a massive crop of weed-related shows (HBO’s “High Maintenance,” Netflix’s “Disjointed,” NBC’s “Buds,” Amazon’s “Highland,” every single show on Viceland, etc.) with its own charming little pot-dealer comedy “Mary + Jane” (you see what they did there), premiering Monday. Matthew Gilbert finds the chemistry a little lacking (compared to, say, the blunt-lighting spark of “Broad City”) but there are ways to make these things funnier. I know a dude.

BOOM TOWN: And no Labor Day weekend would be complete without some explosions in the sky. Head down to the waterfront on Saturday (by 8:30 p.m.) for a fantastic fireworks display over Boston Harbor that should be a blast. Unless you’re a dog. (Which would be weird since you clearly have an e-mail account.)

Have a fantastic Labor Day! Don’t work too hard. See you next week.
