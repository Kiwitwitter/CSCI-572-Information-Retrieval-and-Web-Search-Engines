Recipe: Break the Yom Kippur fast with a chocolate-cinnamon babka

Sally Pasley Vargas for the Boston Globe

Chocolate-cinnamon babka

By Sally Pasley Vargas Globe Correspondent  September 26, 2017

Years ago, when I lived in Indiana and traveled East to visit family, friends would place orders for the chocolate babka from Babka Bakery on Manhattan’s Upper East Side. I was carrying back so many babkas, I had to solve the problem — and start making it. I use instant yeast (also called “rapid rise,”) which is milled into smaller particles than the regular and whose main advantage is that it doesn’t have to be dissolved in water and “proofed” before using. The eggy babka dough is spread with chocolate streusel, rolled up, then twisted so the slices have all kinds of chocolate swirls when you slice them. If you love yeast dough, you’ll have fun with this. This special loaf is not too sweet and will look stunning on the Yom Kippur break-fast table.Sally Pasley Vargas

Chocolate-cinnamon babka

Makes 1 large loaf

Advertisement

DOUGH

2cups all-purpose flour (plus more for
rolling)
1packet instant yeast
½teaspoon salt
⅓cup whole milk
2eggs
¼cup (½ stick) unsalted butter,
at room temperature
Vegetable oil (for the bowl)

1. In the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, stir 1½ cups of the flour, the yeast, and the salt on low speed. Add the milk, eggs and butter, and beat on medium speed for 4 minutes, or until very sticky and stretchy.

2. Add the remaining ½ cup flour and mix for 2 minutes longer on medium speed. The dough will be soft. With a rubber spatula, scrape down the sides of the bowl and form the dough into a ball. (Wet your hands if the dough is very sticky.)

3. Add a few drops of oil to a clean bowl. Place the dough in the bowl and turn it to coat it with oil. Cover with plastic wrap and leave in warm place to rise for 1 to 1½ hours, or until doubled in bulk.

STREUSEL AND ASSEMBLY

⅓cup walnuts
¼cup brown sugar
⅔cup flour
2tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
2teaspoons ground cinnamon
⅛teaspoon salt
3tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into slices
Butter (for the pan)
Extra flour (for sprinkling)
1egg, beaten with 1 tablespoon water

Advertisement

1. In a food processor, pulse the walnuts, sugar, flour, cocoa, cinnamon, salt, and butter until it is crumbly and dark brown. Transfer to a bowl. Set aside ½ cup of the streusel for the loaf pan.

2. Generously butter an 8½-by-4½-inch loaf pan. Sprinkle with ¼ cup of the reserved streusel, tilting the pan to cover the bottom and sides.

3. On a lightly floured surface, roll the dough to a 14-inch square. With a pastry brush, brush a 1-inch border on all four sides with water. Spread the streusel on the dough to the edge of the border. With a rolling pin, gently press the streusel into the dough.

4. Starting at the edge nearest you, roll the dough into a tight cylinder. With your fingers, pinch the long seam together and turn the roll so that the seam side is down. With a serrated knife, cut across the log to make 2 logs. Pinch the ends and roll each log back and forth gently with your hands to stretch each to about 12 inches.

5. Set one log horizontally on the work surface. Place the second roll on top of the first to form a cross. Pick up the left arm of the cross and twist it down and over to the right of the vertical roll. Pick up the right arm of the cross and twist it up and over to the left of the vertical roll. You should now have a loaf with three bumps.

6. Tuck the ends under the loaf and set it in the pan. Cover loosely with plastic wrap and leave in warm place to rise for 1 hour, or until the loaf rises about 1 inch above the top rim of the pan.

7. Set the oven at 350 degrees. Have on hand a rimmed baking sheet.

8. Brush the egg mixture on the loaf and sprinkle the remaining ¼ cup streusel over the top. Place the loaf pan on the baking sheet.

9. Bake for 40 to 45 minutes, or until the top is browned and an instant read thermometer registers 190 degrees when inserted into the center of the loaf. Check after 35 minutes and if the loaf is browning too quickly, place a piece of foil loosely over the top.

10. Set the loaf on a rack and cool in the pan for 10 minutes. Turn it out of the pan and set it right-side up on the rack to cool completely before slicing.
