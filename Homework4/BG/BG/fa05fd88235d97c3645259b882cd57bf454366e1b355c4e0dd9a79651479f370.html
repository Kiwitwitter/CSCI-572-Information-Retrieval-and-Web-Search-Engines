By Kathy Shiels Tully Globe Correspondent  September 15, 2017

DRAMA DAMES Put some drama into your “Girls Night Out.”

The newly renamed Greater Boston Stage Company — formerly known as the Stoneham Theatre — toasts its new season with a deal that includes a pre-show wine and cheese reception, all for $99, in Stoneham.

Advertisement

Get the best seats available on the second Thursday for any three shows that range from musical and romantic to mysterious.

The series kicks off with “Dames at Sea” (through Sept. 24); then the girls’ nights events are “Alligator Road,” (Oct. 19); “She Loves Me,” (Dec. 7); “The Irish and How They Got That Way” (March 15); “Miss Holmes” (April 12); “The Legend of Georgia McBride” (May 10); and “Calendar Girls” (June 7).

Can’t make a performance? Switch to another date at no extra charge. The Greater Boston Stage Company is located at 395 Main St. Visit bostonstagecompany.org.

SMASHING SUCCESS Can you guess what Cassandra, the princess of Greek mythology, political powerhouse Bella Abzug, and scientist Rosalind Franklin have in common?

Find out in “Glass Ceilings,” a collection of four one-act plays about the times in history when women have gained a foothold and tried to thrive. All plays, written and directed by women, are being staged at the Actors Studio in Newburyport through Sunday, Sept. 24.

Advertisement

The plays present the female perspective on the challenges, accomplishments, disappointments, and successes the three women faced that will engage, entertain, provoke, and inspire.

Three contemporary plays are set alongside Edith Wharton’s “Roman Fever,” based on her 1934 short story of the same name. In “Cassandra’s ReVision,” immortals Cassandra and Apollo get reauainted in the age of the Internet. In “Bella’s Choice,” Abzug runs for the Senate in 1976 when all the odds are against her. In “Dr. Franklin’s Spiral Staircase,” the scientist finds her 1952 breakthrough imaging of the DNA strand stolen by male scientists.

Performances are 8 p.m. on Fridays and Saturdays, 5 p.m. on Sundays. The Actors Studio is located at 50 Water St. in the Tannery, Mill No. 1, Suite No. 5.

Tickets are $20 adults, $18 students and seniors. Visit newburyportacting.org.

WALK FOR THE LIGHT Sit down for some heartbreaking facts: suicide claims more lives than the combination of wars, murders, and natural disasters. It’s the fourth-leading cause of death in the United States for adults between the ages of 15 to 64. A person dies by suicide roughly every 12 minutes.

Get up and walk to help shed some light, compassion, and understanding on the subject of suicide and mental illness.

The North Shore Out of the Darkness Community Walk takes place 10 a.m. until 12:30 p.m. in Wakefield on Saturday, Sept. 23 on the Common at Lake Quannopowitt. Check-in and registration is at 8 a.m. (online registration closes at noon on Friday, Sept. 22.)

Whether you walk solo or in a team, you’ll be one of 250,000 people nationwide participating in over 380 cities.

Bring Fido, too. As part of “Paws for Prevention,” friendly, leashed dogs are welcome at the event.

Register online and learn more facts at the American Foundation for Suicide Prevention website, afsp.org. For more information, call Jeannie Brown Dawson or Marguerite Milliren at 617-257-0518 or e-mail wakefieldwalk@gmail.com. Donations will be accepted until Dec. 31.

JUST MY TYPE Remember typewriters? When was the last time you typed on one?

Try it and more during the family-friendly Printing Fair in Haverhill at the Museum of Printing, 15 Thorton Ave. Doors open to the public from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. on Saturday, Sept. 23. Admission is free.

See examples of presses from the 1880s, including an iron hand press from 1886, and some of the very first computers. Print a name (even your own) in metal. Visit museumofprinting.org.

PICTURE THIS Get up close to Matt Lee, a Pulitzer Prize-winning photographer for The Boston Globe, at The Cabot in Beverly.

Get a snapshot of his life covering news, sports, and entertainment for the Globe and some insight into what goes on behind the camera during a slide show and discussion from 5:30 to 7 p.m. on Wednesday, Sept. 20. There will be complimentary hors d’oeuvres, fountain drinks, and a cash bar.

The Cabot is located at 286 Cabot St. This event is free for members only. Memberships range from $50 to $1,000 with benefits varying by level. To join, go to thecabot.org.
