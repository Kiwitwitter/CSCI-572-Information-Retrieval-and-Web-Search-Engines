DELL TECHNOLOGIES NOTEBOOK

Jordan Spieth has Tom Brady in his corner

barry chin/globe staff

Jordan Spieth is second in the FedEx Cup standings entering the Dell Technologies Championship.

By Barbara Matson Globe Correspondent  August 31, 2017

NORTON — During British Open week, Jordan Spieth was wearing Tom Brady pajamas. So was Brady.

The Champion Golfer of the Year and the Patriots quarterback struck up a friendship three or four years ago while working for Under Armour, and when Spieth was playing at Royal Birkdale this summer, Brady was texting encouragement and posting on social media that he was wearing matching pajamas, the TB12 recovery sleepwear.

“It seemed to have worked that week for me,’’ Spieth said. “It seems to work pretty well for him. So that’s not a sell for pajamas, but if you like what we’ve done, then you might want to go buy some.”

Spieth, who played a pro-am round Thursday in preparation for the Dell Technologies Championship at TPC Boston, said Brady is a tremendous guy to have in your corner.

“When he’s rooting for you, you feel like you just have — I keep using the word ‘energy,’ but you really do feel like you have something else when you get guys like him on your side,” Spieth said.

Brady recently joined Spieth at Augusta National, and Spieth discovered exactly what commitment means. While Spieth and his pals ate steaks and potatoes washed down with wine, Brady stuck to his diet.

“I mean, he’s extremely passionate about this,” said Spieth. “He had all this stuff at Augusta when all of us were just, you know, having some wine after a fun round.

“He shipped all of his stuff in there to have it as snacks and protein powder and all this stuff. It’s obviously elongating his career and doing well for him, so I’ll do whatever he’s doing.’’

Well, maybe. The vegetable-heavy Brady diet may be a little too much for the 24-year-old Spieth. Spieth said he recognizes the value of Brady’s food choices, but then scrunched up his face like a kid told to eat his broccoli.

“I’ve tried some of his stuff,’’ Spieth said. “I know it’s good for me. I’m not quite dialed in on the eating as he is. I don’t think he was when he was my age, either.”

Spieth and Justin Thomas planned to watch Brady’s football team play Thursday night at Gillette Stadium, at least for a quarter or so (early tee times Friday morning). Dustin Johnson, whose family is with him in Boston this week, said he’d go if he could, but added, “I don’t think mama’s going to let me out of the house.”

Tight race

The point of the PGA playoffs is to get to the last tournament of the season ranked in the top five in the FedEx Cup standings. If one of the top five wins the Tour Championship at East Lake Golf Club in Atlanta in late September, he wins the FedEx Cup, and the $10 million prize that comes with it.

It is possible for a player ranked No. 6 to win it, as Rory McIlroy did last year, but only if Nos. 1 through 5 fall apart.

Johnson sits first in the current standings with 4,466 points, a four-time winner after he knocked off Spieth in a one-hole playoff at last week’s Northern Trust, the first stop in the playoffs.

Spieth (3,871 points) is second and has three wins this season, including the British Open.

Thomas (3,044), with four wins including a major, is ranked third. Fourth-ranked Hideki Matsuyama (2,869) has three wins. No. 5 is Jon Rahm (one win, 2,404 points), and Rickie Fowler (one win, 1,996 points) lurks at No. 6.

Spieth said he’ll keep his focus trained on the Dell Technologies Championship.

“There’s not a whole lot I can do for myself at this point,” Spieth said, “other than if I could take care of business here, you jump one spot.

“I can drop to No. 5 within the next couple weeks, which isn’t exactly where you want to be, but likely to stay in the top five, which is a really good feeling. And it takes any added pressure off these last events.”

Many of those same players are contending for PGA Player of the Year (the Jack Nicklaus Trophy).

“I think it’s four guys, in my opinion,” Spieth said, “with Justin Thomas and myself and Dustin and Hideki. So the race is alive. It kind of depends on, you know, who wins the FedEx Cup out of the bunch, probably takes that prize.”

What’s up next

The next step in the playoffs is the BMW Championship in Lake Forest, Ill., Sept. 14-17. Players must be in the top 70 in the standings to qualify. Grayson Murray is on the cut line at No. 70, with 655 points. Chasing him are Nos. 71 Rod Pampling (652), 72 Bubba Watson (646), 73 Adam Scott (642), 74 Graham DeLaet (640), and 75 Patrick Rodgers (639). On the other side of the cutoff are Nos. 69 Kevin Tway (663), 68 Brandt Snedeker (663), 67 Bud Cauley (666), 66 Anirban Lahiri (667), and 65 Russell Knox (669). All are in the field this week . . . Thursday was pro-am day. Johnson was asked what advice he’d give to his amateur partners. “Hit more club,” he said. “They always come up short. Always.”
