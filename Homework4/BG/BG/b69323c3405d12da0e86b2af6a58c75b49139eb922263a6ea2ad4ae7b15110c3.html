MONDAY’S SCHOOL ROUNDUP

Masconomet gets perfect test from Lynnfield

Matthew J. Lee/Globe staff

Lynnfield’s Sydney Santosuosso (left) has a nose for the ball — too much so — in front of Masconomet’s Sophia Woodland during the second half Monday.

By Matt MacCormack Globe Correspondent  October 03, 2017

It only took the Masconomet Regional girls’ soccer team 37 minutes to open a 2-0 lead in Lynnfield on Monday afternoon.

The seventh-ranked Chieftains looked well on their way to their ninth straight victory.

Advertisement

Lynnfield, however, rallied to salvage a 2-2 tie against their Cape Ann rival, putting first blemish on an otherwise perfect start for Masco (8-0-1).

The Chieftains had outscored opponents, 32-2, before Monday’s tilt.

“I think you have to be tested . . . it’s good for us to kind of remember what it’s like,” Masco coach Alison Lecesse said afterward. “It’s always good to have those tests earlier in the season rather than later.”

Masco junior Elisa Gildea opened the scoring after cleaning up a cross from junior Sophia Woodland in the 12th minute. Freshman Allie Vaillancourt then gave the Chieftains a 2-0 lead off a deflected shot 37 minutes into the first half.

Lynnfield coach Mark Vermont realized the game was slipping away and called a timeout.

Advertisement

“Just settle down and just play,” Vermont told his squad. “Just keep fighting. And we did.”

Lynnfield senior midfielder Christina Benvenuto halved the deficit three minutes later with a shot from 35 yards out.

“That gave us momentum going into the half,” Vermont said.

Lynnfield (5-2-1) struck first in the second half, with senior Lizzie Shaievitz hammering home the equalizer off a corner kick in the 44th minute. It was Shaievitz’s 10th goal of the season.

The Pioneers withstood a late Masco barrage, earning a hard-fought tie against the league-leading Chieftains.

“We were attacking, attacking and we just couldn’t put one in,” Lecesse added. “Credit [Lynnfield’s] defense. They had some strong physical girls.”

Lecesse said that even though Masco remains undefeated, there’s work to be done.

“We just couldn’t make it happen,” Lecesse said. “We’ll break this down tomorrow in practice tomorrow . . . We have some tough games coming up.”
