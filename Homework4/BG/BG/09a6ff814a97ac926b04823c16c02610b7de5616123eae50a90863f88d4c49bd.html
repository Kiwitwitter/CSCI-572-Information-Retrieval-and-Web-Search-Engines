HIGH SCHOOL FOOTBALL NOTEBOOK | WEEK 4

Lynn, Lynn ... the ‘City of Wins’

Jim Venable

This week marks the return of The Bus, with 30 undefeated teams aboard.

By Henry Brechter Globe Correspondent  October 01, 2017

Of the 30 unbeaten high school football teams in Eastern Mass., several stand out as having enjoyed unexpected success. But the city of Lynn, in particular, which is usually ruled by St. Mary’s (4-0 overall, 2-0 Catholic Central), seems to have cornered the market on undefeated teams.

Based upon early returns, Lynn can now be called “The City of Wins” with four teams — Lynn English, Lynn Classical, Lynn Tech and the Lions — off to 4-0 starts.

Lynn Classical moved up one spot to No. 13 in the Globe’s Top 20 poll, while No. 20 St. Mary’s dropped one spot.

After a 2-9 finish a year ago, Lynn English coach Chris Carroll and his team remain unblemished through the first month of the season. The Bulldogs (4-0, 1-0 NEC South) have experienced a huge defensive turnaround, allowing just 14.5 points per game, a noticeable improvement from the 26.8 they allowed in 2016. Junior Matt Severance, a starter at quarterback and free safety, is a dynamic playmaker on both sides of the ball.

“People oftentimes overlook him because he plays quarterback,” said Carroll. “In reality, he’s a phenomenal free safety.”

Lynn Classical (4-0, 3-0 NEC Central) got off to a strong start in September after winning back-to-back defensive battles against Revere and Gloucester.

While the Rams’ defense has exceeded expectations, junior quarterback Keith Ridley has stolen the show. Ridley has eight touchdown passes, five of them to junior wideout Nashaun Butler.

Joining the Bulldogs and Rams among the unbeaten is Lynn Tech (4-0, 3-0 Commonwealth Small). The Tigers already have matched their win total from a year ago thanks, in large part, to eight returning starters on offense and seven on defense.

The offensive line has been stellar, having paved the way for more than 1,000 yards of team rushing in the first month of the season.

While each coach is focused primarily on his own team, they have echoed one another in appreciation of how well the city’s teams have performed as a whole.

“We work together in the offseason and things like that, and knowing all the work that goes into the different programs, it’s definitely nice to see,” said Lynn Tech coach James Runner. “It’s great for the community to know that these kids are dedicating time to the right things.”

Foxborough’s fine finish

Following an overtime loss to Foxborough in 2016, Canton once again found itself knocking on the door of its first win against the Warriors since 1990 during another overtime battle Thursday night.

But, due in part to some unclear overtime rules, it was not meant to be for Canton.

Following a 20-minute delay during which officials and coaches debated on overtime rules regarding the coin toss and scoring, Foxborough’s defense chose to take the field after winning the toss. The Warriors stuffed the Bulldogs in OT and Devin Hassett’s 10-yard TD pass to Jack O’Donnell sealed Foxborough’s 27-21 win.

It was the second TD connection between Hassett and O’Donnell, and it couldn’t have come at a better time.

“It was a little bit of controversy, but I think the refs got it down,” said O’Donnell. “We said ‘Let’s just end it on this play,’ and that’s what we did.”

Other fantastic finishes

In addition to the Foxborough-Canton showdown, several Week 4 contests had unorthodox endings:

■  Following a game-tying touchdown in the final minutes, Weston’s defense forced a fumble on a Whittier 2-point conversion attempt, Will Van Houten scooped it up, and returned it for two points and a 38-36 victory.

■  Lowell drove 99 yards in the final 1:16 of the game, scoring a touchdown on a Justin Villanueva plunge to beat Andover, 51-48, in another high-scoring Merrimack Valley Conference affair.

■ Rather than punt to Latin Academy with 22 seconds left, up by four points, West Roxbury elected to take a safety to secure a 6-4 home victory over the Dragons.

■ Medfield tied their game against Hopkinton with a pair of fourth-quarter touchdowns, but the Hillers answered when Ryan Kelleher found Will Abbott on a 5-yard TD pass completed a 2-point pass to Matt Lindquist to start overtime. Hopkinton’s defense then the Warriors’ 2-point conversion attempt on the following possession to lock up a 22-20 win and remain unbeaten (4-0).
