Reinforcements help Patriots’ offense regain form

By Ben Volin Globe Staff  January 17, 2016

FOXBOROUGH — Instant Analysis from the Patriots’ 27-20 Divisional Round win over the Chiefs:

■ Well, we got our answer Saturday night: The Patriots can be effective on offense when plugging in several players coming off injury at once. This was the Patriots’ offense we’re used to seeing: A 44-8 pass-run ratio, Julian Edelman making first downs over the middle (16 targets, 10 catches and 100 yards), Rob Gronkowski dominating in the red zone (two touchdown catches), and Tom Brady carving up the Chiefs’ secondary to the tune of 302 yards and two touchdowns. And they were still throwing the ball to close out the win in the fourth quarter (although maybe they should consider running the ball more).

It wasn’t the most explosive offensive performance by any stretch, and Edelman dropped too many passes for our liking, but given all of the new pieces in the lineup, and the quality of the Chiefs’ defense, that was a really positive performance by the offense.

■ The return of Edelman, Gronkowski, and Danny Amendola also greatly helped the offensive line, which gave Brady a clean pocket all night and didn’t allow a single sack. The Chiefs’ pass rush was clearly limited by injuries to Justin Houston and Tamba Hali, but give the offensive line a lot of credit for protecting Brady well all night — particularly Sebastian Vollmer, playing three weeks after suffering a high ankle sprain.

■ It wouldn’t be a Patriots game without an injury or two, and this time it was the linebacker corps that got banged up. Jamie Collins left in the third quarter with a back injury and didn’t return, Jerod Mayo left with a shoulder injury, and Dont’a Hightower was clearly limited by his knee injury. Chandler Jones also hurt his right leg in the fourth quarter and came out of the game. The Patriots had Jonathan Freeny and Dekoda Watson in the lineup at the end of the game. Not exactly what you want to see in the playoffs.

■ The Chiefs moved the ball pretty well all game, running 20 more plays than the Patriots and converting 12 of 20 on third down (at one point it was 10 of 15). Alex Smith deserves a lot of credit for making some big-time throws on third down and keeping the chains moving with his feet, particularly on a 15-yard scramble on third-and-13. But with Jeremy Maclin clearly hobbled by his ankle injury, the Chiefs just didn’t have enough offensive firepower to keep up with the Patriots, and couldn’t punch the ball into the end zone early, settling for two short field goals.

■ Not surprised to see Chandler Jones get the start. The Patriots needed him, and the team made him take his medicine during the week by addressing his incident with the media. He didn’t have his best game, and got pushed around on run defense a bit, but still had four tackles, a sack, and a forced fumble.

■ Smith completed 29 of 50 passes for 246 yards and a touchdown. Travis Kelce had six catches for just 23 yards, with Patrick Chung smothering him for most of the game. The Chiefs did average 4.3 yards per carry, but the Patriots were more than happy to let them chew up yards on the ground.

■ Not a great day for Logan Ryan, however, who got beaten by Albert Wilson for a touchdown, committed a pass interference on the 1, and appeared to give up several catches on third down to Wilson and Jason Avant. He had a great season but has struggled a bit the last few games. No. 3 cornerback Justin Coleman, however, had a really nice game.
