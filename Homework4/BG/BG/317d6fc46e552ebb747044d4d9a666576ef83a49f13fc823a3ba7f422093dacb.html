COLLEGE FOOTBALL ROUNDUP

UMass falls just short in upset bid against Tennessee

  • E-Mail

    Share via e-mail

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Google+
  • LinkedIn
Comments Print
The Boston Globe Tweet Share

Associated Press  September 23, 2017

John Kelly rushed for 101 yards with a touchdown and Tennessee’s defense recorded seven sacks on Saturday as the Volunteers staggered to a 17-13 victory over 27½-point underdog UMass in Knoxville, Tenn.

Tennessee (3-1) was trying to bounce back from a 26-20 loss at No. 20 Florida in which the Vols allowed a tiebreaking 63-yard touchdown pass as time expired. The Vols struggled to put away winless UMass (0-5), which played the entire game without star tight end Adam Breneman and most of the second half without starting quarterback Andrew Ford due to injuries.

‘‘We needed to get win No. 3 and we got win No. 3,’’ Jones said. ‘‘At the end of the day, it’s all about getting the victory, but [that was] just flat-out unacceptable. The performance is unacceptable. We'll get back to work tonight and correct it and correct our deficiencies.’’

Tennessee was held scoreless for the game’s first 25-plus minutes and final 22 minutes. The sluggish performance and the summer-like conditions resulted in a sterile atmosphere at Neyland Stadium. Although the announced attendance was 95,324, there appeared to be approximately 30,000 or so fewer fans in the fourth quarter as UMass attempted to rally.

UMass threatened to take the lead with less than 10 minutes left when Andy Isabella’s 42-yard run put the Minutemen at the Tennessee 38. But the Minutemen went backward from there — thanks in part to a Jonathan Kongbo sack — and punted on fourth and 16 at the 44.

The Minutemen’s next drive stalled after UMass got as far as Tennessee’s 45. Tennessee clinched the victory when UMass lost the ball on downs at its 17 with 22 seconds left.

‘‘I'm proud of the way they played,’’ UMass coach Mark Whipple said. ‘‘We wanted to get it to a one-possession game in the fourth quarter, and we did. We just couldn’t make the play that we needed to, to get us over the top.’’

Both teams used backup quarterbacks for much of the second half. UMass’ Ross Comis took over for the injured Ford late in the third quarter and went the rest of the way. Tennessee’s Jarrett Guarantano replaced Quinten Dormady in the third quarter, but Dormady returned as the Vols failed to move the ball on Guarantano’s three series.

The Vols finally broke a scoreless tie on Kelly’s 12-yard touchdown run around the left end with 4:33 left in the half. The touchdown was set up by Dormady’s 66-yard completion to Brandon Johnson, who had seven catches for 123 yards to set career highs in both categories.

UMass responded with Comis’s 5-yard touchdown keeper around the left end, capping a series helped along by an unsportsmanlike conduct penalty on Jones that gave the Minutemen first-and-goal. Tennessee remained ahead, 7-6, only because Michael Schreiner missed the ensuing extra-point attempt.

Tennessee extended the lead to 14-6 when Dormady found Tyler Byrd alone in the back left corner of the end zone for an 8-yard touchdown with 25 seconds left until halftime. Aaron Medley added a 40-yard field goal midway through the third quarter.

UMass cut the lead to 17-13 late in the third period on Ford’s 13-yard pass to Sadiq Palmer.

Alabama 59, Vanderbilt 0 — Damien Harris ran for a career-high 151 yards and three touchdowns, Bo Scarbrough added two TDs, and the top-ranked Crimson Tide (4-0) routed the Commodores (3-1) in the Southeastern Conference opener for both teams in Nashville.

Alabama has won 19 straight over the SEC East. The Crimson Tide hasn’t lost to Vanderbilt since 1984 or in Nashville since 1969.

Southern Cal 30, California 20 — Stephen Carr ran for a fourth-quarter touchdown two plays after the fifth-ranked Trojans’ defense forced one of their six turnovers in a Pac-12 victory over the Golden Bears in Berkeley, Calif.

Sam Darnold threw for 223 yards and two touchdowns for USC (4-0, 2-0) but also had an interception. Ross Bowers finished 22 of 50 for 303 yards with one touchdown, four interceptions, and two lost fumbles for California (3-1, 0-1).

Michigan 28, Purdue 10 — Backup quarterback John O'Korn rallied the No. 8 Wolverines (4-0, 1-0 Big Ten) from a halftime deficit to top the Boilermakers (2-2, 0-1) in West Lafayette, Ind.

Chris Evans finished with 14 carries for 97 yards and two second-half touchdowns for Michigan. O'Korn was forced into action when starting quarterback Wilson Speight left late in the first quarter with an undisclosed injury, and the former Houston starter finished 18 of 26 for 270 yards with one touchdown and one interception.

Miami 52, Toledo 30 — Malik Rosier threw for 333 yards and three touchdowns, Mark Walton needed only 11 carries to rush for a career-best 204 yards and another score, and the No. 14 Hurricanes rallied at home.

Travis Homer ran for two touchdowns and Braxton Berrios, Dayall Harris, and Christopher Herndon each caught TD throws for the Hurricanes, who hadn’t played in 21 days because of the effects of Hurricane Irma. Miami trailed, 16-10, at the half before controlling the final 30 minutes.

N.C. State 27, Florida State 21 — Ryan Finley threw for 230 yards and two touchdowns as the Wolfpack (3-1, 1-0 Atlantic Coast Conference) got their first road win over a ranked team since 2008, defeating the No. 12 Seminoles (0-2).

Jaylen Samuels had two touchdowns as North Carolina State broke a 10-game losing streak to ranked teams. The Wolfpack’s last win over a Top 25 team coincidentally was against Florida State in 2012. Their last victory on the road over a ranked team was at North Carolina.

Ohio State 54, UNLV 21 — J.T. Barrett threw touchdown passes to five different receivers in the first half, and the No. 10 Buckeyes (3-1) cruised to a nonconference win over the Rebels (1-2) in Columbus, Ohio.

Barrett, who has taken the brunt of the criticism for Ohio State’s inconsistent passing game this season, completed 12 of 17 passes for 209 yards before handing the game over to backup Dwayne Haskins near the end of the first half.

The big half for Barrett moved him into second place behind Art Schlichter for all-time passing yards at Ohio State.

TCU 44, Oklahoma St. 31 — Darius Anderson ran for a career-high 160 yards and three TDs, and the 16th-ranked Horned Frogs (4-0, 1-0 Big 12) upset the No. 6 Cowboys (3-1, 0-1) in Stillwater, Okla.

Kenny Hill passed for 228 yards for TCU, which put itself in great position to start league play.

The Horned Frogs gave Oklahoma State’s Mason Rudolph plenty of time to throw, and he completed 22 of 41 passes for 398 yards and two touchdowns with two interceptions.

Louisville 42, Kent State 3 — Lamar Jackson accounted for three touchdowns to set a school career record and lead the No. 19 Cardinals to a rout of the Golden Flashes (1-3) in Louisville, Ky.

Despite missing Jaylen Smith, the team’s top receiver, the reigning Heisman Trophy winner threw for 299 yards and two TDs on 18-of-22 passing. His 16-yard touchdown pass to Javonte Bagley in the third quarter ended the scoring and gave him 88 total touchdowns, breaking Chris Redman’s career mark.

Yale 49, Cornell 24 — Zane Dudek and Deshawn Salter combined for 316 yards rushing and four touchdowns and the Bulldogs (2-0, 1-0) pulled away from the Big Red (0-2, 0-1) in the second half for an Ivy League victory in New Haven.

James Madison 28, Maine 10 — Bryan Schor tossed four touchdown passes, including three straight in the second half to open up a close game, and the defending FCS champion Dukes (4-0, 1-0 Colonial Athletic Conference) topped the Black Bears (1-2, 0-2) in Harrisonburg, Va.

New Hampshire 28, Rhode Island 14 — Trevor Knight hit Neil O'Connor on a 76-yard scoring strike in the fourth quarter as the Wildcats (3-1 2-0 CAA) secured a victory over the Rams (1-3, 0-2) in Durham, N.H.

Knight threw for a career-high 420 yards and three touchdowns, and rushed for another score.
