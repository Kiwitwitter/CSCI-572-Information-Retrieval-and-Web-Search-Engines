What She’s Having: Popovers worth popping over to Cambridge for

David L. Ryan/Globe Staff

A popover with cream cheese, house cold-smoked salmon, red onion, fresh herbs and capers at NOCA Provisions.

By Kara Baskin Globe correspondent  October 10, 2017

What She’s Having is a new weekly column in which Globe food writers celebrate their local favorites.

In a hectic world, it’s important to honor the neighborhood cafe, the homegrown place, the spot with crinkled newspapers on the benches, local denizens pecking at laptops in the windows, and friendly dogs slurping from bowls on the sidewalk. It is here that your mind might ricochet from brilliant thought to brilliant thought after a few slurps of cold brew — yet also where time stands still before you have to go out into the mean world again.

I pay homage every Friday, when I dive headlong into the belly of the beast and head from Arlington, where I live, to NOCA Provisions, on the fringes of North Cambridge. I drop my kids at school by 8 and have a moment of internal debate: I could point my car homeward and make my own coffee. I could toast a frozen bagel and slather on some cream cheese and maybe add jelly, if I’m feeling frisky.

Nah. I’m going to cross the border for a popover, lights on Mass Ave. be damned.

Part of the excitement is the location. NOCA feels like a discovery each time you approach it from Rindge Avenue, beckoning from an ordinary block, because, well, it’s not really near anything. The area is almost completely residential. It’s not quite at Alewife Brook Parkway. It’s a few blocks over from Ferro’s Foodtown, if you want to name-check a landmark. Not too far from Porter Square, but not exactly close, either. Really, in its own world.

Meanwhile, several blocks over, Bagelsaurus has cornered the breakfast market with its excellent bagels and hot smoked salmon. The lines are long. Really long on the weekends. NOCA still feels somewhat raw and new — you can breeze in and breeze out — and its popovers with salmon are, dare I say it, every bit as good as anything you’d find down the street.

It’s also completely of its place. Last time I was there, I sat next to a woman nibbling avocado toast wearing on-trend leisurewear and Birkenstocks, scribbling notes in a well-loved copy of “Edible Boston.” It was just so Cambridge, in the coziest, most eye-rollingly lovable way possible.

But you don’t come to people-watch, even though that’s part of the fun. You come for the popovers with house cold-smoked salmon, cream cheese, red onions, and capers. The menu advertises the dish with house-made multi-grain bread, and you can definitely go that route. But ask for a popover. Trust me. It’s crusty on the outside, soft and eggy within, with plenty of crannies for capers to disappear into, only to reappear on your final bite or two, just when you thought that your happiness had ended.

The food is good because you’re in good hands. The place is run by Robert Harris, who owns a Season to Taste, a catering company, and the Table, an excellent special-occasion restaurant, both in North Cambridge. His popovers are made from scratch; salmon is cured in-house and comes from Wulf’s Fish.

“I thought: What can we do that not everyone is doing? Popovers came to mind as something that, hey — it has the egg and some richness, but it’s light and airy,” Harris says.

That’s just it: You can eat a couple of these babies without feeling weighed down. And if you’re not a salmon fan? That’s OK, too. Get ’em with a rotating assortment of toppings: avocado with pear, walnut, and Great Hill blue cheese, or maybe fresh chèvre, bruléed peaches, and cider syrup. It’s just so pleasant, so unexpected, to find this kind of offbeat breakfast (with a soul music soundtrack, even) in a quiet corner of the city. There are no circus lattes here, no easy chairs that swallow you whole.

Service is fast, too. Most dishes are ready in five minutes, tops. You can linger on a bench with a newspaper of choice or hide behind your laptop until you complete the Great American Novel, or you can just order at the counter and bolt down the street. And while many people seem to bike here — on my last visit, I was nearly mowed down by a man in a three-piece plaid suit and top hat, really — there’s also a parking lot around back, which will come in handy when they open for dinner in a few weeks.

In the meantime, it’s a morning kind of place. A spot to start your day off a little bit quirkily, with nooks and crannies and syrups and salmon, with maybe a guy in a plaid three-piece suit whizzing on by.

156 Ridge Ave., Cambridge, 617-902-0764, www.nocaprovisions.com
