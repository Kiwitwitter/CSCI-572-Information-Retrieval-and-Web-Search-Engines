Players of the year

Division 1: Madison Sollows, Barnstable

Sollows helped Banrstable win its third state title in four years. She was named to the MGVCA All-State team and was an Old Colony League all-star. Sollows amassed 413 kills with a .332 hitting percentage in her senior season.

Division 2: Corinne Cox, Notre Dame (Hingham)

One of the leading setters in the state, Cox finished the year with 771 assists while helping Notre Dame win the Division 2 state title. A three time all-state selection, she was also an Under Armour All-American.

Division 3: Jenny Weston, Ursuline

Advertisement

Weston turned in 410 assists, 112 kills, and 149 aces while helping Ursuline reach the South sectional semifinals. Weston, a four-time Catholic Conference All-Star and MGVCA All-State selection, will play at New Hampshire.

All-Scholastics

Athena Ardila, Weston, junior

Ardila followed her record-setting sophomore campaign by turning in 338 kills this season. She now has a total of 1,265 kills through three seasons. A two-time MGVCA All-State selection, she plans to play at Northeastern.

Wyllie Boughton, Andover, senior

Forming a potent tandem with Maddy Reppucci, Boughton helped lead Andover to a fourth straight North sectional title and a second state final appearance. She earned MGVCA All-State honors, tallying 171 kills and 78 aces.

Jillian Boyle, North Andover, senior

Boyle was named Division 1 Player of the Year in the Merrimack Valley Conference, turning in 59 aces and 573 digs from the libero position. A three-time MVC All-Star, she hopes to continue volleyball in college.

Emma Brown, Newton North, junior

The Bay State Conference All-Star helped lead the Tigers to the Division 1 Central-East title for the second straight year. A member of the garden club, she also enjoys playing squash, running, and reading.

Marissa Cataldo, Melrose, senior

Advertisement

The MVP of the Middlesex League, Cataldo finished with 200 kills and 401 digs while earning MGVCA Division 2 All-State team honors. She is a two-time Middlesex League All-Star, as well as a National Honor Society member.

Demi Fogarty, Arlington Catholic, senior

Fogarty earned Catholic Central League MVP honors for a second time while helping lead the Cougars to the Division 2 North sectional title. She finished her career with over 1,000 kills, and was also a league all-star in basketball.

Elizabeth Hamilton, Canton, senior

Hamilton earned Hockomock MVP honors, leading the Bulldogs to a 15-4 season and a berth in the Division 2 Central final. She is a three-time Hockomock League All-Star and a MGVCA All-State honoree.

Riley James, Barnstable, freshman

Only a freshman, the 5-foot-10-inch outside hitter had an astounding 293 kills this year and helped lead Barnstable to a state title. She was named an Old Colony All-Star and to the MGVCA All-State team.

Gabriella Koenig, Concord-Carlisle, senior

Koenig helped lead Concord-Carlisle to the Division 1 Central-West sectional title, as well as the Dual County League championship. She led the team with 168 kills and 57 aces, earning league MVP honors.

Kaitlyn Kopecky, Case, senior

Kopecky helped Case to the Division 3 South sectional title and the South Coast Conference championship for the second year in a row. The middle hitter was the leading blocker in the SCC as well as an all-star.

Lauren Manis, Bishop Feehan, senior

Manis helped Bishop Feehan to a South sectional semifinal. The middle hitter was named an Eastern Athletic Conference All-Star. Also a league all-star in basketball, she has committed to play hoops at Holy Cross.

Yuleska Ramirez-Tejeda, Cambridge, senior

The Dual County League Player of the Year and three-time league all-star finished with 401 kills and 157 aces this season, both among the best marks in the state. A three-time league all-star, she also plays basketball and softball.

Maddy Reppucci, Andover, senior

Reppucci helped lead Andover to its fourth straight North sectional title and second straight state final appearance. She tallied 172 kills and 83 blocks. The senior is a two-time MGVCA Division 1 All-State team member.

Shannon Ryan, Lynnfield, senior

Ryan earned Cape Ann League Player of the Year honors for the second straight year, turning in 275 assists to finish her career with 668. Her efforts helped Lynnfield to the North sectional title and a state final berth.

Avery Spaziani, Notre Dame (Hingham), senior

Spaziani turned in 198 kills and had 301 digs while helping Notre Dame to the Division 2 state title. She finished her career with 570 kills and 148 aces. The two-time All-State team member will play volleyball at Connecticut.

Coaches of the Year

Division 1: Jim Crandall, Concord-Carlisle

Crandall led the Patriots to a 22-1 season, the Dual County League title, and the Division 1 Central-West sectional title. His record is 91-36 in five years at the school. He has also coached the Smash Volleyball Club for 25 years.

Division 2: Rachel Reis, Arlington Catholic

Reis lead the Cougars to a 20-5 record that included the Catholic Central League title and the Division 2 North sectional title. A Stoneham grad and Hall of Fame member, she has a 103-30 record in six years at Arlington Catholic.

Division 3: Michaela Goncalves,Case

Goncalves led Case to the Division 3 South sectional title and a South Coast Conference championship this season. She boasts a 44-6 record after two seasons. A Durfee and Bridgewater State graduate, she played softball in college.
