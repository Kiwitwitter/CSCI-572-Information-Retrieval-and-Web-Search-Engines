Cohasset boys’ soccer rallies past Whitman-Hanson

By Matthew MacCormack Globe Correspondent  October 10, 2017

The Cohasset Skippers are three-time defending boys’ soccer champions of the South Shore League’s Small division.

Coach Jim Willis, wanting to maintain that success and have his team tested before the tournament, aims for teams from a higher division when scheduling a nonleague foe.

Advertisement

The host Skippers (12-0) logged a thrilling 2-1 over Division 2 foe Whitman-Hanson at Alumni Field on Monday.

“We try to schedule all of our non-league games against big-time opponents,” Willis said. “When it comes down to the later rounds of the tournament, you have to have experience against good teams.”

No. 4 Cohasset persevered on senior Jay Frederick’s header with 10 minutes remaining.

Whitman-Hanson (5-6) seized the lead 12 minutes into the game before junior Michael McDougald tied it six minutes into the second half .

“We pretty much controlled the game after that,” Willis said of the early-second half equalizer.

Advertisement

Willis said senior defender Grant Guempel was remarkable in helping to hold the Panthers to just one goal.

“He’s just locked down the defense,” Willis added. “I couldn’t ask any more of him.”

The Skippers reached the Division 4 state championship game last season, but fell to Monson, 3-2. Cohasset had outscored their last four opponents 19-0, entering Monday.

Whitman-Hanson provided a sturdier test – one that Willis hopes will prepare his team when the playoffs begin.

“They haven’t faced a lot of adversity yet,” Willis said.

“When the tournament starts, we need to be in the right frame of mind.”
