HIGH SCHOOL GIRLS’ VOLLEYBALL NOTEBOOK

Rob Slavin’s ‘courage and loyalty’ feted after 500 wins at Sacred Heart

Debee Tlumacki for the Globe

Sacred Heart girls’ volleyball coach Rob Slavin returned the applause in acknowledgement of his 500th career win after Tuesday night’s triumph over Avon.

By Dan McLoone Globe Correspondent  September 28, 2017

For Sacred Heart girls’ volleyball coach Rob Slavin, 500 career wins was a long time coming. So when junior outside hitter Abby Slavik slammed home the final point of the game to punctuate win number 500 – a dominant 25-8, 25-5, 25-11 sweep of Avon on Tuesday – the veteran coach took a moment to turn aside and reflect before his players mobbed him.

“This school has been such a blessing in my life,” said Slavin, who started the volleyball program at Sacred Heart in 1989. “I taught here for a while. My daughter went to school here. My wife taught here. So it’s been a big part of our lives . . . A number of the kids who are on my team, I’ve coached their sisters and sisters of sisters, so it’s been a real family thing.”

Slavin came to Sacred Heart in 1989 as an English teacher. As is the case with many small Catholic schools, he was asked what sports he would potentially be willing to coach. The ones that he had experience with, volleyball and hockey, were not offered by the school at the time.

But what the Saints did have was a gymnasium and a handful of field hockey players who were upset the school had shut down the field hockey program. And so, in the strangest of pairings, Slavin became the head coach of the field hockey-turned-volleyball team.

“That first year, I inherited disgruntled field hockey players,” said Slavin, a Brewster resident. “That was my team. No one had ever played volleyball before. It kind of started on rocky footing, but that’s how it happened. It’s not that unusual that kids start the sport in the ninth grade, but in this case, you had sophomores, juniors and seniors who had all played another sport and they all decided to play volleyball.

“So it was a little chaotic from the onset.”

The Saints went 3-17 that first season, which has been the only losing season in Slavin’s career. His players started to get involved in summer programs and offseason teams, steadily improving as Sacred Heart became a power in the Mayflower League despite never attracting top volleyball talents.

“We’ve never been a team built around size or a power player,” Slavin said. “It’s more defense, serving, serve receiving. Basic things that we’ve always been pretty strong at. In 28 years, I’ve maybe had three or four 6-foot kids. You have to build around other fundamentals.”

In his 26 years coaching at Sacred Heart – he briefly left the position in 2010 when his daughter Noelle graduated before returning in 2014 – Slavin has built up quite a resume of accomplishments.

He has coached Junior Olympics since 1992 and was honored with the Mintonette Award by the International Volleyball Hall of Fame in 2014. He was inducted into the Massachusetts Girls’ Volleyball Coaches Association Hall of Fame in 2006 and served as MGVCA president from 1999 to 2011, during which time he increased membership from 28 coaches to over 250.

He has amassed 19 league titles, 13 Mayflower League Coach of the Year honors, and was named the Boston Globe Coach of the Year in 1999.

“It’s not so much about the winning, it’s about the kids,” said Sacred Heart athletic director Bob Duquette. “He expects a lot from the kids. The kids know what’s expected of them and they’re willing to put in the time because they see the time he puts in . . . And as far as loyalty goes, he’s as loyal to the school and the kids as anybody. And that’s what counts.”

As Slavin addressed the Sacred Heart crowd in a ceremony after win number 500, his team stood behind with balloons and customized t-shirts. On the back of those tie-dyed t-shirts made for their head coach’s milestone win was the phrase “Courage and loyalty.” It seemed appropriate since there were no better words to describe Slavin.

“I’ve never really looked for another job,” Slavin said. “I’m involved in the game in many other areas, but in terms of coaching, I’ve never wanted to coach another school or look to the college ranks. This was a natural fit.”

Quick digs

■ Billerica (4-5) will host North Andover (5-2) in its annual Dig Pink match on Oct. 4 at Ed James Gymnasium to raise money for the Side-Out Program, which teams with the volleyball community to raise funds for breast cancer research. A snack bar, raffle table and tickets to a 50-50 raffle will be sold throughout the evening, beginning with the freshman and JV games at 4 p.m. and running through the varsity match at 5 p.m.

Fans are encouraged to wear pink, and all proceeds from the evening will go directly to the Dig Pink Side-Out Foundation.

■ Rob Slavin was not the only head coach to hit a milestone this week. Bourne head coach Andy Mather picked up his 300th career win on Saturday with a 3-0 win over Ursuline.

Mather’s high school coaching career has included stops at Falmouth, Plymouth, Medway, Oliver Ames and Fontbonne Academy, while also serving as the director of the Beantown Volleyball Club.

■ With Andover dropping two sets to Central Catholic in a five-set win earlier this week, Arlington Catholic (7-0) and New Bedford (8-0) remain the two lone teams in Eastern Massachusetts who have won every set so far. Extending those streaks won’t be easy, however, as the Cougars take on No. 9 Melrose while the Whalers travel to No. 19 Case on Friday.

Players of the Week

Lauren Flynn, Hamilton-Wenham — Flynn helped the No. 17 Generals stay undefeated with 27 kills and 27 digs in a 3-2 win over Lynnfield.

Julia Johnston, Winchester — The No. 18 Sachems stayed undefeated with a 2-0 week thanks to a combined 56 assists and 12 aces from their senior setter.

Alanna MacDonald, Natick— The senior captain recorded 13 kills and seven blocks as the No. 13 Redhawks came back from a 2-0 deficit to best Wellesley in five sets.

Alexis Mancha, Andover — In a 2-0 week for the No. 5 Golden Warriors, the junior served up three aces in the fifth set to preserve a win over Central Catholic before tallying 11 kills and 20 assists in a sweep of Haverhill.

Ava Tang, Wayland —The sophomore setter had 30 assists, four aces and two blocks as Wayland recorded its first win since 2015.

Games of the Week

No. 12 Bourne at No. 19 Case, Monday (6 p.m.) — The Canalmen (10-0) shocked the defending D3 state champion Cardinals (5-2) on Sept. 18 with a 3-0 sweep and look to prove their undefeated start is for real.

No. 6 Hopkinton at No. 4 Medfield, Monday (6:30 p.m.) — The Hillers (7-2) look to avenge a season-opening loss to a Warriors squad that has dropped two straight matches.

Westborough at No. 3 Duxbury, Tuesday (6 p.m.) — The 6-0 Dragons host one of the top teams in the west in the Mid-Wach-leading Rangers (7-0).

No. 18 Winchester at Woburn, Tuesday (5:30 p.m.) — The undefeated Sachems (8-0) take on a one-loss Woburn squad whose only loss came against No. 9 Melrose.

No. 2 Newton North at No. 20 Wellesley, Thursday (4 p.m.) —The Tigers (6-0) look to distance themselves from the pack in the Bay State, while the Raiders look to right the ship after a tough loss to No. 13 Natick.
