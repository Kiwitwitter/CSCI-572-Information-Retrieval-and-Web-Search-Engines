By Sean Smyth Globe Staff  May 31, 2017

President Trump caught everyone’s attention early Wednesday with a rather curious tweet.

One could assume that Trump was meaning to tweet about “press coverage,” but . . .

There’s also debate on how to pronounce covfefe.

What is the correct pronunciation of #covfefe?

How the dusty Merriam-Webster dictionary reinvented itself. Bigly.

From their Springfield headquarters, a team of lexicographers is defending the honor of the English language in an era of “alternative facts.”

Trump’s misspelling popped up hours before the National Spelling Bee started a few miles from the White House, in National Harbor, Md.

At spelling bee...
Judge: The word is #covfefe
Contestant: Can you use it in a sentence?
Judge: Despite the constant negative press covfefe

Trump’s tweet brought to mind late-night tweets from former US Senator Scott Brown in January 2013. The #bqhatevwr hashtag still gets some run on Twitter.

At around 6 a.m., Trump deleted the tweet and sent a replacement.

“Who can figure out the true meaning of “covfefe” ??? Enjoy!” the president wrote on Twitter.
