By Rob DeCola Globe Staff  May 13, 2017

For the seventh straight year, locals took their porches, driveways, and yards to serenade fellow residents at the Somerville Arts Council’s annual Porchfest event.

The overcast weather didn’t seem to deter the crowds, who stood on sidewalks and streets to watch performances from a wide variety of genres.

Advertisement

The event has been a continued success in Somerville since resident Nancy Goodman partnered with the Arts Council in 2011 to bring the festival to life. Goodman had been inspired after attending a similar event in New York.

During the daylong festival, acts stagger their performances in three different sections of the city. Music started at noon in the Eastern part of the city and lasts until 6 p.m. in the Western part.

John Tlumacki/Globe Staff

Emily Jawadekar, of Brighton, was swept off her feet by Clayton Jennings, of Somerville, as they danced to The Humdingers! set during Somerville’s Porchfest.

John Tlumacki/Globe Staff

Audience members took it easy curbside while listening to a set from SheBoom.
