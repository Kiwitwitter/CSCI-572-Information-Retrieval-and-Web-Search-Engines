HIGH SCHOOL FOOTBALL NOTEBOOK

Former backups thriving in new roles as starting quarterbacks

Jim Venable

Seven previously undefeated teams were shown the Exit from The Bus this week.

By Henry Brechter Globe Correspondent  October 08, 2017

At the heart of most successful high school football programs is a strong quarterback, someone with great leadership and on-field performance who can be counted upon in every situation.

So when such a quarterback graduates, there can be great uncertainty about his replacement.

Several great quarterbacks in Eastern Mass. moved on to play college football following high school Super Bowl wins last season, such as Everett’s Jordan McAfee and Duxbury’s Bobby Maimaron. But their successors, among others, have answered the call in a big way.

The Crimson (4-0), the Globe’s top-ranked team in the state, have barely missed a beat thanks in large part to new quarterback Jake Willcox.

The junior has tossed 11 touchdowns in four games, helping lead Everett past tough opponents like Xaverian and St. John’s Prep. His trust in receivers Mike Sainristil and Jason Maitre — each have caught two touchdowns thus far — has helped settle an offense seeking a true identity entering the season.

“Going into the first game, I really wanted to limit my mistakes,” Willcox said. “I have a lot of great players around me, so I knew we would be fine if I set them up. At this point, the only team that can beat us is ourselves.”

Taking over for Maimaron, the son of the head coach, is a challenging feat. But new signal-caller John Roberts has done just that, and then some.

After throwing two late interceptions in a season-opening loss to Bridgewater-Raynham, the junior has found his stride.

Since Week 1, Roberts has thrown 13 touchdowns and run for three more, helping the Dragons (4-1) outscore their last four opponents, 154-22.

Another defending Super Bowl champion, Mashpee, had four-year starter and two-time state champion Nick Carpenter graduate following last season, giving way to senior Jacob Johnston.

Falcons head coach Matt Triveri credits Johnston for thriving in his new role as one of the team’s leaders.

“The kids really believe in him, 100 percent,” said Triveri. “They have the utmost confidence in Jake — he’s a foxhole guy. The kids will go wherever he tells them to go.”

The Falcons’ (5-0) offense has soared with Johnston at the helm, scoring at least five touchdowns in all five of their games so far. The senior has tossed 10 touchdowns and added two more on the ground this season.

“I really like being the guy to go to right now,” said Johnston. “I know I’m the focal point of the offense right now, but I couldn’t do any of it without the offensive line and all the weapons we have at wide receiver.”

Scituate lost Peter Churchill (29 career TD passes) to graduation after last year, but junior Aidan Sullivan has picked up right where his predecessor left off.

Sullivan and the Sailors (4-1) aren’t exactly blowing teams away, scoring 23.8 points per game. But head coach Herb Devine says Sullivan’s smart decision-making has been key to the team’s success.

“The way we run our offense, a lot of decisions are made by the quarterback,” said Devine. “Aidan has picked it up so quickly, his football IQ is extremely high, his accuracy is extremely high, and I think with each week he’s just getting better and better.”

“The coaches have really put me in a position to succeed,” said Sullivan, who has passed for more than 700 yards and eight touchdowns in five games for the Sailors. “I just have the mentality that I have to go out and play my best, and let the game come to me.”

Coyle & Cassidy ends drought

Seven long seasons have come and gone without Coyle & Cassidy notching a victory in league play.

That drought finally came to an end Friday night, as the Warriors (3-2, 1-0 EAC) outlasted Bishop Stang for a 6-0 win in overtime to capture their first win in the Eastern Athletic Conference since 2009.

“It felt pretty nice, to be the team that finally got that league win,” said Coyle senior Ben Vaz. “The emotions were crazy, even the coaches went crazy, and the bus ride home was a good one.”

When the Spartans (1-4, 0-1 EAC) threw an interception to begin overtime, the Warriors jumped at the opportunity.

Senior tailback Jacob Cabral scampered for a 10-yard touchdown to end the game and, at long last, give Coyle a win over a league opponent.

“The kids knew how much this win would mean to the team,” said Warriors coach Chris Pabst. “For these kids that are seniors and have been in the program for four years, their first time beating a league opponent, they were on Cloud Nine last night.”

Norton triumphs in OT

Down three touchdowns at halftime is just about the last place a team wants to be.

But Norton proved it was up to the challenge, scoring 21 points in the final two quarters and sealing a 29-27 road win over Norwood with an overtime touchdown.

“Norwood was running a really aggressive defense, sending someone in the A-gap almost every play, so we tried to exploit that in the second half,” said Lancers assistant coach Andy Gomes. “The kids did a good job bouncing back and not getting down on themselves.”

Lancers senior receiver McKinley Kearsley was unstoppable after halftime, making touchdown catches of 68 and 64 yards in the fourth quarter to tie the game, 21-21.

After Norwood scored on the opening drive in overtime, but failed on a 2-point attempt, Norton quarterback Tyler Elliot tied the game with a 2-yard touchdown run. Then, it was McKinley again making a play, this time on the 2-point conversion to break the tie.

“It really wasn’t even supposed to come to me,” said McKinley of the final play. “I saw a little window and saw Tyler’s eyes, and he just threw it in the right position . . . Altogether I thought it was a great team win, I couldn’t just place the whole win on me.”
