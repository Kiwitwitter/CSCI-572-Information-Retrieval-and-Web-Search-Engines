Players of the Year

Division 1: Brooke Troy, Westwood

Troy had 66 goals and 86 points for the Division 1 runner-up Wolverines this season, garnering a spot on the Under Armor All-American team. The Tri-Valley League MVP will play lacrosse at Boston College.

Division 2: Stephanie Croke, Norwell

The South Shore League Player of the Year had 80 goals and 25 assists in her junior season, leading the Clippers to their second Division 2 title in three years. She will captain the soccer and basketball teams her senior year.

All-Scholastics

Charlotte Allard, Notre Dame (Hingham), junior

Advertisement

One of the country’s top juniors, Allard scored 86 goals for the Cougars, the Division 1 South runner-up. She totaled 118 points this season, raising her career total to 333. She garnered All-American honors for the second time.

Katie Belval, Needham, senior

Belval had 37 goals and 32 assists this season. The Bay State League MVP finished her career with 151 points. Belval is a member of school leadership program and was a standout in soccer and ice hockey.

Amanda Burns, Billerica, junior

The co-MVP of the Merrimack Valley Conference was a force at midfield, controlling 96 draws and collecting 84 ground balls. She recorded 84 points while leading the Indians to the Division 1 North final.

Leah Cardarelli, Acton-Boxboro, senior

Cardarelli scored 37 goals and assisted on 39 others for the Colonials, while racking up 66 ground balls. An All-Scholastic in field hockey and ice hockey, Cardarelli was named the Globe’s Athlete of the Year.

Leah Chittick, North Andover, senior

The co-MVP of the Merrimack Valley Conference had 70 goals and 39 assists in leading the Scarlet Knights to their second consecutive Division 1 North championship. Chittick will play lacrosse at UMass.

Kailey Conry, Needham, junior

Advertisement

Conry led the team in scoring with 70 goals and 93 assists. Conry is a three-time Bay State All-Star and is committed to play lacrosse at BU. Conry plays field hockey and basketball and will captain the field hockey team in the fall.

Hanna Cooper, Notre Dame (Hingham), senior

Cooper held opponents to 6.5 goals per game while saving 170 shots this season. As a sophomore, she backstopped the Cougars to their only state title. Cooper, a member of the National Honor Society, will play lacrosse at Navy.

Hannah Dudley, Concord-Carlisle, junior

The midfielder scored 60 goals and 82 points to go along with 111 draw controls, leading the Patriots to the Division 1 East title. Dudley was named a first-team All-American and is verbally committed to Stanford.

Emily Jeffries, Franklin, senior

The Hockomock League MVP recorded 63 goals and 86 points while controlling 69 draws for the Panthers, who made the Division 1 East semifinals. Jeffries finished her career with 200 points and will play lacrosse at UMass.

Keeley MacAfee, Notre Dame (Hingham), senior

The midfielder scored 64 goals and added 28 assists to wrap up her career with 306 points. A two-year captain for the Cougars, she cotrolled 58 draws this season. A 2014 academic All-American, she will play lacrosse at Harvard.

Emma McDonagh, Reading, senior

McDonagh led the Rockets with 97 goals and 35 assists to finish her career with 356 points. She also controlled 98 draws while causing 39 turnovers. McDonagh also captained the Reading ice hockey team that won the state title.

Andrea Meincke, Westwood, junior

The UMass commit had 56 goals, 27 assists, and 67 ground balls this season. Meincke is also a Tri-Valley League All-Star in basketball and will captain the team next winter. She is a member of the National Honor Society.

Taylor Pinzone, Waltham, sophomore

With 331 points through two seasons, Pinzone is already Waltham’s all-time leading scorer. She added 141 goals and a state-high 186 points this spring. Pinzone was also a DCL All-Star in cross-country and basketball.

Jillian Reilly, Westwood, junior

Reilly had 30 goals and 12 assists while collecting 25 ground balls. She was a Tri-Valley League All-Star and will captain the soccer team in the fall. The National Honor Society member is committed to Boston College for lacrosse.

Caroline Walsh, Norwell, senior

Walsh came on strong in the postseason, scoring 25 of Norwell’s 77 goals en route to a state title. Walsh was also a SSL All-Star in basketball and is a member of the National Honor Society. She will play lacrosse at Holy Cross.

KC Williams, Duxbury, junior

The Patriot League MVP scored 90 goals and assisted on 34 others. The midfielder is a member of the National Honor Society and plays soccer and ice hockey for the Dragons. In the summer she plays Mass Elite lacrosse.

Coaches of the Year

Division 1: Ashley Martell, Billerica

Martell led the Indians to their first Merrimack Valley title since 2004, finishing as the runner-up in the Division 1 North tournament. She credits assistant Brittany Whiteway for much of the team’s success.

Division 2: Kerri O’Connor, Ipswich

The 2015 Cape Ann League Coach of the Year led the Tigers to the Division 2 North crown, the first sectional title in school history. O’Connor is a graduate of Gordon College, where she helped start the women’s lacrosse program.
