Five theories on who will kill whom on ‘Big Little Lies’

Hilary Bronwyn Gayle//HBO

Will Perry (Alexander Skarsgard) kill Celeste (Nicole Kidman)? Vice versa?

By Matthew Gilbert Globe Staff  March 31, 2017

One of the great things about “Big Little Lies” — and there are a number of great things about the HBO mystery series, not least of all its orgasmic Monterey, Calif., real estate — is that it’s a who-got-dun as well as a whodunit.

As Sunday’s finale approaches, we know that A.) someone is a killer, B.) someone is dead, and C.) a number of the characters could easily be either one of those someones. The script, written masterfully by David E. Kelley, has set up many folks in the Monterey community as killer or victim; the show is lousy with motivations.

Here are a few of the possibilities. By the way, I haven’t read Liane Moriarty’s novel (or read the Wikipedia page), so I don’t know anything about where the story is heading. Also, I haven’t included Renata (Laura Dern) in these speculations because, as the prime mover in all the big drama, she’s too obvious as either killer or victim. Also, her reparations with Jane (Shailene Woodley) were just too touching, reminding us that ultimately the drama is about the bonds between these women, not their battles with one another.

1. Ed (Adam Scott) kills his wife, Madeline (Reese Witherspoon). Ed has been portrayed as a sensitive man who may not have the passionate streak that Madeline desires. Maybe he’ll show her he’s a real man once and for all, when he finds out she had an affair with “Avenue Q” director Joseph. Madeline and Joseph were in a car accident together, and if Ed puts the pieces together he just may figure it all out. Sub-theory: Ed directs his anger at Joseph, and kills him with a rod puppet.

2. Perry (Alexander Skarsgard) kills Celeste (Nicole Kidman). The miniseries is at its tensest when Perry is onscreen. Behind his beautiful blue eyes, he is a monster who beats his wife. Whenever she says or does anything independently, he goes crazy on her. Perhaps he finds out she’s leaving him in the finale, and counters her biggest move yet toward independence with his biggest move to stop her: murder. Sub-theory: Celeste kills Perry, after all her suppressed fury finally fully surfaces.

3. Madeline kills her ex-husband, Nathan (James Tupper). Madeline is a tinderbox of sorts, feeling anger and disappointment with three important men in her life – Ed, Joseph, and Nathan. It’s pretty clear to everyone, not least of all Ed, that she is still caught up in too-strong feelings about her ex, and news of their teen daughter’s virginity auction hasn’t helped. Maybe she takes him down once and for all? Sub-theory: Ed kills Nathan, out of a warped sense of loyalty to Madeline. The two men have always been tense with each other, as their bicycle confrontation proved.

4. Madeline and/or Jane kills Perry. They’re both extremely close to Celeste. What if Celeste tells them about being abused by Perry, since the therapist advised her to, and what if they decide to solve the problem together for Celeste? Sub-theory: There’s an idea floating around online that Perry is in fact Jane’s rapist. What if Jane fits the pieces together and kills him, solving two, two, two problems at the same time?

5. The Pacific Ocean kills all the adults. All along, “Big Little Lies” has been showing us gorgeous images of the tide — a tide that is filled with wrath and power. “Oh, you’re all rich and pretty,” the tide seems to be yelling as it crashes outside their spectacular windows, “but money and looks can’t protect you from my natural dominance, bwahahahaha.” Maybe the ocean is tired of whiny wealthy people and decides to swallow them whole? Sub-theory: Maybe the ocean just decides to take Perry, to surgically remove the malignancy from this particular group of parents.
