HIGH SCHOOL FOOTBALL

MIAA set to stage six Super Bowls at Gillette Stadium

By Craig Larson Globe Staff  September 29, 2017

In a quick-hitting, 1-hour-5-minute meeting Thursday morning in Franklin — one devoid of realignment discussions/gripes with the 2017-21 realignment set for now — the MIAA Football Committee confirmed six Super Bowls (Divisions 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, and 8) will be played at Gillette Stadium on Saturday, Dec. 2.

The Division 2 and Division 7 bowls will played at sites yet to be finalized. It followed an annual divisional rotation started last year in which the Division 1 (Everett-Xaverian in Lynn) and Division 4A/8 games (Maynard-Millis at Worcester State) were played at other sites.

Gillette can only accommodate six games in a single day.

“We’re trying to find the best sites,” said Richard Pearson, associate executive director of the MIAA.

The state semifinal sites for the Central (Doyle Field, Leominster) and West (Westfield State) brackets are booked, but the North and South locations are a work in progress. A few sites have become cost prohibitive, according to North tourney director Barry Haley, the athletic director at Concord-Carlisle.

“And we have to put schools in places that logistically make sense,” Haley said, adding it did not make sense for a Boston City League team to travel to Lowell for a semifinal.

In a related matter concerning the scheduling of non-playoff games in Weeks 8-10, the committee approved unanimously to release the schedule on a week-to-week basis, rather than a three-week block.

“It sets up better rivalry games,” said Haley. That will apply to North, South, Central, and West games.

 ■ The North and South tournament seeding meetings will be held Sunday, Oct. 22, in Franklin. The Central/West seedings are scheduled for Oct. 29.

■ Representing the state coaches association, Milton coach Steve Dembowski asked the committee to consider two changes to the preseason schedule.

On first two days of conditioning, he proposed coaches be allowed to use a blocking pad to teach proper techniques. “It’s a teaching mechanism,” he said. “We want to educate the players better about blocking and tackling.”

Also, he asked for flexibility regarding the scheduling of the first scrimmage on Day 8. This year, that date (Saturday, Aug. 26) conflicted with SAT exams. It was proposed to move the scrimmage to the previous night, in the event of such a conflict.

Pearson wanted to include input from the MIAA’s sports medicine committee on the matter when they meet in mid-October. He asked the coaches association to make a more formal presentation at the Football Committee meeting on Feb. 14, 2018. If the changes are approved, it could be voted in by June.

■ Maynard, the Division 4A runnerup in 2016 which co-ops with the Advanced Math and Science Academy Charter in Marlborough, has been moved up to Division 6 from D8 because AMSA is now the larger school of the two.

■ Grafton High principal Jim Pignataro was unanimously approved to continue as committee chair, with St. John’s Prep AD Jim O’Leary returning to his seat as vice chair. Weymouth AD Kevin Mackin was voted in as secretary.

Assistant director Peter Smith will step away from the Football Committee to focus solely on managing and overseeing the association’s Unified Sports initiative.
