CAMPUS ANGLE

Kaba scoring on and off the pitch

Jim Pierce

Norwood’s Adama broke the soccer scoring ice for Northeastern University.

By John Johnson Globe Correspondent  September 16, 2017

Adama Kaba picked an ideal time to score his first collegiate goal for the Northeastern University men’s soccer team. The Huskies were shut out in their first three games, all 1-0 losses. Kaba’s tally not only ended NU’s offensive drought, but propelled the Huskies to a 2-1 upset victory over No. 12 UMass-Lowell.

Kaba, a freshman who moved to Norwood four years ago, tapped in a left-to-right cross from Harry Swartz of Needham at the right goal post.

Kaba graduated from Saint Sebastian’s School, where he scored 24 career goals along with 10 assists as a midfielder and forward. He was a two-time All Independent School League first team selection, and earned Massachusetts All-State honors in 2015.

Q. Your first collegiate goal was a big one. Can you describe the circumstances around the goal?

A. Harry Swartz, who is one of our best players, dribbled down toward the sideline and crossed the ball in. I just chased after it, and I was at the right place at the right time. It was an easy finish.

Q. How did it feel to score your first collegiate goal?

A. It felt good, and I felt proud for the team after scoring, but ultimately I just wanted to get that first win so we could build some momentum. Before the season I was a little anxious to get that first goal, so it helped me to feel more relaxed playing at this high level.

Q. What has been the biggest adjustment to playing Division 1 soccer?

A. I think the biggest thing is the mental part and going into each game confident. And of course the game is faster, so the speed is very different, and the tactical stuff is much more important than in high school.

Q. How did you end up at NU?

A. Originally I had interest in Dartmouth because of former Dartmouth coach Chris Gbandi, who is the head coach at NU now. I was interested in his coaching because he is from Liberia, which is where my parents are from. My mother [Geraldine] was a refugee, and she left the country because of the civil war in 1992. I knew that coach Gbandi, with his experience as a player and a coach, could help me develop mentally and physically. I’m also a city kid and so NU is also close to home.

Q. What is your strongest on-field asset that you bring to NU?

Advertisement

A. I think my biggest attributes are my communication, my athleticism, and my speed, as well as off ball movement toward the net. Obviously, I always want to improve to get to the next level.

Q. Where do you see yourself in five years?

A. My ultimate goal is to play professional soccer in Europe, but I need to continue to improve to get to that level. It will take more practice, more time spent on film, more time in the gym, and just being prepared to play with guys at that level. That’s the goal, but whatever happens happens.

Q. You belonged to Men with Positive Attitudes in high school. What was your affiliation with the group?

A. MPA is a diversity club where kids from different races and backgrounds get together to talk about social issues from around the world and get to know different cultures. As a sophomore I was a coordinator, so I really learned how to use leadership skills. I was president during my senior year and in charge of the whole group, helping younger kids develop their social skills and leadership skills. The experience helped to make me a better person on and off the soccer field.

Q. What do you like to do in your spare time?

A. I like to watch Netflix and HBO. I like watching “Friends,’’ that’s always a go-to, and “That 70s Show’’ is really funny. I also like “The Real Husbands of Hollywood’ with Kevin Hart.

Q. Do you have any pre-game rituals?

A. I like to pray before the game, and have the bible app so I usually scroll through that. I also listen to a lot of music, and Kendrick Lamar is my favorite artist, and maybe some A Boogie and Future Drake. Then I just try to talk to my teammates and get the social scene correct before the game.

Q. Favorite place around campus to get a bite to eat?

A. As a freshman I don’t go out that much, but the nearest one I like is Amelia’s Taqueria on Huntington Ave. I usually get a burrito with chicken, guacamole, refried beans, lettuce and Mexican rice.
