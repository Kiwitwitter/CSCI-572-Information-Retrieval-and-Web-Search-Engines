Torey Krug should be ready to play in Bruins’ game vs. Colorado

Charles Krupa/AP

Torey Krug was injured in a preseason game on Sept. 19.

By Fluto Shinzawa Globe Staff  October 08, 2017

Torey Krug made it through the Bruins’ entire practice on Sunday at Warrior Ice Arena, practicing alongside usual defense partner Adam McQuaid. Krug concluded the session with power-play work in his usual spot atop the first unit. All these signs point toward the defenseman making his regular-season debut on Monday against Colorado after missing the opener because of his fractured jaw. 

Patrice Bergeron (lower body) skated on his own before practice. Bergeron participated in part of practice in a non-contact jersey, but left before its conclusion, indicating he will not be in uniform for a second straight game.

Ryan Spooner took Bergeron’s five-on-five practice shifts between Brad Marchand and David Pastrnak. 

With Krug back, Matt Grzelcyk should be the odd man out. Grzelcyk played well against Nashville in the season-opener on Thursday, paired with McQuaid and working the No. 2 power-play unit.

Charlie McAvoy practiced on the No. 2 man-up unit. McAvoy was joined by four left-shot wingers: Frank Vatrano, Matt Beleskey, Anders Bjork, and Jake DeBrusk. It’s not an ideal setup for faceoffs without a center and no right shots. But the Bruins are down Bergeron, David Backes, Austin Czarnik, and Noel Acciari, all right shots.

Coach Bruce Cassidy explained that Riley Nash is an option, but that his priority is penalty killing.
