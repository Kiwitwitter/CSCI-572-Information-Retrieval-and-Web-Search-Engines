HIGH SCHOOL CROSS-COUNTRY NOTEBOOK

Milton chasing a third straight girls’ cross-country crown

By Shawn McFarland Globe Correspondent  September 29, 2017

Milton girls cross-country coach Tom Shaw knows the rarity of the situation his Wildcats are in.

He knows the privilege of having the defending Division 2 individual state champion back to lead the squad for another year. He understands that most teams don’t return three runners who placed in the top 10 at last year’s All-State meet.

Most importantly, he realizes that most programs going for a third consecutive state title usually aren’t fielding the strongest roster they’ve ever had.

But Milton has all three of those things, and that’s why expectations are as high as they’ve ever been for the Wildcats.

“Our expectations are to win states again and to qualify for nationals,” Shaw said. “Our expectations are the same goals as last year. We have the team that’s good enough to meet those goals. We’ve just got to perform and come to races healthy.”

It starts with depth for Milton, a school which boasts arguably the deepest roster in the state. Led by Bridget Mitchell, who placed first at the Division 2 All-State meet last year, the Wildcats realistically have three girls who would be the best runner on the majority of teams in the state. Juniors Elise O’Leary and Ella Affanato finished seventh and ninth, respectively, at All-States. The only core piece that Milton graduated from last year’s title squad is Colette O’Leary, who placed 19th.

“We have to keep the same mentality we had last year, and not let it get to our heads that we won a title,” Mitchell said. “It’s going to take a lot of hard work still, but we have a bunch of girls willing to work hard for it.”

A deep lineup helps Milton in a litany of ways. As the Wildcats showed last fall, they can place a trio of runners in the top 10 at states. But during the regular season, it’s more about who doesn’t run, as opposed to who does run.

“You don’t want to have to race your top runners at every single meet if you can avoid it,” Shaw said. “These are 3-mile races, and at the end of the season, 10 5K races, your body is at some point going to need a break. We can provide those breaks with our depth throughout the season. We did that last year. There will be injuries, and you need that depth to fill in those holes.”

It helps take some of the pressure off of Mitchell, too. Most teams are reliant on their top runner to rack up points at regular-season and postseason meets. But in Milton’s case, the fate of the team doesn’t necessarily rest on Mitchell’s shoulders.

With that being said, Mitchell understands her role well. As a senior and a defending state champion, her final season will emphasize going out a champion once again, but also priming the team to be just as successful once she departs.

“When I was younger on the team, I always looked up to the older girls, and knew what they did was really special,” Mitchell said. “I wanted to take over and make sure that legacy keeps going. The juniors right now — there’s a really good group of them — they’re already acting like leaders.”

Added Shaw, “[Mitchell] is critical. She’s had good performances in big meets, and she’s had rough performances in big meets. She’s seen it all and done it all. While my top four, or top five runners are pretty seasoned athletes in cross country, the rest of the team are rookies. If we want this tradition to continue, we want someone like Bridget leading the way. She’s certainly our spiritual leader. When she’s around, people know it.”

As part of the MIAA’s realignment, Milton will compete in the Division 1 All-State meet this season (and D3 at the divisional meet). But Shaw said the team’s first goal is to win the Bay State conference, which is home to other top-tier programs at Wellesley, Walpole, Newton North, and Natick.

While the big invitationals don’t truly kick off until October and November, the Wildcats are seeing All-State level talent in their conference each week, and it’s only going to sharpen their mentality for the postseason.

“It’s really awesome that our conference is so competitive, and every week is something different,” Mitchell said. “Dual meets are what gives us our edge. During those dual meets, everybody just focuses on the team, and it gets you ready for those tough times at states.”

Going the distance

■ Brookline’s Lucas Aramburu is making a case to be the top boys runner in the state. As a sophomore last fall, Aramburu finished third at the Division 1 All-State meet in Gardner, and tied St. John’s Prep’s Tristan Shelgren in time at 15 minutes, 59 seconds. Last weekend at the Amherst Invitational, Aramburu claimed first place with a time of 15:43

■ Breaking into the top three boys’ teams in the state will be a tall task for any program, as Wellesley, St. John’s (Shrewsbury), and Lowell have a tight grip atop those rankings. But as Lowell coach Scott Ouelett noted, Newton North may have the best odds to break into the top three. Led by senior Andrew Mah, who finished 10th at last year’s Division 1 All-State meet, the Tigers could continue to make noise as the season goes along.

Runners of the Week

Tristan Shelgren, St. John’s Prep: Coming off a cross-country season in which he finished second at the All-State meet behind Peabody’s Marcelo Rocha, the senior already has the state’s top mark of the fall of 15 minutes, 32 seconds, set at the Ocean State Invitational in Warwick, R.I. on Sep. 23.

Lucas Aramburu, Brookline: In a last-mile burst to the front of the pack, the Warriors’ junior took first at the Amherst invitational on Sunday with a time of 15:43.

Zander Kessler, Concord-Carlisle: The senior was at the head of the Patriots’ pack at the Amherst Invitational, leading his group to the team title. His finished second overall in 15:51.

Thomas Weiss, Wellesley: The senior crossed the finished line in 16:22 at the Ocean State Invitational and led the Raiders to a 32-point victory.

Cameron McLeod, Needham: After missing most of last season due to injury, the junior is back and poised to be one of the state’s best. He finished third at the Amherst Invitational (15:53).

Meets to watch

Frank Kelley Invitational, Wrentham, Saturday: The Frank Kelley Invitational is traditionally the first large invitational of the season. Wellesley, Newton North, St. John’s Prep and a host of other top programs will be in attendance.

St. John’s Prep at Malden Catholic (at Franklin Park), Tuesday, Oct. 3: St. John’s Prep senior Tristan Shelgren is off to an electric start to the season, and has placed first in his first three races of the season.

Lexington at Belmont, Tuesday, Oct. 3: Minutemen senior Thomas Lingard placed seventh at last year’s All-State meet, and figures to be a top-five finisher come this November.

Mansfield at King Philip, Tuesday, Oct. 3: Led by senior Kalin Petrov and junior Patrick Heavey, Mansfield entered the season at No. 7 in the MSTCA coaches poll.

Newton North at Weymouth, Wednesday, Oct. 4: Newton North senior Andrew Mah and his Tigers continue their march toward a potential state title as they hit the road to take on Weymouth.
