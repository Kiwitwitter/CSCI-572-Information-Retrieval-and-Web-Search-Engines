HARVARD 41, GEORGETOWN 2

Harvard drops hammer on Georgetown

By David Driver Globe Correspondent  September 30, 2017

WASHINGTON — Harvard junior Justice Shelton-Mosley said that almost anyone could have run through the gap he saw in the first quarter Saturday.

“That was a big, big hole that I had,” said the California product.

Advertisement

Shelton-Mosley broke a punt return record that had been around nearly 70 years and the Crimson dominated nonconference host Georgetown, 41-2, on a cloudy, windy Saturday afternoon at RFK Stadium, the former home of the NFL’s Redskins. It was the first time Georgetown, located just a few miles west, hosted a football game at RFK Stadium and it may be the last as the Hoyas were totally outclassed.

“Anytime you win 41-2 on the road you have to be pretty happy,” said Harvard coach Tim Murphy. “We still have a lot of work to do offensively. We just have to be more consistent. Special teams was kind of good and bad. We gave up too much field position on returns. But it is great to get a win on the road.”

Shelton-Mosley set a school record when he returned a punt 91 yards for a touchdown to give Harvard a 6-0 lead with 12:38 left in the first quarter. That broke a mark that had stood since 1948 when Hal Moffie went 89 yards against Holy Cross.

“It was great that got done today. We had been talking about that as a unit this past week,” Shelton-Mosley said of trying to be the best punt return unit in the country.

The Hoyas drove to the Harvard 25 on the next drive before defensive tackle Stone Hart forced a fumble that was recovered by defensive tackle Ritchie Ryan.

Harvard took advantage and marched down the field, as Charlie Booker III went 11 yards for a touchdown and a 14-0 lead with 5:56 left in the first quarter.

Georgetown quarterback Clay Norris forced a pass and Raishaun McGhee took advantage with a 23-yard interception return as Harvard took a 21-0 lead with 14:32 left in the first half. It was the second week in a row the Crimson returned an interception for a touchdown.

“We knew coming in they had a good passing attack,” Harvard senior captain and linebacker Luke Hutton said. “Our defensive backs did an incredible job. Our defensive line is doing a fantastic job the past two weeks. And finally zero points given up by the defense. We are not where we want to be. There is room for improvement.”

The Hoyas got on the board with a safety on bad snap by Harvard that went out of the end zone to make it 24-2 with 1:36 left in the first half.

Booker went over from 1 yard out with 28 seconds left in the first half as Harvard led, 31-2.

Harvard freshman Jake Smith started at quarterback for the second week in a row and completed 11 of 21 passes for 139 yards in the first three quarters.

The Crimson made it 34-2 midway through the third with a second field goal by Jake McIntyre. The Crimson forced four turnovers in the first half.

Harvard senior Joe Viviano, who started the first game of the season at quarterback, entered the game late in the first half but Smith started the third quarter. Viviano was 5-for-10 passing for 66 yards and a touchdown.

“We are still a work in progress,” Murphy said of his quarterbacks. “We have two good quarterbacks. I look at the glass as half full. It is not perfect. We will figure it out.”
