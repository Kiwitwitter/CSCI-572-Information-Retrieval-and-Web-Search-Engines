Here’s what players were saying about John Farrell after Game 4

Jim Davis/Globe Staff

After being thrown out of Game 4, Red Sox manager John Farrell continued his argument with first base umpire Mike Everitt.

By Alex Speier Globe Staff  October 09, 2017

While John Farrell became the first manager in Red Sox history to steward his club to back-to-back American League East titles as well as a total of three division crowns, his team’s back-to-back exits in the Division Series round of the playoffs raise make his future in that job unclear.

In the aftermath of the Red Sox’ season-ending 5-4 loss to the Astros in Game 4 of the ALDS, some uniform personnel were asked to take stock of the job he did and whether he should remain at the helm of the team going forward. Here are some reactions:

Farrell

Advertisement

On what he feels the team needs to get past the Division Series and whether he is confident that he can lead it to that point: “Personally, yes, I feel confident in that. I know that we have got an opportunity to assess where we are as a team. I can’t begin to talk about what the offseason plans are and what the changes may be realized, but still there’s a lot of good things that are going on here.”

On whether he expects to be back next year: “Again, we just walked off the field 10 minutes ago.”

Dustin Pedroia

On whether Farrell should be back next year: “I thought John did a great job. We won the division. There was never any quit in this team. I’m proud of everybody in here. We dealt with a lot. Our fight continued every single day. I know we didn’t achieve our goals, but I’m proud of how everybody went about their business and showed up for everybody and played to win.”

Craig Kimbrel

“He was there for us. He came out and we just got done losing a hard game and he came out and told us how proud he was of us and everything that we did this year. He watched us all year long, he knows each and every guy in here, he knows what we’re made of and for him to go out every night, put it together and try to help put the best team on the field — we won the East, unfortunately we didn’t get where we want in the playoffs. But I think he did a great job.”

Chris Sale

“This is a game that you can second-guess anything. Every game, every inning. We play 162 games, nine innings every time, sometimes extras, so there’s a lot of room for debate and would’ves, should’ves. He gave us every opportunity to succeed. I can say the same for everybody on this team.”
