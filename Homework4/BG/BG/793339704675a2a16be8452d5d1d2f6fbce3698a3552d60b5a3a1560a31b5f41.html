Protesting SNL will only help Donald Trump

By Marcela García   October 28, 2015

It’s been four and a half months since Donald Trump launched his presidential candidacy with insulting remarks about Mexican immigrants. Now, The Donald is scheduled to host NBC’s “Saturday Night Live” on Nov. 7. And Latinos find themselves caught in the classic Trump Paradox: Protest, and feed energy to the Trump campaign, or passively accept the ongoing elevation of a race-baiting blowhard.

For NBC, the Trump appearance is a not-so-shocking reversal. In late June, the network announced it would end its business relationship with Trump because “respect and dignity for all people are cornerstones of our values.” But Trump impossibly evolved into the front-runner — and ratings gold. He would be the first to tell you: Trump sells.

The Hispanic community has forcefully denounced the move. A significant coalition of Latino nonprofits, media, and advocacy organizations launched an online campaign called #RacismIsntFunny, as well as petitions asking NBC to rescind its invitation. Congressman Luis Gutiérrez of Illinois took to the House floor to demand that SNL “dump Trump.”

For many Latinos, failure to take a stand is not an option, because Trump’s malicious speech sends legitimizing signals, telling supporters that it’s okay to be hateful. Last week, at a campaign stop in Miami, a Trump supporter attacked a Latino protester. At another event in Virginia earlier this month, a Trump proponent spat and cursed at a small group of immigration activists.

But these protests are playing right into Trump’s hands. His campaign gains strength on controversy, which is why he doubles down on his inflammatory statements. Protesting his SNL gig only provides him and his supporters with another opportunity to deploy his jaded excuse for anti-immigrant rhetoric: that he’s fighting “political correctness.”

Indeed, there are more effective ways to channel anti-Trump efforts. Carlos Diaz Rosillo, a lecturer on government at Harvard University, understands the Latino community’s anger and frustration. But he argues that energy is best spent on voter mobilization efforts.

“The only thing that will help is to fund-raise, get involved in campaigns, and rally behind Trump’s opponents, whether Republicans or Democrats,” says Diaz, who teaches one of Harvard’s most popular courses, “The Road to the White House.”

Advertisement

Diaz is right. Calling for NBC to “dump Trump” is pointless, because it will never happen. Plus, the biggest weakness in Trump’s game may be that he loves attention too much. So it’s better to let the jokes wear thin on their own — and they will. For Latinos, it will be far more fruitful to speak up with their votes.
