SATURDAY’S SCHOOL ROUNDUP

Unbeaten Middleborough football rallies for thrilling win

    Share via e-mail

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Google+
  • LinkedIn
Comments Print
The Boston Globe Tweet Share

By Henry Brechter Globe Correspondent  September 30, 2017

Did it match last year’s 39-33 triple-overtime thriller?

Yes. East Bridgewater and Middleborough can certainly put on a show.

Advertisement

Erasing a 14-0, second-half deficit on Saturday afternoon, host Middleborough surged ahead with 21 straight points to pull out a 28-21 victory at Battis Field.

With 2:02 remaining in regulation, East Bridgewater answered with a tying 54-yard touchdown pass from Liam LeVangie to Ben Ross. On the ensuing kickoff, the ball bounced to Isaiah Cobbs, and on his first touch of the season the sophomore shed several tackles on his way to a 60-yard return for the score.

When the Middleborough defense forced a three-and-out, the unbeaten Sachems (4-0) had pulled out a nail-biting, and yes, thrilling South Shore League Sullivan victory.

Cobbs delivered the game-changing return after heeding a direct message from his coach.

“It’s funny because a few plays before, he had a chance to make a tackle on the 1-yard line, but he slowed up and hesitated,” said Middleborough coach Pat Kingman.

Advertisement

“He’s a sophomore playing varsity football, but I told him, ‘You’ve got to make something happen now.’ ”

The Sachems were led by their senior duo of quarterback Evan Gwozdz (8-of-17 passing, 147 yards) and tailback Jeremy Soule (18 carries, 203 yards).

It was the 5-foot-8, 180-pound Soule who gave Middleborough the lead for the first time, 21-14, running over, and through defenders for a 79-yard scamper with 8:52 left.

“We never had a team come at us like EB did,” said Soule. “It took a lot of mental toughness to adjust and come back at them.”

After 21 scorelss minutes, LeVangie scored on a 4-yard keeper and Anthony Fabrizio tacked on the extra point to hand the Vikings (1-3) a 7-0 lead at halftime.

East Bridgewater junior Evan Dyer then took the second half kickoff 74 yards for a score, Fabrizio booted the PAT, and it was 14-0.

Two plays later, Gwozdz put his first-half woes (one fumble, two interceptions) in the rearview mirror, firing a 74-yard TD pass to a wide-opener Colin O’Brien. And he pulled the Sachems within a point, 14-13, with a 4-yard keeper at the end of the third quarter. The extra point, however, was wide left.

After Soule put Middleborough on top, Gwozdz ran in the 2-point conversion for a 21-14 lead.

LeVangie and EB, the defending Division 6 champion, responded. But it was Middleborough that had the final say.

“They have a great senior crew, and we’re not executing the way we want to,” said East Bridgewater coach Shawn Tarpey. “They made a few more plays than us.”

Nantucket 55, Monomoy 6 — Sophomore Devonte Usher took a punt (55 yards) and a kick return (80 yards) back for TDs, Torane Burnton scored 2 points on an 88-yard return of a blocked PAT, and fullback Alex Small scored three TDs for the Whalers (4-0) in Harwich.

Archbishop Williams 55, Austin Prep 6 — Neil Sanders completed 7 of 8 passes for 160 yards and TDs of 36, 35, and 53 yards to lift the visiting Bishops (2-2).

Brendan O’Brien scored on a 50-yard punt return and a 43-yard run.

Bridgewater-Raynham 13, Chelmsford 0 — Senior Bryce Shaw ran 22 times for 157 yards and a pair of second-half touchdowns for the visiting Trojans (3-1).

Norwell 39, Randolph 14 — Bryce Griffin rushed for TDs of 8 and 25 yards in the second quarter to power the Clippers (4-0) to a 32-6 halftime cushion.

St. John’s Prep 35, Malden Catholic 0 — Aise Pream scored on a 2-yard run and a 26-yard pass from Matt Crowley, who also threw a 32-yard TD pass to Max Freedman for the host Eagles (2-2).

Danvers 36, Malden 2 — Justin Mullaney had three TD passes of 13, 12, and 18 yards, connecting with Zach Dillon on the latter two scoring tosses while John Kelter also scored on a 62-yard interception return for the Falcons (4-0).

Lynn Tech 48, Minuteman 12 — Keoni Gaskin (7 carries, 153 yards, 3 TDs) and Steffan Gravely (6 rushes, 131 yards, 1 TD) teamed up for 284 yards on the ground for the host Tigers (4-0).

Old Colony 28, South Shore Voc-Tech 8 — Jarred Gagne racked up 194 yards on 29 carries, scoring on 64 and 28-yard runs in the first quarter for the visiting Cougars (3-1).

Southeastern 14, Tri-County 0— Kyle Burns (20 carries, 194 yards) scored on 34 and 64-yard runs for the visiting Hawks (3-1).

Lynn English 46, Saugus 22 — Quarterback Matt Severance connected with Prince Brown for two first-quarter touchdowns for the Bulldogs (4-0). Ski Gaston also scored on TD runs of 20 and 10 yards.

Boys’ soccer

Whitman-Hanson 2, Cardinal Spellman 0 — Senior Anthony Pasciuto registered the shutout for the visiting Panthers (3-4).

Girls’ soccer

Pembroke 5, Dennis-Yarmouth 0 — Senior Amanda Schafer netted a pair of goals and assisted on three others for the host Titans.

Hingham 6, Duxbury 0 — Senior Caroline Harkins, junior Kira Maguire, sophomore Phoebe Murphy, and freshman Lilly Reale tallied goals for the third-ranked Harborwomen (6-1-1).

Field hockey

Somerset Berkley 10, New Bedford 0 — Siblings Lucas (3 goals, 1 assist) and Cami (1 goal, 3 assists) Crook carried the eighth-ranked Blue Raiders (8-0) to road victory.
