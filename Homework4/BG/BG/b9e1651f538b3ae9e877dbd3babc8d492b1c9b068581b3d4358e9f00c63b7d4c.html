Bring the kids to Buttonwood; plus, CAVA comes north

    Share via e-mail

  • Facebook
  • Twitter

The Boston Globe Tweet Share

By Kara Baskin Globe correspondent  September 15, 2017

Coming soon: The team behind Newton’s Little Big Diner and Sycamore will open Buttonwood later this fall at the old 51 Lincoln space (51 Lincoln St. at Columbus Street). Owner Dave Punch calls Buttonwood a more laid-back, family-friendly version of Sycamore. “We want people to bring their kids! We have two high chairs at Sycamore, and we’ve only used them twice. I understand it, given everyone’s pedigree, but we want this to be a real neighborhood restaurant that you can eat at every night of the week,” he says. “It’s a little casual offshoot of Sycamore with the same quality ingredients.”

The pedigree at Buttonwood isn’t too shabby, however: The executive chef is Francisco Millan, a former chef at Island Creek Oyster Bar and Row 34.

Advertisement

Punch calls the menu “simple and rustic, hearth-to-table,” enhanced by a smoker. He’ll also add plenty of beer taps. It will serve dinner nightly and, eventually, weekend brunch. As for the name? “It’s another name for a sycamore tree,” Punch says. “A customer told me that years ago, and I filed it away.”

Openings: Eastern Mediterranean restaurant anoush’ella has launched in the South End (35 W. Newton St. at Washington Street). Owners Nina and Raffi Festekjian hail from Beirut and serve Armenian and Lebanese dishes like m’anoush, a thin flatbread with savory toppings like hummus and za’atar. Meanwhile, a second branch of Sabrosa Taqueria has opened at Somerville’s Assembly Row (475 Revolution Drive at Grand Union Boulevard); the original is in the Financial District.

Imports: Washington, D.C.-based Mediterranean sensation Cava plans several upcoming Massachusetts locations. The fast-casual restaurant will open in the Fenway (1350 Boylston St. at Kilmarnock Street) and in the Back Bay (667 Boylston St. at Exeter Street), as well as at Dedham’s Legacy Place and Hingham’s Derby Street Shoppes. The Fenway location will open first, hopefully in January 2018. It serves grain bowls, pitas, and a popular jalapeno-infused feta mousse.

Makeovers: Chef Jason Santos will close Back Bay Harry’s (142 Berkeley St.) on Sept. 24 and replace it with Citrus & Salt. The sister restaurant of his slinky New Orleans-inspired restaurant Buttermilk & Bourbon will serve Mexican coastal cuisine, says a rep.

The new spot will open in late fall or early winter with a similar menu as Buttermilk, but with south-of-the-border riffs. You can order blue cornmeal agave biscuits with lime honey butter, crab and cactus chowder, black bean hummus, and soft-serve ice cream in tropical flavors, or perch at a raw bar.

Tipples: Ronsky’s at The Street Chestnut Hill (3 Boylston St.) has added a wine bar, open Wednesday through Friday nights. For $12, sip a glass of spumante with marinated olives, pizza, marinated artichokes, meats and cheeses, and more.

KARA BASKIN
