BRIDGEWATER-RAYNHAM 13, CHELMSFORD 0

Bridgewater-Raynham shuts out Chelmsford

By Nate Weitzer Globe Correspondent  September 30, 2017

CHELMSFORD — It took a little ingenuity and a lot of grit for Bridgewater-Raynham to finally break through in a defensive battle at Chelmsford on Saturday afternoon.

Knotted in a scoreless deadlock and faced with a fourth and 5 at the Lion 34-yard line, Trojans coach Dan Buron gave the signal for a fake punt. There was some confusion with the audible, but senior captain Bryce Shaw was able to take the direct snap and stiff-arm several defenders on his way to a first down.

That preserved the drive long enough for Shaw to break a 21-yard touchdown run, which proved to be the difference in a 13-0 victory for the Trojans.

“Coach gave us the call from the sideline and I was the only one who really got the call for a fake,” explained Shaw. “When I started running I saw three or four guys coming at me so I got worried, but I was able to bounce it to the outside and make something happen.”

With virtually no blocking, Shaw somehow muscled his way for 6 yards in what proved to be the most pivotal play of the game.

“We made a few mistakes and we lucked out at times,” said Buron. “Bryce Shaw makes me look like a great coach. We didn’t block [the fake punt] and he just did it all on his own.“

The Trojans failed to come away with points after recovering a fumble in Lions territory early on, and Chelmsford (2-2) held strong again when the visitors drove down to the Lions 19-yard line in the second quarter.

In the second half, B-R (3-1) finally broke through with a 13-play scoring drive to take 6:34 off the clock, and put the game away with another clock-killing, 93-yard drive capped by an 11-yard touchdown run from Shaw in the fourth quarter.

Next week, the Trojans begin conference play against Old Colony League rival Dartmouth as part of a brutal finish to their regular season schedule.

“I think we grew up in the second half but we can’t afford to come out and play flat in the first half going forward,” said Buron. “We have to come right from the start ready to play and ready to work every day in practice.”
