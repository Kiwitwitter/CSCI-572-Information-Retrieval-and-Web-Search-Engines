Actual questions travelers ask TSA

A sampling of the wacky and practical things passengers ask the agency’s Twitter hot line.

  • E-Mail

    Share via e-mail

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • LinkedIn
Comments Print
The Boston Globe Tweet Share

By Neil Swidey   December 08, 2016

Travelers can now use TSA’s social-media hot line for quick answers to questions about what they can get through security checkpoints. Here are some of the more interesting recent queries.

Can I bring my cute friend?

Hey @AskTSA can I travel w this in my carry-on? (The hedgehog not the cup-know how you feel about liquids) #askTSA pic.twitter.com/QfCM07Jf3X

— Lizzie Hylton (@idreamoflizzie) August 15, 2016

@thelizzielean No worries! Small animals are allowed thru security. Pls refer to your airline for addt'l policies. https://t.co/SjWlnGIntQ

— AskTSA (@AskTSA) August 15, 2016

How about if my friend is a skull?

@mplusr26 Great question! A fake skull is allowed in your checked bags. Safe travels!

— AskTSA (@AskTSA) November 17, 2016

How about my deodorant?

@AskTSA are standard sized sticks of deodorant allowed in carry-on baggage? #AskTSA pic.twitter.com/d8JcabFYk0

— Rob McNicholas (@robbiemcnicks) August 6, 2016

@rob34h Any size solid deodorant is allowed in your carry-on, Rob. Safe travels!

— AskTSA (@AskTSA) August 4, 2016

What about my cardboard c0-worker?

Hey @AskTSA! Can you bring a cardboard cutout of a co-worker on a plane? #AskTSA #AskingForAFriend #NothingToSeeHere pic.twitter.com/pYjOPHadhQ

— Matt Chambers (@WeatherChambers) July 29, 2016

@WeatherChambers Your cardboard co-worker is allowed thru the checkpoint, Matt. Pls check w/your airline regarding their size restrictions.

— AskTSA (@AskTSA) July 29, 2016

There’s also some potty talk:

@THEYCALLMEAHRI Your item is allowed through the checkpoint, Ahri. Pls check with your airline about any size restrictions. Safe travels!

— AskTSA (@AskTSA) July 3, 2016

Liquids bring up plenty of issues:

. @AskTSA are large platform shoes with goldfish in the allowed through security? pic.twitter.com/aKQRx60rCX

— Sean (@ayy_yo_sean) July 30, 2016

@ayy_yo_sean Live fish are allowed in carry-on. Pls declare it to our officers prior to screening. See details at: https://t.co/GH8Jia3BMy

— AskTSA (@AskTSA) July 30, 2016

Advertisement

Is this a carry-on or checked item?

@AskTSA am I able to pack this "flyshooter" toy in my carry on? It's a gift for my grandpa that I'm visiting... pic.twitter.com/qrIYh3qBsT

— Justina Janas (@justinajanas) November 6, 2016

@justinajanas Good question! All replica weapons, including a flyshooter toy, are allowed in checked bags only. See: https://t.co/WVC30uPclv

— AskTSA (@AskTSA) November 6, 2016

@AskTSA can I bring my life-size armored knight as a carry on to FL or do I have to purchase a seat or check it pic.twitter.com/ChU045n1K8

— Tyler Bottke (@tbottke13) November 8, 2016

@tbottke13 Thanks for asking us, Tyler. Because your knight has a sword, it must be placed in checked bags. Pls see: https://t.co/ps1D8dvVUH

— AskTSA (@AskTSA) November 8, 2016

@AskTSA Can I bring a bottle of dead beetles onboard? pic.twitter.com/NPIgfjbZqL

— Adam Frawley (@AFrawleyz) November 8, 2016

@AFrawleyz Thanks for asking! A bottle of dead beetles is allowed in your carry-on bag.

— AskTSA (@AskTSA) November 8, 2016

How about power tools?

@AskTSA can I pack a dremel tool in my carry on? #askTSA pic.twitter.com/LMtvZBSLng

— Thomas Nay (@thomasnay) September 24, 2016

@thomasnay Thanks for asking, Thomas. Power tools are allowed only in checked bags. Safe travels!

— AskTSA (@AskTSA) September 24, 2016

Are these allowed?

@AskTSA Is it okay to bring one of these to my flight? #asktsa pic.twitter.com/RmcqQ9RWDY

— Kehrtom (@keentom) August 20, 2016

@keentom Bullets and replica bullets are allowed only in checked bags. Please visit: https://t.co/kxOw60B14u Safe travels!

— AskTSA (@AskTSA) August 20, 2016

What about turkeys?

.@AskTSA where are we coming down on frozen turkeys this Thanksgiving?? #asktsa pic.twitter.com/N5BLjCFxnB

— Michael K. Graham (@MichaelKGraham_) November 22, 2016

@MichaelKGraham_ Frozen turkeys are allowed in both carry-on and checked bags, Michael. Pls visit: https://t.co/qEGFI5eCVy

— AskTSA (@AskTSA) November 22, 2016

@MaddieLFrancis Dry ice is allowed. FAA policy states it must be limited to 5lbs & be properly packaged. Visit: https://t.co/vIWyKclAJu 1/2

— AskTSA (@AskTSA) November 15, 2016

Finally, how about lightsabers (size matters not)?

Hey @TSA @AskTSA can we take our lightsabers on airplanes? #asktsa #starwars #lightsaber pic.twitter.com/vnXcDnsdmn

— David Brewster (???) (@DBrewster316) August 3, 2016

@DBrewster316 A light saber is allowed in carry-on or checked bags, David. May the force be with you!

— AskTSA (@AskTSA) August 3, 2016

To check out more Ask TSA queries, visit the agency’s Twitter feed.
