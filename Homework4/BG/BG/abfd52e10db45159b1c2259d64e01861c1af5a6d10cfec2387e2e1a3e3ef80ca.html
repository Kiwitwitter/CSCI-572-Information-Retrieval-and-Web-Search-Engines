FRIDAY’S SCHOOL FOOTBAL ROUNDUP

Governor’s Academy rides defense to second straight win

Craig F. Walker/Globe Staff

Governor's Academy's Jared Dupere threw a pair of TD passes in the win over Milton Academy.

By Karl Capen Globe Correspondent  September 30, 2017

For the second consecutive week, the Governor’s Academy football proved why the games are played on the field, and not on paper.

Behind a physical defense that finished with six sacks and four turnovers, the Governors held on to defeat Milton Academy, 34-28, Friday afternoon at the Sager Bowl in Byfield.

Advertisement

“We are just ready to play this year. I don’t think anybody thought of us as one of the better teams this year so we kind of got a little bit of a chip on shoulder,” said Governor’s coach Jim O’Leary.

Governor’s (2-0) got the fireworks going early, taking its first drive of the game to the end zone on four plays, capped by junior Quintan Stott’s 35-yard run down the right sideline.

Milton Academy (1-1) found very little offensive momentum in the first half. The first two possessions of the game for the Mustangs resulted in an interception by Governor’s senior linebacker Trevor Durkin and a strip sack.

After the fumble recovery gave Governor’s Academy the ball inside the Milton 10-yard line, senior quarterback Jared Dupere connected with senior Patrick Flaherty to make it a 14-0 lead late in the first quarter.

The teams went back and forth for much of the second quarter until junior Joe Pridgen stripped Milton Academy’s quarterback Alec Beesmer on a sack, and the ball was returned 60 yards for a touchdown by sophomore Kevin Lemieux, giving the Govs a 20-0 advantage heading into halftime.

Dupere tossed second touchdown pass — this time to junior Shane Smith — early in the third quarter to make it 26-0 before the Mustangs finally got their offense moving.

Senior wideout Gregory Livingston reeled in a 30-yard touchdown pass from Beesmer to make it 26-7. After forcing a turnover, Beesmer took it in on a 1-yard run to cut it to, 26-14.

Facing a fourth-and-1 inside his 35-yard line on the ensuing possesion, O’Leary took the gamble and handed the ball off to the Govs’ most productive offensive player all day — the 5-foot-10-inch tailback Stott.

Stott found his way up the middle of a gain of four yards, and later in the drive ran 10 yards to paydirt to put the game out of reach.

“I believe in these guys so much that if we can’t get a yard we shouldn’t be in the game,” said O’Leary.

Beesmer directed the Mustangs to two more scores to make it close.

Nonleague

BC High 42, Brockton 18 — Playing on the Harvard Stadium turf, his home next fall, senior Danny Abraham rushed for 121 yards and two touchdowns on 15 carries for the host Eagles (4-0). Quarterback Will Bowen scored on scoring runs of 7, 3, and 5 yards for a 28-6 halftime lead.

Dartmouth 27, Durfee 7 — Senior Nate Ellis (128 yards rushing, 75 passing) ran for two scores and passed for another for the Indians.

Bourne 35, Martha’s Vineyard 28 — John Knowlton and Spencer Rose ran for two scores apiece as the Vineyarders got past the Canalmen.

New Bedford 38, Barnstable 8 — Nygel Palmer ran for a pair of touchdowns and caught a pair of TD passes to help lift the host Whalers (2-2).

Dennis-Yarmouth 34, Pembroke 6 — Geoffrey Jamiel had 214 receiving yards and three touchdowns — including an 80-yarder in the third quarter, to propel the visiting Dolphins (4-0).

Lynnfield 47, Winthrop 6 — Brett Cohee scored two touchdowns for the Pioneers — including an 85-yard scamper — as the Pioneers (4-0) cruised past the Vikings.

ISL

Middlesex 21, Rivers 0 — Junior Kabelo Tsotetsi (102 yards rushing) found pay dirt twice in the second half to lift host Zebras (1-1).

St. Mark’s 31, Roxbury Latin 16 — Senior Laquan McKever bolted into the end zone from 2, 1, and 4 yards before adding a 37-yard scoring run in the second quarter as the visiting Lions (1-1) secured their first win.

BB&N 21, Belmont Hill 0 — Junior quarterback Ryan Eamer completed 12 of his 15 passes for 147 yards and a touchdown while rushing for another score to lead the Knights (1-1) in Belmont.

Lawrence Academy 35, Nobles 0 — Brady Martin connected for a pair of TD passes, including a 73-yard strike to Thomas Greaney in the second quarter. The Spartans (2-0) scored all of their points in the first three quarters.

St. Sebastian’s 48, Tabor 18 — In his first varsity start, freshman Michael Phelps of Natick completed 11 of 14 passes for 189 yards and four touchdowns to propel the visiting Arrows (1-1) to the ISL win in Marion. Phelps threw scoring strikes to Alex Cherry (35 yards), Will Frisoli (24 yards), and Brian Craven (15 yards) as St. Seb’s sailed to a 27-0 first-quarter lead.

Boston City

Burke 30, Madison Park 14 — Patrick Culbreath rushed for touchdowns of 3 and 35 yards and tossed a 10-yard pass for Audriel Guante, and Steve Banks took a fumble recovery 90 yards for the Bulldogs (2-1).

O’Bryant 24, East Boston 22 — With 20 seconds left, Luke Muffeo lateraled to Thomas Solomon, who connected with Anthony Francis on a 70-yard pass for the winning touchdown for the Tigers (2-2). Francis caught two TD passes, ran for a score, and returned an interception 40 yards for another score.

Brighton 20, Tech Boston 6 — Erick Brown ran for 189 yards and a touchdown, threw for a TD, and ran in a 2-point conversion for the Bengals. Jerrod Clark caught a 30-yard touchdown and added 18 tackles on defense, including three sacks.

Boston English 22, South Boston 14 — With a minute and a half left in regulation, Sam Denton ran 80 yards for the go-ahead score for the Eagles (1-3). David Robinson sealed the win with an interception off a deep pass down the sideline on fourth down. Denton ran for a career-high 178 yards on nine carries for English.

Catholic Central

St. John Paul II 30, Cathedral 18 — Devan Harris tossed three TD passes and Richard Mulcahy produced a late score on the ground as SJPII (4-0) pulled away for a pivotal Catholic Central Small victory over the host Panthers (3-1).

Apponequet 21, Greater New Bedford 7 — Shayne Cooney converted a pair of short scoring runs in the second half for the Lakers (4-0).

Seekonk 50, Wareham 24 — Senior Issac Nascimento had four touchdowns for the Vikings (3-1).

Hamilton-Wenham 42, Amesbury 12 — Sophomore Ian Coffey ran for three touchdowns and 191 yards on 10 carries for the Generals (4-0).
