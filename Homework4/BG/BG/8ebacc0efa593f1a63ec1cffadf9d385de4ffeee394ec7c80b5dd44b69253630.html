Players of the Year

Division 1: Elayna Grillakis, Central Catholic

After earning an All-Scholastic nod as a sophomore, the All-State junior racked up 32 goals and 23 assists for the Division 1 state champion Raiders, making her Merrimack Valley Conference Player of the Year.

Division 2: Eve Hewins, Hingham

Hewins capped her career by scoring the overtime winner against Arlington in Hingham’s victory in the Division 2 state title game. An All-American, her 38 goals and 16 assists this season brought her career point total to 150.

Division 3: Shannon Aviza, Medway

Advertisement

Aviza’s 24 goals and nine assists earned her All-New England honors for a second time as well as the Tri-Valley League MVP award. The Syracuse recruit ends her Medway career with 54 goals and 50 assists.

Division 4: Mackenzie Vlachos, East Bridgewater

A two-time Player of the Year, Vlachos broke the single-season school record for goals (37) and assists (30) as well as the career points record with 198. An All-American, she led the Vikings to the state title this fall.

All-Scholastics

Michela Agresti, Swampscott, sophomore

The Boston College recruit and NEC MVP registered 14 goals and 17 assists from her center back position. Opponents scored just four goals against the Big Blue when the EMGSCA D3 North Player of the Year was on the field.

Jenna Barnes, Lincoln-Sudbury, senior

The All-New England center midfielder repeats as an All-Scholastic after tallying 15 goals and nine assists for the 14-3-4 Warriors. The senior DCL All-Star graduates with 101 career points and a 3.86 GPA. She’ll attend Holy Cross.

Elizabeth Dumas, Brookline, senior

Dumas paced the 18-2-1 Warriors with 27 goals and 15 assists, making her the Bay State Carey MVP and an All New England selection. After an 88-point career, the National Honor Society student plans to play soccer at Fairfield.

Caroline Harkins, Hingham, junior

Advertisement

The forward tallied 25 goals and 19 assists this fall en route to the Division 2 state title. An All-State selection and Patriot League All-Star, Harkins has won a Division 2 state title in each of her three seasons at Hingham.

Katerina Hassapis, North Reading, senior

The Cape Ann League MVP posted 15 shutouts for the Hornets (15-5-1), earning her second consecutive All-Scholastic honor. The All-State keeper graduates with 35 shutouts and a 0.48 career goals against average.

Taylor Kofton, Whitman-Hanson, junior

Kofton’s 14-goal, 21-assist campaign earned her All-American honors and a second-straight All-Scholastic nod. The forward led the Panthers to the D1 South sectional final. She plans to play soccer at Boston University.

Sarah Lofstrom, East Bridgewater, senior

Lofstrom ends her career with 134 points and two MIAA state championships after tallying 20 goals and 17 assists this year. She scored a hat trick in EB’s 4-3 win over Monson in the D4 state title game. She’ll attend Colby.

Lexi Rothmann, Norwell, sophomore

Rothmann tallied 20 goals and 16 assists to pace the Clippers, who earned a share of the South Shore League Large title. A Providence College commit, Rothmann earned EMGSA All-State honors. She’s also a high honors student.

Caitlin Ryan, Hull, senior

A Division 4 All-State selection, Ryan broke the school record with 40 goals this season. The striker finished her career with 101 points and was a three-time South Shore League All-Star. She also plays basketball and lacrosse.

Kate Ryan, Winchester, junior

Ryan, a junior forward, registered 15 goals and 12 assists for the Middlesex Liberty champion Sachems (13-3-3) and was the league MVP. The All-State selection is also a three-year varsity member of the Winchester girls’ hockey team.

Emily Stephenson, St. Mary’s, senior

Stephenson posted 10 shutouts and a 0.88 gaa for St. Mary’s (12-10), which reached the Division 4 North final. She has 16 career shutouts. The EMass D4 North Player of the Year is also a goalie for the Spartans’ hockey team.

Olivia Wingate, Wilmington, junior

Wingate was voted All-New England despite missing six games while playing with the US U-16 National Team in the Netherlands. She managed to rack up 17 goals and 5 assists for the 16-1-2 Wildcats.

Coaches of the Year

Division 1: Casey Grange, Central Catholic

The two-time reigning Merrimack Valley Coach of the Year led the Raiders to a 23-0 record and a Division 1 state title. An All-American forward at Bryant, Grange has a 111-13-14 record in her seven years at Central Catholic.

Division 2: Ryan Puntiri, Hingham

Under Puntiri, the Harborwomen (21-0-1) won their third consecutive Division 2 state title. He was named D2 EMass Coach of the Year for the third time in three seasons, and welcomed a baby daughter in October.

Division 3: Ken Leeder, Swampscott

The Division 3 EMass Coach of the Year led the Big Blue to a 17-1-2 record and a Northeastern Conference title. Leeder was also named the Northeastern Conference Coach of the Year and has a 59-15-9 career record.

Division 4: Stew Bell, Hull

In his 17th season, Bell led the Pirates to a South Shore League Tobin title. The Division 4 EMass Coach of the Year teamed with senior striker and All-Scholastic Caitlin Ryan as Hull finished an impressive 14-3-2.
