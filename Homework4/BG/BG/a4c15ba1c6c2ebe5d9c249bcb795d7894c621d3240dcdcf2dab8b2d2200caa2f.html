In GOP debate, some reason on immigration (though not from Trump)

By Marcela García   August 07, 2015

As expected, immigration was a frequent topic in the first Republican presidential debate — as well as the top issue discussed on Facebook while the debate went on. Predictably, the conversation devolved into a contest: Which candidate would crack down most harshly on unauthorized immigration, and who would be the toughest on border security and enforcement?

Marco Rubio emerged as the most nuanced thinker on the subject: He demonstrated the best level of understanding of border dynamics, and called out Donald Trump for singling out Mexico as a source of “criminals.” As Rubio noted, most unauthorized immigrants in the US come from Central America. Granted, that was a quick flash, but Latinos who were watching surely noticed.

Trump was actually somewhat correct when he said, “If it weren’t for me, you wouldn’t be talking about illegal immigration.” His divisive and caustic comments on the issue, particularly about Mexicans and the border, are most likely the reason why he stays atop in the polls. He knows he’s touched a nerve with the Republican hard-core base.

The real challenge now for Republicans vying for the presidential nomination — especially those who want a real shot at earning the Latino vote — is to move away from Trump’s tired hard-line, uncompromising stances. Really, how much more money can we throw at border enforcement?

Rubio, somewhat jokingly, dismissed the idea of building a wall across the border and beefing up security even more. “If [Mexican drug kingpin] El Chapo can build a tunnel under that fence,” Rubio said, “we need to deal with that.”

Bush, for his part, deserves credit for standing by his predebate position that immigration reform must include an earned path to citizenship. In his closing remarks, he noted that the broken immigration system needs to be fixed and turned into an economic driver.

Indeed. Here’s hoping we hear more of those sensible concepts from Republicans in the campaign ahead.
