FRIDAY’S SCHOOL ROUNDUP

In memory of her sister, Caroline Brodt powers Barnstable volleyball

  • E-Mail

    Share via e-mail

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Google+
  • LinkedIn
Comments Print
The Boston Globe Tweet Share

By Lenny Rowe Globe Correspondent  September 09, 2017

It was an inspirational and emotional night for Barnstable girls’ volleyball team. The reigning Division 1 champions remembered their late former teammate, Olivia Brodt, with a moving ceremony before their match against visiting Dennis-Yarmouth.

Her younger sister Caroline, a senior, unveiled the banner and then delivered 42 assists, 6 aces, and 3 kills in a 25-14, 25-8, 25-8 victory.

Advertisement

“For her to have the performance she had today under the circumstances was pretty remarkable,” said Barnstable coach Tom Turco.

Olivia Brodt, who helped lead Barnstable to the D1 state championship in 2013, died April 2 at age 20 from a recurrence of rhabdomyosarcoma . She was in her sophomore year at Babson College.

“She’s a special player, person, and incredible student,” said Turco. “They all know her and all of them play for Olivia.”

Junior Riley James, the Globe’s reigning D1 Player of the Year, had 21 kills and contributed 13 digs. Classmate Ingrid Murphy racked up 20 kills.

“I’m just proud of the team the way they performed and the support they gave to Caroline,” said Turco.

Hamilton-Wenham 3, Triton 0 — Senior outside hitter Lauren Flynn converted 62 percent of her hits as the Generals (3-0) cruised.

Westford 3, Waltham 0 — The Grey Ghosts (2-0) were led by junior Elise Sepe (7 kills, 7 assists), and senior Paige Karcherian (5 kills, 5 aces).

Waltham 1, Westford 0 — Senior Gustavo Costa scored the lone goal for the Hawks (2-0) and sophomore Karl Bernet recorded his second straight shutout of the season.

Pentucket 3, Triton 0 — Senior captain Jake Dickson (two goals) propelled the Sachems to the road victory.

Westwood 1, Hanover 0 — Junior Tommy Boukouvalas converted in the 55th minute for the Wolverines (1-0-1).

Rockland 8, Randolph 1 — Sydney McKenna and Chloe Jones scored two goals apiece for the Bulldogs.

Medfield 3, Hopkinton 1 — Senior Taylor Sherman registered a pair of goals and freshman Allie Dubbs tacked on another to lift the visiting Warriors (2-0).
