CLEMSON 34, BC 7

BC finally wears down in loss to Clemson

By Julian Benbow Globe Staff  September 23, 2017

CLEMSON, S.C. — For a few surreal moments, after Boston College running back A.J. Dillon plowed through a pile of bodies to punch in a 1-yard touchdown late in the third quarter, the sea of 81,500 orange-clad fans at Clemson Memorial Stadium was uncomfortably quiet, taking in the thought of the impossible unfolding in front of their eyes.

After Dillon’s score, the defending national champion Clemson Tigers found themselves knotted in a 7-7 game as the fourth quarter approached with a BC team that came in as a 34-point underdog and was supposed to go into Death Valley as, more or less, a sacrificial lamb.

The entire stadium was trying to make sense of it.

“The only people you could hear was our fans down here screaming for us,” BC defensive end Harold Landry said. “You could definitely tell that we had them right where we wanted them.”

The Eagles had lost to eight straight ranked teams since 2014. They had only beaten one ranked team since 2008. They hadn’t beaten Clemson since 2007.

Box score: BC vs. Clemson

The decibel level didn’t spike again until early in the fourth quarter when the Tigers put together a 10-play, 67-yard scoring drive propped up by a 23-yard pass from quarterback Kelly Bryant to sophomore receiver Diondre Overton and capped by a 6-yard touchdown run by Adam Choice.

About six minutes later, when freshman running back Travis Etienne shed a tackler and broke away for a 50-yard TD, everyone in orange could exhale.

A defensive slugfest ultimately panned out as the lopsided loss it was expected to be as Clemson rattled off 27 points in the fourth quarter against a drained BC defense and ran away with a 34-7 win.

The Tigers remained perfect to start the season, winning their fourth straight game. The Eagles dropped their third straight game and fell to 1-3 (0-2 in the ACC).

After Notre Dame ran roughshod over the Eagles last week, BC’s defense was determined to redeem itself against the No. 2 team in the country.

“We knew coming in it was going to be a big-time game and I think our defense — we love this moment, we love when the pressure is on,” said Landry.

They set the tone with pad-popping hits and never let Bryant, Clemson’s electric quarterback, get comfortable, intercepting two of his passes, sacking him twice, hurrying him once, and breaking up four more of his throws.

Lukas Denis made 12 tackles and came up with his third interception of the season. Isaac Yiadom made eight tackles and picked off his first pass of the year.

After watching Notre Dame run for a staggering 515 yards on the Eagles, Clemson pounded the ball on the ground with 58 rush attempts.

By the fourth quarter, an already thin BC defense was running on fumes and leaning on players with little experience. Already missing senior linebacker Connor Strachan, the Eagles lost sophomore linebacker Max Richardson after he went down with a knee injury in the second quarter, which forced redshirt freshman John Lamot to step in.

Bryant had to grind to complete 17 of 26 passes for 140 yards. But he rushed for 106 yards and two TDs, including a 4-yard score in the fourth that made it 27-7.

Etienne (nine carries, 113 yards, two touchdowns) tacked on another 10-yard touchdown with 52 seconds left to pad the lead.

The Eagles suffered their third straight 20-point loss. They’ve lost seven of their last 15 games by 20 or more going back to last season.

“We battled hard, we were physical, we wanted to drag them into the fourth quarter — we dragged them into the fourth quarter,” BC coach Steve Addazio said.

Down by just a touchdown going into the half, the Eagles never saw the avalanche coming.

The Eagles’ defense was in a staredown with the Tigers, and by and large refused to flinch. The bulk of the Tigers’ 222 total first-half yards came in the opening quarter on a scoring drive that saw Clemson gash the Eagles for 94 yards on the ground, capped by an 11-yard touchdown run by Bryant.

BC’s offense couldn’t capitalize on the turnovers its defense gifted them. After Denis’s pick in the first quarter, Eagles kicker Colton Lichtenberg missed a 43-yard field goal attempt off the right upright (his first miss of the season). When Yiadom came up with another interception in the third quarter that set the Eagles up at their 34, the Eagles mustered 30 yards on six plays before punting the ball away.

“To take down the No. 2 team in the country, you’ve got to take advantage of those opportunities,” said Landry. “All of them. As many as you can get, you’ve got to take advantage of them.”

Once again the Eagles tried to take away the positives from a lopsided loss. But what makes it more difficult this time is watching the injuries pile up.

With Jon Baker (knee), Strachan (knee), and Charlie Callinan (foot) already out prior to the game, the Eagles then lost Richardson and lineman Aaron Monteiro (lower body injury). Also, quarterback Anthony Brown left the game in the fourth quarter with an undisclosed injury.

“I’m very, very concerned right now, with the amount of injuries we have right now and what we just took in this game,” Addazio said. “I don’t know the extent of them right now. I just have to get my arms around where we are. It seems like every week, we have to restart something.”
