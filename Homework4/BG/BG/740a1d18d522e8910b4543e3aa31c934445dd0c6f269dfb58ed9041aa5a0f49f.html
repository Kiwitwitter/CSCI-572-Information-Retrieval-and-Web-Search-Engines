HIGH SCHOOL CROSS-COUNTRY NOTES

Now healthy, Needham’s Cameron McLeod aiming high

By Shawn McFarland Globe Correspondent  October 06, 2017

For Needham’s Cameron McLeod, last year’s cross-country season brought some terrible feelings.

After suffering a stress reaction prior to his first regular-season meet, the then-sophomore McLeod was sidelined for the entirety of the cross-country season. Instead of helping the Rockets to a potential all-state berth, his days were filled with recovery, rehab, and most significantly, frustration.

“There’s nothing like it,” McLeod recalled. “I’m a really competitive person, so it really killed me. You’ve just got to know that your body has to do its thing.”

Needham went on to place third at the Eastern Massachusetts Division 2 meet, and fifth at the Division 1 All-State meet. All of which came without the help of McLeod, who despite not being able to compete with his team spent every day improving.

He worked on his core as opposed to his stamina. He did cross-training. He did anything he could possibly do to stay in shape despite the season-ending injury. By the first few weeks of the indoor track season, McLeod was running again — but not without a few hitches.

“I started running when I was a freshman, and it was kind of like that all over again,” McLeod said of the rust he had to shake off following months on the shelf. “It was like, ‘Am I ever going to get back to where I was?’”

Eventually, he did. He placed third in the mile at the Bay State Conference Championship in February with a time of 4 minutes 31 seconds. He finished 11th at states, and shaved seconds off his mile time at 4:28. He followed up with a 15th-place finish at the All-State meet in the spring.

Needham coach Chris Van Cott recalls the meet in which he believes McLeod’s confidence peaked. He placed sixth at the Division 3 Championship in May, thanks in part to a personal-record 4:22 mark in the mile.

“While it’s never just one race, I think that race certainly made him a lot more confident, and made him excited to get to that next level in the fall,” Van Cott said.

Now in the midst of his junior cross-country season, McLeod is running healthy again, and despite a successful sophomore year on the track, he still feels he has more to prove.

“I want to show that I’m not just some miler kid,” McLeod said. “I really just want to show that I am a cross-country runner. I’m not soft, I’m tough. I just really want to show that I’ve put in the work.”

McLeod broke out at the Amherst Invitational in September, and ran a time of 15:53 — good for third place, and just 10 seconds behind Brookline’s Lucas Aramburu, the third-place finisher at last year’s Division 1 All-State meet.

“I feel good, our team’s really good,” McLeod said. “We’re just getting things rolling.”

It’s still early in the season, but a clear favorite for top boys runner in the state has yet to arise. Aramburu and St. John’s Prep’s Tristan Shelgren have made strong cases, while Newton North’s Andrew Mah isn’t far behind. McLeod, realistically, has a chance to be in the same tier .

“He wants to be one of the top five guys in the state,” Van Cott said. “That’s a tall order, particularly coming from someone who didn’t have a season last year. But he has a great mental game, he’s really mentally tough.

“He’s a good competitor, and he certainly does have the natural ability.”

Added Van Cott, “Whether it’s top five or top 10, I think we both expect that he’ll be in the mix come November.”

Going the Distance:

■ The second round of MSTCA cross-country coaches poll released this week, and the Milton girls still sit atop the rankings, as they did to open the season. Wellesley jumped from fifth to third, thanks in part to junior Maggie Donahue, who’s placed first or second in each of the Raiders’ first four regular-season meets. Natick and Woburn rose from unranked to eighth and ninth, respectively. The Redhawks are led by defending state champion Grace Connolly, while the Tanners are coming off a first-place team finish at the Frank Kelley Invitational.

■ In Massachusetts high school cross-country, there’s always a Rocha. Following the graduation of defending state champion Marcelo Rocha from Peabody, his younger brother Claudio is next in line. The junior finished sixth in the boys’ championship race at the Frank Kelley Invitational this past weekend with a time of 16:05.71.

Runners of the Week:

■ Sarah Roffman, Littleton: In her first ever ross-country invitational, the junior won the girls’ championship at the Frank Kelley Invitational with a time of 18:46.62.

■ Grace Connolly, Natick: The defending state champion set a new course record at Elm Bank Reservation in Wellesley with a time of 17:27 in a Bay State Conference tri-meet versus Wellesley and Brookline.

■ Alexina Hurley, Woburn: The senior placed fourth in the girls’ championship at the Frank Kelley Invitational with a time of 19:19.84, and helped Woburn to a team championship.

■ Katherine Hartnett, Franklin: Hartnett placed first in a dual meet versus Attleboro this week with a time of 18:39.

■ Mia Costa, Walpole: The freshman placed first in a tri-meet with Needham and Braintree with a time of 18:38.

Meets to Watch

Norwood at Wellesley, Oct. 11: The Raiders have been one of the hottest squads in the state through the early weeks of the season.

Plymouth South at Plymouth North, Oct. 11: South’s Jillian Callahan (sixth at All-States last year) highlights this rivalry meet.
