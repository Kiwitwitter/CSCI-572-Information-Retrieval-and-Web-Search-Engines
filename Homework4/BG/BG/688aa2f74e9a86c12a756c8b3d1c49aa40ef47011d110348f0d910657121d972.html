FALL TV PREVIEW

From ‘Curb’ to ‘The Crown,’ eight shows to welcome back

Netflix

Stranger Things

By Matthew Gilbert Globe Staff  September 22, 2017

“Curb Your Enthusiasm”

(Sunday, Oct. 1, HBO)

While we worry about all the big things, from the state of our democracy to nuclear war, Larry David will sweat the small stuff for us. The great comedy returns after a six-year hiatus for a ninth season, with Bryan Cranston irresistibly guest-starring as Larry’s therapist.

“This Is Us”

Advertisement

(Tuesday, Sept. 26, NBC)

Time to get dust in your eyes again every week. Last year’s breakout network drama returns with more past and present Pearson angst. Is Kate the new Adele? Will Randall and Beth adopt? And, most important, how did Jack die?

“Shameless”

(Sunday, Nov. 5, Showtime)

Watching this show is like a visit with family. As the Gallagher kids continue to fight to raise themselves up, with Lip trying to stay sober and Debbie taking welding classes, season eight is also going to incorporate a Trump-era ICE plotline involving Fiona’s restaurant.

Robert Viglasky/Netflix

THE CROWN

“The Crown”

(Friday, Dec. 8, Netflix)

Advertisement

The beautifully acted biographical drama about the reign of Queen Elizabeth II and the meanings of royalty returns, and I can’t wait for a holiday season binge. New to the cast: Matthew Goode as Lord Snowdon and Michael C. Hall as John F. Kennedy. This is the fantastic Claire Foy’s last season; the 33-year-old will be replaced by an older actress as Elizabeth ages.

“Stranger Things”

(Friday, Oct. 27, Netflix)

The 1980s-styled sci-fi series, an unexpected sensation, will be set a year after the events of season one. Show creators Matt and Ross Duffer are hinting that Will may be a different kid after his visit to the Upside Down. Cue Winona Ryder cryface.

Diyah Pera/THE CW

RIVERDALE

“Riverdale”

(Wednesday, Oct. 11, CW)

The dark and twisty adaptation of the wholesome Archie comics is filled with stock soap tropes. What can I say? I got sucked in by the murder mystery, emo Archie, and Jughead’s hat. I stayed for Veronica’s wit. This season: Who shot Luke Perry’s Fred Andrews?

“Saturday Night Live”

(Saturday, Sept. 30, NBC)

Last season, the show had its best ratings in decades, thanks to timely political comedy featuring Alec Baldwin, Melissa McCarthy, and Kate McKinnon, all of whom won Emmys. Let’s see if the cast and writers can keep the ball rolling. Baldwin says he’ll be back as President Trump.

Mammoth Screen for BBC and MASTERPIECE

POLDARK

“Poldark”

(Sunday, Oct. 1, PBS)

Ah life is pretty, oh so pretty and oh so tragic. The Cornwall-set “Masterpiece” soap returns with Ross and Demelza trying to repair their marriage after he cheated with former flame Elizabeth. Elizabeth, now married to Ross’s arch-enemy, is pregnant. Who’s the daddy?
