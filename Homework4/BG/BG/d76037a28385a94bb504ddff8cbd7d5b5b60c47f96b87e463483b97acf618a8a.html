2012 Girls’ Indoor Track All-Scholastics

By Nick French Globe Correspondent  June 24, 2012

Meet the standout performers who earned All-Scholastic honors for their work this spring.

Runner of the Year:

Carla Forbes Newton North, Junior

Advertisement

Forbes capped off a brilliant season with two national titles, winning the triple jump in a New England record 42-9.5 and the long jump. The junior has dominated the field events all season, capturing state titles in the long jump (19-1) and the triple jump (40-0).

All-Scholastics:

Lucy Alexander Lincoln-Sudbury

Alexander was the top EMass performer in the high jump this season, capturing the Division 1 title with a leap of 5-5.25. She followed that with a third-place finish at All-States, and another third at New Englands where she leaped 5-5. She also won at the State Coaches meet.

Elizabeth Bray Wakefield, Senior

Bray was unmatched in the shot put this season, including an impressive 8th-place finish at nationals. She was the All-State champion with a toss of 41-7, after winning the Division 3 state title and the State Coaches meet championship earlier in the spring.

Stephanie Brown Newton North, Senior

Brown captured the State Coaches (10-6) and Division 1 (11-0) titles and finished second at All-States, clearing 11-0. She was the All-State champion in 2010. She is also a member of the swim team, and indoor track teams. Brown will attend Dartmouth in the fall.

Vanessa Clerveaux Brockton, Senior

Clerveaux was one of the top sprinters in the state this season and easily the top short distance hurdler. She captured the All-State title (14.36), Division 1 championship (14.19), and the State Coaches title (14.08) this season. She also finished second at the New Englands in 14.32.

Nikki Dempsey Norwell, Senior

Dempsey captured the 100-meter title in the Division 4 meet in 12.79 but she saved her best performance for the long jump. After finishing second at the Division 4 meet and sixth at All-States, the South Shore League All-Star won the New England title at 18-0.

Jen Esposito Mansfield, Senior

Esposito finished her year in style, taking third place at the nationals. She also won the Division 2 title with a meet record time of 1:01.98 and then won the All-State title as well. An indoor track champion as well, she will attend Elon University.

Nicole Genard Somerville

Genard had the top throw in the state this year with her winning toss of 139-10 at the Division 1 meet. She also won the State Coaches meet at 120-9. She finished the season undefeated but was unable to compete in the All-States because of graduation.

Meghan Grela Ursuline, Junior

Grela set the Division 4 record in the 800 with a 2:13.53 after capturing the State Coaches title in the 800. In the All-States, she ran a 2:15.80 to take home second place. A Catholic Conference All-Star, she is also an indoor track team member and honor roll student.

Liz Holmes Foxborough

Only a freshman, Holmes burst onto the scene this year capturing the Division 3 title in 5:03 and then bettered that with a 5:00.23 performance at All-States that gave her second place. She was the freshman indoor national champion in the mile with a 4:55.

Maya Jarostchuk Acton-Boxborough

Jarostchuk took home plenty of hardware this season, winning the Dual County League title, the Division 1 title in a school record 56.85, and the All-State title in 57.36. She is a DCL All-Star in soccer, and also runs indoor track for the Colonials.

Rebecca Robinson Arlington, Senior

Robinson dominated the indoor season at the 300, and she didn't skip a beat in moving to the 200 in the spring. She was the Division 2 champion (24.82) and the State Coaches champion (24.78) before capping the season with the All-State title (24.74). She is also a soccer all-star.

Briana Robitaille Attleboro, Senior

Making a return appearance on these pages after earning All-Scholastic honors during the indoor season, Robitaille swept the Division 1 meet (12.13) and the State Coaches meet (12.09). The Hockomock All-Star punctuated her season and stellar career with a win at All-States, finishing in 12.31.

Catarina Rocha Peabody

Rocha makes her third All-Scholastic appearance this year. Rocha bested the field at the Division 1 meet, running in 11:03.43. She followed that up with a win at All-States in 10:45.50. She was the Globe's cross-country runner of the year and an indoor All-Scholastic in the mile.

Emily Stark Middlesex, Senior

Stark’s 9:56 in the 3K was the fastest recorded in Massachusetts in 2012. The senior captain has been the ISL champion in the 1,500m and 3,000m for the past three years and holds the New England record in the 1,500m and 3K. She plan to attend Yale University.

Jackie Vahey Winchester, Junior

The state discus champion, Vahey swept Division 2 and All-State championships this spring tossing a 123-08 in the All-State meet. She was also the Middlesex League champ in the discus and shot put. A National Honor Society member, she also competes in indoor track.

***

Relays:

Relays: 4 x 100 — Brockton

The Brockton relay team of (left to right) Vanessa Clerveaux, Nikayla Simpson, Milan Neville, and Talia Halliday was an unbeatable group this spring, winning the State Coaches Invitational (48.84), Division 1 (48.73), and All-State titles (48.94). All four earned Big Three Conference All-Star honors and Clerveaux also was state champion in the 100 hurdles. None of this was surprising considering the Boxers also dominated the winter season in the 4x200 and Clerveaux was the winter Runner of the Year.

Relays: 4 x 400 — Mansfield

After winning the Division 2 state title the Hornets proved to be the best 4x400 relay team in EMass this season with their second place finish at all-states. At All-States it was the freshman Amerantes who got off first, followed by Oldow who helped the relay team set a meet record at the Division 2 race. All-scholastic Esposito ran third. The squad was anchored by Jones. For the year they also won the Hockomock title in the 4x400.

Relays: 4 x 800 — Hamilton-Wenham

This fearsome foursome ran a blistering 9:18.23 at the All-State meet to capture the top spot and earn a trip to nationals. Phelan the Cape Ann League's two-mile champion, got out quickly to start. Blatchford, who finished eighth in the 800 at All-States, kept up the pace in the second leg. Running third was Duffy in place of Wiegand, who ran at the Division 4 meet. Closing out the race was Senning, the CAL champ in the 400 and runner-up in the 800. She was able to finish with a burst and hold off Newton North by six seconds for the win.

***

Coaches of the year:

Coach of the year: Myles Eldridge Foxborough

Foxboro had one of its best seasons ever led by Eldridge and a group of young runners with a great future. In his four years Eldridge has overhauled the program and this spring the Warriors finished third in the Division 3 meet.

Coach of the year: Dan Goldner Acton-Boxborough

The Colonials had their best outdoor season in school history, finishing undefeated in the Dual County League for the first time since 1985. Under Goldner's guidance, A-B also won the league meet for the second straight year. They finished second in the Divison 1 state meet.

Coach of the year: Rick Kates Notre Dame (Hingham)

One of the more dominant programs in any league in any sport, Kates led Notre Dame to its 24th straight Catholic Conference title this year with another undefeated dual meet season. He has amassed 300 wins in 25 years and is a member of the MSTCA Hall of Fame.

Coach of the year: Thomas Shaw Ursuline

In just his second year, Shaw helped lead Ursuline to a second-place league finish and seventh in the Division 4 meet. He also coaches cross-country and indoor track at Ursuline after spending six years as an assistant at Milton. A track and cross-country runner, he graduated from UNH in 1998.
