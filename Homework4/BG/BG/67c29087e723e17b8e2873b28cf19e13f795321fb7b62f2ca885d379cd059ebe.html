MARK YOUR CALENDAR

In church, music from Bach, Verdi, and Standing Rock

St. Mary of the Assumption of Hull

Mary Lou Quinlan performs "The God Box, A Daughter's Story" Oct. 13 at Notre Dame Academy in Hingham.

By Robert Knox Globe Correspondent  October 06, 2017

CLASSIC, CONTEMPORARY The First Unitarian Universalist Society of Middleborough presents a concert of classical and contemporary music, by organist Natsumi Malloy, the UU Chalice Choir, soprano Leilani Dalpe, and other ensembles and soloists presenting a broad spectrum of music and styles. Works include music by Bach, Mozart, Chopin, Verdi, the choral piece “Crossing the Bar” set to music by Gwyneth Walker, and “Water Is Life — Mni Wičoni,” written in English with phrases in Lakota and inspired by the Standing Rock Sioux. 25 South Main St.; Sunday, Oct. 8, 3 p.m.; $10 donation.

STRONG PREDICTION Spiritualists, mediums, palmists, psychics, and tarot readers take part in raising funds for Theatre One at the theater’s annual psychic fair. Mitchell Memorial Club, 29 Elm St., Middleborough; Saturday, Oct. 14, 11-3; $2 door, $20 for readings.

FAMILY TRADITION The “Mad Love Music Festival” celebrates the life of musician David Jodka, who died three years ago. Featuring local musicians, food, drink, and activities for the whole family. South Shore Conservatory of Music, 1 Conservatory Drive, Hingham; Sunday, Oct. 8, 11-5; $30, $10 children, www.madlovemusicfestival.org.

POEMS AND PICTURES The annual poetry showcase takes place in conjunction with the Plymouth Art Guild’s “Juried Art Show and Festival.” Readers are Michael Daley, Kevin Gallagher, Gloria Mindock, Tim Suermondt, Susan Cook Thanas, and Pui Ying Wong. The art show continues through Nov. 5. Plymouth Center for the Arts, 11 North St.; Sunday, Oct. 15, 1 p.m.; free.

TRAVELOGUE Librarian and traveler Nancy Kelly shares stories and insights on Swiss traditions and tales of the snow-covered Alps. Ventress Memorial Library, 15 Library Plaza, Marshfield; Tuesday, Oct. 10, 6 p.m.

Also at the library, travel photographers Ed and Cindy Esposito present “Discovering Scotland,” a multimedia slideshow that travels from Edinburgh to the Scottish Highlands and the Isle of Skye. Monday, Oct. 16, 7 p.m.; free, no registration.

Finally, archeologist Stephen Silliman delves into facts and theories claiming that Vikings, Welsh, Chinese, Africans, and Polynesians visited the Americas before Columbus. Wednesday, Oct. 11, 7-8:30 p.m.; free.

SONG OF HIMSELF Actor Stephen Collins impersonates 19th-century poet and visionary Walt Whitman, based largely on the author’s poetry. Walpole Public Library, 143 School St.; Monday, Oct. 16, 7 p.m.; free, www.walpolelibraryfriends.org.

The Spire Center

Martin Barre, guitarist for Jethro Tull, performs in Plymouth.

JETHRO GUITAR Martin Barre, Jethro Tull guitarist for 43 years, and his band play classic songs from the Tull catalog. Spire Center, 25 ½ Court St., Plymouth; Sunday, Oct. 8, 8 p.m.; $50, www.spirecenter.org.

Also at the Spire, New England performs on Friday, Oct. 13, 8 p.m.; premium seating $75, regular $30.

OCTOBERFEST Winslow Farm Animal Sanctuary’s “Kid Friendly Haunted Trail” is open weekends in October. 37 Eddy St., Norton; Fridays and Saturdays, 6-8 p.m., Saturdays and Sundays, noon-4 p.m.; $10 adults, $5 children, free under 2, www.winslowfarm.com/events.html.

BOG TRIP The Duxbury Cranberry Festival has a field trip to the Herndon Cranberry Bogs, where Mark Herndon gives a tour of wet and dry cranberry harvesting. Transportation from Duxbury Senior Center, 10 Mayflower St.; Wednesday, Oct. 11, and Monday, Oct. 16, 12:45 p.m.; $1.

TASTES OF AUTUMN Daniel Webster Estate hosts Fall Victorian Tea with scones and breads, sandwiches, and desserts. 238 Webster St., Marshfield; Oct. 15, 2-4 p.m.; $20, reserve by calling 781-834-7818, www.danielwebsterestate.org.

TWO DIMENSIONAL The 334 Gallery Committee at the Milton Art Center presents “OpenUp,” a show ranging from representational paintings capturing the natural environment to abstract expressionism. 334 Edge Hill Rd.; reception Friday, Oct. 13, 6:30-9 p.m.; show continues through Nov. 17.

LAUGH OUT LOUD Yoga teachers Linda and Bill Hamaker lead a free “Laughter Yoga” session at the Unitarian Church of Sharon. 4 North Main St.; Saturday, Oct. 14, 11-noon; bring canned item for food pantry.

MEDICAL MYSTERIES Dr. Stuart Mushlin speaks on his book, “Playing the Ponies and Other Medical Mysteries Solved,” and shares some of his most intriguing cases as a diagnostician. Kingston Council on Aging, 30 Evergreen St.; Thursday, Oct. 12, 7 p.m.; free.

HEAD CASE Marie Fricker of Scituate discusses her journey and road to survival with brain cancer and speaks on her book, “All in My Head: How a Hypochondriac Beat Brain Cancer.” She also offers her “top 15 list” of practical strategies for coping with cancer. Scituate Town Library, 85 Branch St.; Thursday, Oct. 12, 7 p.m.; www.scituatetownlibrary.org.

GHOST STORIES Local author Darcy H. Lee reads from her newly released book, “Ghosts of Plymouth,” at the Plymouth Public Library. 132 South St.; Friday, Oct. 13, 3-4 p.m.; light refreshments.

GOD BOX St. Mary of the Assumption of Hull presents “The God Box, A Daughter’s Story,” a one-woman play to benefit the parish. The play is by Mary Lou Quinlan, the author of the best-selling book “The God Box” that told the story of her mother’s God boxes filled with hundreds of private petitions. Cardinal Cushing Theatre, Notre Dame Academy, 1073 Main St., Hingham; Friday, Oct. 13, 7 p.m.: $25, www.stmaryhull.weshareonline.org.
