Singles All-Scholastics

Sarah Campbell, Lincoln-Sudbury, freshman

Campbell advanced to the state semifinals for individuals. She finished the regular season undefeated and helped Lincoln-Sudbury reach the Division 1 North quarterfinals. Campbell is on the USTA New England zonal team.

Emma Davis, Cohasset, senior

Davis again won the state individual title, becoming the first athlete to ever win four singles titles. The senior finished her career 84-0 without losing a set. Davis led her team to the Division 3 South semifinals. She’ll attend Wake Forest.

Izzy Essi, Wayland, sophomore

Advertisement

Essi is a two-time Globe All-Scholastic selection and a returning Dual County League All-Star. The sophomore was a state individual finalist. Essi helped Wayland reach the Division 2 North semifinals. She ended with a 16-2 mark.

Sonya Khudyakov, Brookline, freshman

Khudyakov advanced to the South sectional individual finals, going undefeated in the Bay State Conference. Khudyakov was an integral part of a Brookline team that went 19-1 and reached the Division 1 state team semifinals.

Diana Prinos, Foxboro, junior

Prinos finished the season as a South sectional individual semifinalist. A Hockomock All-Star, she helped Foxboro take home the league title. With Prinos playing first singles, the Warriors reached the Division 2 state final.

Sophia Prinos, Foxboro, freshman

Prinos won in straight sets to advance to the South sectional semifinals for individuals. The freshman was a Hockomock All-Star, and along with her older sister helped Foxboro reach the Division 2 state championship.

Mia Wallace, Arlington, sophomore

Wallace advanced to the state semifinals for individuals, winning the first round without dropping a game. The sophomore finished with a record of 15-2. Wallace also participates in the USTA outside of playing for Arlington.

Sydney Weinberg, Winchester, freshman

Advertisement

Weinberg advanced to the state semifinals for individuals, going undefeated in the regular season and helping Winchester reach the Division 2 North sectional finals. She is in the top 20 in the USTA U-20 New England rankings.

Doubles All-Scholastics

Laurie-Maude Chenar, Andover, senior

Chenard and Paige Webster won the state individual doubles championship, finishing with a record of 16-0. Chenard was all-conference in the Merrimack Valley Conference and helped lead Andover to a Division 1 state title.

Paige Webster, Andover, senior

Webster teamed up with Laurie-Maude Chenard to take home an MIAA individual state title. The two finished undefeated and led Andover to the Division 1 state championship. Webster, a senior captain, will attend Union College.

Addisyn Kendall, Apponequet, junior

Kendall and Shawna Walker reached the state individual semifinals. Kendall was perfect in third singles the past two seasons, and she completed a perfect regular season alongside Walker, leading Apponequet to the South semis.

Shawna Walker, Apponequet, sophomore

Walker advanced to the state individual semifinals with Addisyn Kendall. She finished the regular season undefeated for the second year in a row. The tandem helped Apponequet advance to the Division 2 South sectional semifinals.

Taya Berler, Barnstable, junior

Berler reached the South sectional doubles final alongside Michaela Cornwall; both are two-time Globe All-Scholastics. The partners went undefeated in the regular season and helped Barnstable to the Division 1 South semifinals.

Michaela Cornwall, Barnstable, junior

Cornwall, a repeat All-Scholastic, led Barnstable to the Division 1 South semifinals with her partner Taya Berler. The duo finished the regular season 17-0 and reached the South individual final. The duo led Barnstable to a 17-2 mark.

Stephanie Cohen, Reading, senior

Cohen and her partner Ruthie Wilson were individual North sectional finalists. Cohen, a senior captain, finished the regular season 15-1 record in first doubles. The pair helped lead Reading to the Division 2 North semifinals.

Ruthie Wilson, Reading, sophomore

Wilson made it to the North sectional finals with Stephanie Cohen. The pair wrapped up the regular season with a 15-1 record. The honors student also helped guide Reading to the Division 2 North semifinals.

Coaches of the Year

Division 1: Cathy Ellis, Lynn Classical

Ellis led Lynn Classical to the Northeastern Conference title and a 16-2 season. In five years of coaching, Ellis has a 58-25 record. A Classical graduate, Ellis was once a Globe All-Scholastic softball Player of the Year.

Division 2: Barbara Horne, Scituate

Horne led Scituate to the Division 2 South sectional semifinal. The Sailors finished the season with a 22-1 record. Under Horne, Scituate secured a Patriot League title. The Scituate graduate has coached the Sailors for six years.

Division 3: Nina Bramhall, Martha’s Vineyard

Bramhall guided the Vineyarders to the Division 3 state title this season, her first at the school. Under Bramhall, the Vineyarders made their fourth straight tournament appearance and finished with a perfect record.
